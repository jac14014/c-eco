c-ECO
c-ECO Operational Governance System
Integrated Architecture for Monitoring, Classification, Escalation, and Recalibration
Operational Architecture v1.0 — May 2026
c-ECO Fellowship & Enterprise Program
Document Scope: This visual operational guide translates the c-ECO Model Law, the Temporal Foresight & Prudence (TFP) Manual, and the contractual instruments (FPA, MAI, CDGA, LLEP, CSAM) into traceable operational procedures. It is designed for fellows, enterprise clients, lab administrators, governance authorities, and external auditors.

The c-ECO Operational Governance System is not a theoretical framework. It is a procedural architecture that connects data certification, prudential classification, governance escalation, contractual response, and systemic recalibration into a single, auditable, traceable, and operationally reversible sequence.

Each section of this document corresponds to a functional layer of the system: intake and routing, monitoring and certification, classification, scoring, and prudential response, escalation and intervention, restoration and feedback. Together, these layers form what the Model Law calls "systemic reversibility before harm" — the procedural precondition for enforceable ecological co-responsibility.

Operational Structure

The c-ECO Operational Governance System is organized as a layered procedural architecture. Each section represents a functional component of the monitoring, classification, escalation, restoration, and recalibration sequence.

TFP Operational Engine

The Temporal Foresight & Prudence (TFP) Engine converts certified operational data into prudential scores that drive governance classification, contractual response, and monitoring intensity. Based on the c-ECO TFP Manual.

TFP Variables: P, ΔV, σ, Lr

Input Parameters
Probability (P) likelihood of systemic contraction or threshold breach
Velocity (ΔV) rate of change in the monitored variable
Signal Uncertainty (σ) confidence dispersion around the monitored signal
Reversibility (Lr) remaining systemic restoration capacity before irreversible contraction
P — Probability

Systemic Contraction Likelihood

Derived from certified data signals. Represents the estimated probability that the monitored system will breach its operational threshold within the forecast horizon.

ΔV — Velocity

Rate of Change

Measures how fast the variable is approaching criticality. High velocity shortens response time and may trigger pre-emptive governance activation.

σ — Signal Uncertainty

Confidence Dispersion

Quantified uncertainty propagated through the data pipeline. Not eliminated — documented and carried forward as co-input to all downstream scores.

Lr — Reversibility

Restoration Capacity

Assesses remaining systemic restoration capacity before irreversible contraction. Low Lr triggers enhanced precaution and may justify Safe Mode activation before threshold breach.

Input rule: all four variables must be certified through the Data & Signal Architecture (Section IV) before entering the TFP Engine. Uncertified inputs trigger an integrity breach and route to the Audit & Integrity System.

Operational Scores: SPS, TRS, RLS

Score Computation
SPS Systemic Probability Score
TRS Temporal Risk Score
RLS Reversibility Loss Score
↓
SPS + TRS Interaction probability-pressure under temporal constraint
RLS Adjustment reversibility loss modifies escalation sensitivity
↓
Composite TFP Score context-weighted, entity-adjusted aggregation
SPS

Systemic Probability Score

Primary output of P and ΔV interaction. Indicates the combined likelihood and urgency of threshold breach.

TRS

Temporal Risk Score

Integrates time-to-threshold with governance response capacity. Short time + limited response capacity = high TRS.

RLS

Reversibility Loss Score

Inverse of Lr. High RLS means low restoration capacity, justifying precautionary escalation even before threshold approach.

Score rule: each score is computed automatically by the TFP Engine. Override by the Methodology Lead requires documented justification and routes to the Governance Decision Matrix: authority = Methodology Lead + Board, max response = 72 hours, archive = TFP Audit Trail.

Governance action rule: band transitions are not automatic. Each transition requires documented justification, confirming authority, and archive entry. Reverse transitions (e.g., RED → AMBER) require restoration validation and Board approval (Decision Matrix, 7 days).

Restoration-first rule: restoration takes precedence over enforcement when: (a) reversibility is technically feasible, (b) intervention cost is proportionate to systemic value at risk, and (c) delay does not increase irreversibility probability. This principle is derived directly from the Model Law's conditioned immediate object doctrine.
Resource note: Restoration-First activation requires Board + External Reviewer authorization (Decision Matrix, 7 days). Resource intensity: High. Duration: up to 60 days. Financial responsibility: Enterprise Client, subject to engagement-specific recovery provisions. Specific terms defined per engagement in the MSA / Service Terms.

Perclusion Conditions

When Declared

Systemic perclusion is declared when: (a) irreversibility is technically or economically disproportionate, (b) restoration cost exceeds systemic value at risk, or (c) delay increases irreversibility probability beyond acceptable threshold.

Functional Conversion

Contractual Effect

The contractual object is not terminated — it is converted to a preservation or monitoring mandate. Obligations shift from active intervention to systemic documentation and legacy governance and preservation oversight.

Perclusion rule: systemic perclusion is not termination. It is a legal recognition that the original contractual purpose has become operationally impossible, and the parties' obligations must convert to preservation, monitoring, or wind-down protocols. Authority: Board + Legal (Decision Matrix, 14 days). Archive: Legal Effects Archive.

IEX rule: ex-ante unenforceability (IEX) means that contractual obligations are not enforceable unless the systemic conditions that made them viable at formation remain present. This is not merely a defense — it is a structural feature of c-ECO contracts. Authority: Legal + External Counsel (Decision Matrix, 21 days). Archive: Contract Repository.

Systemic default rule: systemic default is not a breach. The response is not punitive — it is restorative or convertive. Authority: Board + Legal (Decision Matrix, 14 days). Archive: Legal Effects Archive.

Living Lab & Deployment

Living Labs are territorial and operational governance environments where c-ECO monitoring, validation, and coordinated response are physically deployed. They are not clients — they are institutional infrastructure that hosts enterprise-embedded monitoring, field validation, and systemic stress-testing.

Living Lab ≠ Client

Entity Distinction
Element Enterprise Client Living Lab
Legal nature Contracting party Operational governance environment
Function Monitored entity Monitoring infrastructure
Data role Operational data source Data custodian / validator
TFP status Classified entity Classification environment
Contractual instrument MSA / Service Terms LLEP Protocol
Distinction rule: a Living Lab is never a contractual counterparty. It is a territorial and methodological node authorized by the LLEP Protocol (Exhibit C) to host monitoring, validation, and coordinated response activities within the c-ECO operational architecture.

Living Lab Types

Typology
Type I

Territorial Lab

Ecosystem monitoring in defined territorial boundaries. Primary function: baseline data collection, biodiversity assessment, contraction detection.

Type II

Enterprise-Embedded Lab

Monitoring infrastructure integrated into enterprise operations. Primary function: operational validation, compliance verification, real-time signal generation.

Type III

Methodology Infrastructure Lab

Controlled environment for stress-testing methodologies, governance responses, and threshold integrity. Primary function: calibration, simulation, methodological refinement.

Type IV

Transitional Lab

Pilot deployment for new sectors, territories, or methods. Primary function: proof-of-concept, threshold calibration, protocol validation before full authorization.

Typology rule: each Living Lab type requires specific authorization, custodian assignment, and monitoring protocol per the LLEP Protocol. Type authorization is not transferable between territories or sectors without Board review (Decision Matrix).
