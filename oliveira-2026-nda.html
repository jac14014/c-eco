This Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into for the purpose of enabling limited discussions, evaluations, institutional collaboration activities, and strategic coordination relating to the c-ECO Framework, the Threshold Function Protocol (“TFP”), the c-ECO Fellowship Program, the Amazon Living Lab, and associated governance structures developed, administered, or controlled by HF.

This Agreement operates as a preliminary confidentiality and intellectual-property protection instrument. It does not replace any future Institutional Referral & Collaboration Agreement, Fellowship Participation Agreement, Confidentiality & Data Governance Agreement (“CDGA”), data-access protocol, licensing instrument, or implementation agreement that may later be required by HF.

IMPORTANT: Access to c-ECO or TFP materials does not create any license, ownership right, implementation right, teaching right, commercial right, derivative-framework right, or authority to represent, reproduce, adapt, disclose, or operationalize the c-ECO Framework without HF’s prior written authorization.

1. Confidential Information

For purposes of this Agreement, “Confidential Information” includes, without limitation, all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of HF, whether written, oral, visual, digital, technical, operational, strategic, institutional, legal, scientific, financial, or otherwise.

  • TFP operational structures, variables, indicators, formulas, trigger logic, threshold logic, Safe Mode procedures, analytical architecture, and implementation design;
  • c-ECO governance methodologies, legal theories, institutional architecture, model clauses, protocols, draft statutes, manuals, papers, templates, and strategic materials;
  • Fellowship structures, candidate materials, partner discussions, institutional negotiations, implementation plans, funding strategies, partner contacts, and referral opportunities;
  • Technical, legal, scientific, commercial, educational, operational, or financial information disclosed in connection with HF, c-ECO, TFP, the Fellowship Program, or the Amazon Living Lab;
  • Any information that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the disclosure or the context in which it is provided.

“Trade Secrets” include any proprietary methodology, unpublished framework, operational logic, threshold system, analytical model, know-how, implementation architecture, strategic process, or confidential compilation of information related to c-ECO or TFP that derives actual or potential value from not being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy.

2. Confidentiality Obligations

The Recipient agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all Confidential Information and to use such information solely for the limited purpose expressly authorized by HF.

  • The Recipient shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without HF’s prior written approval.
  • The Recipient shall not copy, reproduce, distribute, publish, upload, teach, present, summarize, circulate, or transmit Confidential Information except as expressly authorized in writing by HF.
  • The Recipient shall adopt reasonable security measures to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, or misuse.
  • The Recipient shall promptly notify HF of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, access, loss, or misuse of Confidential Information.

3. TFP Integration and Safe Mode Disclosure Restrictions

Disclosure of Confidential Information related to TFP, c-ECO implementation, institutional strategy, candidate data, technical models, or partner contacts may be restricted, suspended, delayed, or withdrawn by HF at any time when HF determines that continued disclosure would create institutional, legal, operational, reputational, data-governance, or systemic risk.

Without limiting the foregoing, HF may automatically suspend disclosure or access where the applicable internal governance status, risk review, or TFP-related evaluation enters a heightened-risk condition, including any Red or Black disclosure band, Safe Mode condition, emergency review, or comparable restriction defined by HF.

The Recipient acknowledges that TFP-related information may be disclosed progressively and conditionally, and that no disclosure shall be interpreted as authorization to access higher-restriction layers of the c-ECO architecture.

4. Return and Destruction of Materials

Upon HF’s request, or upon termination of discussions or collaboration, the Recipient shall promptly return, delete, or destroy all Confidential Information and related copies, notes, summaries, extracts, working files, digital files, printed materials, and derivative materials in the Recipient’s possession or control.

Upon request, the Recipient shall provide written certification confirming such return, deletion, or destruction.

5. Intellectual Property and No License

All rights, title, and interests in and to the c-ECO Framework, TFP, related methodologies, legal architecture, operational logic, drafts, models, protocols, publications, visual materials, training materials, and institutional strategies remain exclusively with HF and/or their respective authors, contributors, or affiliated entities.

No license, assignment, transfer, waiver, commercial right, implementation right, teaching right, sublicensing right, publication right, derivative-work right, ownership interest, or implied authorization is granted under this Agreement.

6. No Reverse Engineering or Derivative Frameworks

The Recipient shall not reverse engineer, reconstruct, imitate, repackage, adapt, translate, teach, commercialize, implement, or create derivative frameworks, methodologies, protocols, models, clauses, training programs, consulting services, or institutional structures based on the c-ECO Framework, TFP, or any Confidential Information.

The Recipient shall not use Confidential Information to develop, support, advise, promote, or participate in any competing or substantially similar framework, initiative, fellowship, governance model, threshold protocol, or institutional program without HF’s prior written authorization.

7. Non-Circumvention

The Recipient shall not directly or indirectly circumvent HF in relation to institutional contacts, partners, fellows, candidates, funders, implementation opportunities, licensing discussions, referral opportunities, strategic negotiations, or related initiatives introduced, disclosed, or developed through the collaboration.

The Recipient shall not independently approach, solicit, negotiate with, or enter into arrangements with such contacts or opportunities in a manner that bypasses, excludes, prejudices, or undermines HF’s role, rights, authorship, institutional position, or strategic interests.

8. Authorized Disclosure

Any disclosure to another individual or entity requires HF’s prior written approval for each specific person or entity. General authorization, implied authorization, professional status, institutional affiliation, or participation in discussions shall not constitute authorization to receive Confidential Information.

HF may condition any disclosure on additional NDAs, CDGAs, data-access restrictions, role-based permissions, Safe Mode review, or other governance controls.

9. Relationship to CDGA and Data Governance

This Agreement does not authorize the Recipient to access, process, store, transfer, analyze, or govern candidate data, fellow data, institutional data, sensitive data, technical datasets, or operational system data.

Any such access shall require a separate CDGA, data-processing authorization, or other written instrument approved by HF. In the event of inconsistency between this Agreement and any later CDGA concerning data governance, the more specific data-governance instrument shall control.

10. Remedies

The Recipient acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure, misuse, circumvention, reverse engineering, or unauthorized implementation of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone may be insufficient.

HF shall therefore be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, equitable remedies, damages, attorneys’ fees where available, and any other remedies available under applicable law.

11. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

12. Term and Survival

This Agreement shall remain in effect for five (5) years following the last disclosure of Confidential Information.

Obligations relating to Trade Secrets, proprietary methodologies, TFP logic, c-ECO architecture, non-circumvention, no-license obligations, no-reverse-engineering obligations, and unauthorized derivative use shall survive indefinitely to the extent permitted by law.

13. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving its original intent.

14. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the confidentiality and protection of Confidential Information disclosed in connection with the matters described herein, and supersedes prior oral or written understandings concerning such subject matter.

Any modification or waiver of this Agreement must be made in writing and signed by HF.
