This Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of [DATE] (“Effective Date”) for the purpose of enabling limited discussions, evaluations, institutional collaboration activities, and strategic coordination relating to the c-ECO Framework, the Threshold Function Protocol (“TFP”), the c-ECO Fellowship Program, the Amazon Living Lab, and associated governance structures developed, administered, or controlled by HF.

This Agreement operates as a preliminary confidentiality and intellectual-property protection instrument. It does not replace any future Institutional Referral & Collaboration Agreement, Fellowship Participation Agreement, Confidentiality & Data Governance Agreement (“CDGA”), data-access protocol, licensing instrument, implementation agreement, or other written instrument that may later be required by HF.

Important: Access to c-ECO or TFP materials does not create any license, ownership right, implementation right, teaching right, commercial right, derivative-framework right, or authority to represent, reproduce, adapt, disclose, or operationalize the c-ECO Framework without HF’s prior written authorization.

1. Confidential Information

For purposes of this Agreement, “Confidential Information” includes, without limitation, all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of HF, whether written, oral, visual, digital, technical, operational, strategic, institutional, legal, scientific, financial, educational, or otherwise.

  • TFP operational structures, variables, indicators, formulas, trigger logic, threshold logic, Safe Mode procedures, analytical architecture, band classification logic, and implementation design;
  • c-ECO governance methodologies, legal theories, institutional architecture, model clauses, sectoral workbooks, protocols, draft statutes, manuals, papers, templates, and strategic materials;
  • Fellowship structures, candidate materials, partner discussions, institutional negotiations, Living Lab plans, implementation roadmaps, funding strategies, partner contacts, and referral opportunities;
  • Technical, legal, scientific, commercial, educational, operational, or financial information disclosed in connection with HF, c-ECO, TFP, the Fellowship Program, or the Amazon Living Lab;
  • Any information marked or identified as “Confidential,” “Proprietary,” or “Trade Secret,” or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the disclosure or the context in which it is provided.

1.1 TFP Integration and Disclosure Cessation

In the event of TFP band classification Red, Black, Safe Mode activation, emergency review, or any comparable heightened-risk disclosure condition determined by HF, all disclosure, discussion, dissemination, and further processing of Confidential Information shall immediately cease pending institutional review.

Such cessation is non-discretionary and reflects internal governance, systemic risk, legal risk, reputational risk, data-governance risk, or operational-risk conditions as assessed by HF.

1.2 Trade Secret Definition

“Trade Secrets” include any proprietary methodology, unpublished framework, formula, pattern, compilation, program, device, method, technique, process, threshold system, analytical model, know-how, implementation architecture, strategic process, sectoral mapping, confidential compilation, or operational logic related to c-ECO or TFP that derives actual or potential value from not being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy.

The TFP core logic, Safe Operating Space calibration parameters, sectoral threshold mappings, proprietary analytical instruments, and unpublished c-ECO methodological materials constitute Trade Secrets to the extent permitted by applicable law.

2. Confidentiality Obligations

Recipient agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all Confidential Information and to use such information solely for the limited purpose expressly authorized by HF.

  • Recipient shall use at least the same degree of care used to protect Recipient’s own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.
  • Recipient shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without HF’s prior written approval.
  • Recipient shall not copy, reproduce, distribute, publish, upload, teach, present, summarize, circulate, or transmit Confidential Information except as expressly authorized in writing by HF.
  • Recipient shall not use Confidential Information for any purpose detrimental to HF, the c-ECO Framework, the TFP, or associated institutional interests.
  • Recipient shall adopt reasonable physical, electronic, and procedural security measures to prevent unauthorized access, copying, disclosure, or misuse.
  • Recipient shall promptly notify HF of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, access, loss, or misuse of Confidential Information.

2.1 Authorized Disclosure

Recipient may disclose Confidential Information only to individuals or entities expressly approved by HF in writing on a case-by-case basis before disclosure.

General authorization, professional status, institutional affiliation, participation in discussions, or involvement in a related matter shall not constitute authorization to receive Confidential Information. Recipient shall ensure that any authorized person is bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those contained herein, and Recipient shall remain responsible for any breach by such person.

2.2 Return and Destruction

Upon written request by HF, or upon termination of discussions or this Agreement, Recipient shall promptly return, delete, or destroy all Confidential Information and related copies, notes, summaries, extracts, working files, digital files, printed materials, derivative materials, and tangible embodiments in Recipient’s possession or control.

Recipient shall certify such return, deletion, or destruction in writing within fifteen (15) days of HF’s request. Recipient may retain one archival copy solely to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement, provided such copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations herein.

3. Intellectual Property and No License

All rights, title, and interests in and to the c-ECO Framework, TFP, sectoral workbooks, methodological instruments, legal architecture, operational logic, drafts, models, protocols, publications, visual materials, training materials, institutional strategies, and associated concepts remain exclusively with HF and/or their respective authors, contributors, or affiliated entities, including Jacqueline A. Ennis as lead architect and doctrinal custodian.

No license, assignment, transfer, waiver, commercial right, implementation right, teaching right, sublicensing right, publication right, derivative-work right, ownership interest, or implied authorization is granted under this Agreement or by virtue of any disclosure of Confidential Information.

Recipient shall not apply for, register, claim, or assist any third party in applying for, registering, or claiming any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, domain name, methodology, framework, program, protocol, or other intellectual-property protection based on or derived from Confidential Information.

4. No Reverse Engineering or Derivative Frameworks

Recipient shall not reverse engineer, reconstruct, imitate, repackage, adapt, translate, teach, commercialize, implement, operationalize, or create derivative frameworks, methodologies, protocols, models, clauses, training programs, consulting services, institutional structures, or governance architectures based on the c-ECO Framework, TFP, or any Confidential Information.

Recipient shall not use Confidential Information to develop, support, advise, promote, or participate in any competing or substantially similar framework, initiative, fellowship, governance model, threshold protocol, consulting model, academic program, or institutional program without HF’s prior written authorization.

5. Non-Circumvention

Recipient shall not directly or indirectly circumvent HF in relation to institutional contacts, partners, fellows, candidates, funders, implementation opportunities, licensing discussions, referral opportunities, strategic negotiations, or related initiatives introduced, disclosed, or developed through the collaboration.

For a period of twenty-four (24) months from the Effective Date, Recipient shall refer all such opportunities to HF and shall not pursue them independently, through third parties, or through affiliated entities without HF’s prior written consent.

Recipient shall not independently approach, solicit, negotiate with, or enter into arrangements with such contacts or opportunities in a manner that bypasses, excludes, prejudices, or undermines HF’s role, rights, authorship, institutional position, or strategic interests.

6. Relationship to CDGA and Data Governance

This Agreement does not authorize Recipient to access, process, store, transfer, analyze, govern, export, disclose, or otherwise handle candidate data, fellow data, institutional data, sensitive data, technical datasets, operational system data, or any other data subject to data-governance controls.

Any such access shall require a separate CDGA, data-processing authorization, role-based access approval, or other written instrument approved by HF.

In the event of inconsistency between this Agreement and any later CDGA concerning data governance, the more specific data-governance instrument shall control.

7. Exceptions

The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement shall not apply to information that Recipient can demonstrate through written records:

  1. Was already known to Recipient prior to disclosure by HF;
  2. Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by Recipient;
  3. Is rightfully received by Recipient from a third party without restriction and without breach of any confidentiality obligation;
  4. Is independently developed by Recipient without use of or reference to HF’s Confidential Information; or
  5. Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided Recipient gives HF prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order or confidential treatment.

8. Remedies

Recipient acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure, misuse, circumvention, reverse engineering, derivative use, or unauthorized implementation of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone may be insufficient.

HF shall therefore be entitled to seek injunctive relief, temporary restraining orders, preliminary and permanent injunctions, specific performance, equitable remedies, accounting, damages, attorneys’ fees where available, and any other remedies available under applicable law, in equity, or under this Agreement.

9. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

Any dispute arising from or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in Columbia, Missouri, under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitrator’s decision shall be final and binding. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, HF may seek injunctive, equitable, or emergency relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent or remedy unauthorized disclosure, misuse, circumvention, reverse engineering, or infringement of Confidential Information or intellectual-property rights.

10. Term and Survival

This Agreement shall remain in effect for five (5) years from the Effective Date.

Obligations relating to Trade Secrets, proprietary methodologies, TFP logic, c-ECO architecture, non-circumvention, no-license obligations, no-reverse-engineering obligations, unauthorized derivative use, and unauthorized implementation shall survive indefinitely to the extent permitted by law.

11. Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email with confirmation of receipt, certified mail, courier, or other traceable delivery method to the addresses set forth in the parties block, or to such other address as either party may designate by written notice.

12. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court or arbitral tribunal of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

13. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding the confidentiality and protection of Confidential Information disclosed in connection with the matters described herein and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral, concerning such subject matter.

No amendment, modification, or waiver shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by HF.
