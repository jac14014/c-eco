TDR // Systemic Risk Architecture
Systemic Risk Architecture for Pre-Threshold Governance
Contemporary law already deploys precaution, prudential regulation, due diligence and systemic-risk supervision across multiple domains. The unresolved problem is that these anticipatory regimes remain fragmented, sector-bound, probabilistic and predominantly managerial.
They do not yet provide a general juridical architecture through which ongoing systemic incompatibility — especially under conditions of declining reversibility — alters the validity, enforceability, and continuity of legal and operational acts in real time.
Trajectory Illegality Systemic Proof Ex-Ante Unenforceability
Section 01
The Problem — Structural Insufficiency
The thesis is straightforward: contemporary law already contains ex-ante instruments. The problem is that existing anticipatory regimes remain insufficient at the exact point where systemic conditions require continuous compatibility with reversibility limits to condition juridical validity itself.
What Exists What Is Missing
Macroprudential regulation (finance) Trajectory-based (not event-based) governance
Corporate due diligence (CSDDD) Loss of reversibility as present legal condition
Disaster risk governance Automatic legal effects from continuous monitoring
AI Act systemic risk regimes Transversal architecture (not sectoral)
Critical Gap
Six of nine planetary boundaries transgressed; NBFI vulnerabilities persist post-2008; AI systemic risks emerge. Existing ex-ante reduces vulnerabilities but does not resolve systemic dynamics in complex, mutable ecosystems.
— BIS, FSB, OECD, UNDRR, IPCC 2021-2023
Section 02
Five Critical Displacements — How c-ECO Differs from Existing Regimes
Displacement 01
From risk of harm to loss of reversibility
Current regimes treat risk as probability of adverse impact; capital requirements calculated as probability of default × exposure.

c-ECO displacement: treats erosion of reversibility as legally relevant fact in itself. When Lr < 1.0, automatic execution suspension applies regardless of present solvency or formal compliance.
Displacement 02
From events/impacts to trajectories
Current regimes ask "what damage may occur?" and follow sequential logic: identify → prevent → mitigate → remediate adverse impacts.

c-ECO displacement: shifts to "what trajectory is being legally permitted to consolidate?" Sustained negative ΔV + P approaching boundary → juridical reclassification ex lege, before materialization of harm.
Displacement 03
From managerial to constitutive
Current regimes operate managerially: supervisory discretion to impose additional requirements, duties to monitor, report, or mitigate.

c-ECO displacement: systemic compatibility becomes condition of validity, enforceability and continuity — not merely duty. Systemic incompatibility → automatic invalidity of execution rights (IEX), not just enhanced supervision.
Displacement 04
From administrative cycles to immediate legal effects
Current regimes follow administrative cycles: assessment → notification → compliance period → potential sanction. This creates delays incompatible with systemic velocity.

c-ECO displacement: Systemic Proof transforms continuous technical evidence into juridical effects without declaratory delays. Certified sensor data → Γ score calculation → automatic trigger activation via smart contracts.
Displacement 05
From sectoral to transversal architecture
Current regimes are fragmented: Finance (Basel III), Supply chain (CSDDD), AI (AI Act), Disaster (SFDRR) — each with distinct grammars, metrics, and enforcement mechanisms.

c-ECO displacement: establishes common juridical grammar for critical systems under material limits. Single variable set (P, ΔV, σ, Lr) applicable to any system with material limits — finance, ecosystems, supply chains, AI infrastructure.
Section 03
The c-ECO Response — Three Interconnected Innovations
01. Trajectory Illegality
Legality conditioned by systemic compatibility over time. Formal compliance ≠ systemic validity. Contractual/actuarial validity requires continuous certification of trajectory stability.
Mechanism: ΔV sustained negative + P approaching boundary → automatic reclassification to Amber/Red regardless of present performance metrics.
Legal Effect: Execution rights become conditional; obligations transform into Join Duty (cooperative systemic preservation), transcending individual contract boundaries.
02. Systemic Proof
Certified sensor-based data, continuous monitoring. Immediate juridical effects, no declaratory delays.
Architecture: Data Custodians (certified entities) collect, verify, and cryptographically sign sensor data. Blockchain anchoring ensures immutability; zero-knowledge proofs preserve commercial confidentiality where required.
Operational Sequence: L1: Sensor ingestion → L2: Certification → L3: Γ calculation → L4: Juridical trigger (smart contract execution).
03. Ex-Ante Unenforceability (IEX)
Automatic suspension of execution when trajectories become incompatible with stability. Ex lege, ex ante — not sanction, but functional adjustment to biophysical reality.
Trigger Conditions: Γ > 0.7 (Red State) OR Lr > 0.9 (irreversibility threshold) → automatic execution suspension. This is not default; it is reconfiguration to Safe Mode for preservation of asset value and systemic integrity.
Juridical Nature: Constitutive, not punitive. IEX operates as "systemic force majeure" — the contract continues, but execution rights are conditionally suspended to preserve reversibility. The legal relationship is preserved, not terminated.
"The preservation of systemic integrity becomes constitutive of juridical validity itself."

Section 05
The Four Variables — TFP: Threshold Function Protocol
Multiplicative Coupling Effects
Variables interact non-linearly. High P (proximity to boundary) amplifies the impact of ΔV (velocity); high σ (volatility) increases effective Lr (reversibility loss) through uncertainty premiums.
Amplification Example
P = 0.8 (near boundary) + ΔV = -0.4 (negative velocity) → Combined effect: 0.8 × 1.5 = 1.2× base risk
Damping Example
P = 0.3 (safe distance) + ΔV = -0.4 (negative velocity) → Combined effect: 0.3 × 0.5 = 0.15× base risk
Threshold Interdependencies
Lr Dominance (Irreversibility Override)
When Lr > 0.9, other variables become secondary. System approaches irreversible state; automatic trigger activation regardless of P, ΔV, or σ values. This is the "point of no return" detection.
σ-Volatility Override (Precautionary Activation)
High uncertainty (σ > 0.8) triggers precautionary principle: Γ calculation shifts to worst-case scenario weighting until σ normalizes. Uncertainty never justifies risk expansion.
ΔV Trajectory Lock (Inertia Effect)
Sustained negative ΔV over T+3 periods creates "trajectory inertia" — requires exponentially positive counter-velocity to reset. Mere stabilization insufficient; active reversal required.

Section 06
Prudential Architecture — Four Levels of Systemic Response
Green — Nominal Operation
Γ ≤ 0.3 — Systemic compatibility verified. Standard monitoring protocols apply. Full execution rights.
Amber — Heightened Vigilance
0.3 < Γ ≤ 0.7 — Trajectory instability detected. Reinforced reporting, enhanced guarantees, Join Duty activation. Conditional execution.
Red — Safe Mode (Systemic Curatorship)
Γ > 0.7 — Critical systemic risk. Execution reconfiguration — not default, not termination. Parties become System Curators; obligations transform to systemic preservation.
Black — Restoration First (External Intervention)
Lr > 0.95 — Irreversibility imminent. Automatic asset conversion to certified restoration providers. IEX fully activated.
State Transition Logic (Hysteresis)
From Transition Condition To
Green Γ > 0.3 sustained for 2 consecutive periods Amber
Amber Γ > 0.7 OR Lr > 0.9 (immediate) Red
Red Γ < 0.5 sustained for 3 consecutive periods Amber
Amber Γ < 0.3 sustained for 4 consecutive periods Green
Hysteresis Mechanism
State transitions employ asymmetric thresholds to prevent oscillation. Downgrading requires lower Γ thresholds than upgrading. Path dependency: once systemic trust is compromised, restoration requires sustained evidence of stability.
Key distinction: Safe Mode = non-default protective state. The contract continues; execution is reconfigured to preserve reversibility and asset value. This is not termination, not bankruptcy, not force majeure — it is adaptive continuity.
