Executive Overview

The insufficiency of existing anticipatory legalities

Contemporary law already deploys precaution, prudential regulation, due diligence and systemic-risk supervision across multiple domains. The unresolved problem is that these anticipatory regimes remain fragmented, sector-bound, probabilistic and predominantly managerial.

They do not yet provide a general juridical architecture through which ongoing systemic incompatibility — especially under conditions of declining reversibility — alters the validity, enforceability, and continuity of legal and operational acts in real time.

TRAJECTORY ILLEGALITY SYSTEMIC PROOF EX-ANTE UNENFORCEABILITY
01

The Problem

Not absence of ex-ante, but structural insufficiency

Thesis: Contemporary law already contains ex-ante instruments. The problem is that existing anticipatory regimes remain insufficient at the exact point where systemic conditions require continuous compatibility with reversibility limits to condition juridical validity itself.

What exists

  • ✓ Macroprudential regulation (finance)
  • ✓ Corporate due diligence (CSDDD)
  • ✓ Disaster risk governance
  • ✓ AI Act systemic risk regimes

What is missing

  • ◆ Trajectory-based (not event-based) governance
  • ◆ Loss of reversibility as present legal condition
  • ◆ Automatic legal effects from continuous monitoring
  • ◆ Transversal architecture (not sectoral)

Result: Six of nine planetary boundaries transgressed; NBFI vulnerabilities persist post-2008; AI systemic risks emerge. Existing ex-ante reduces vulnerabilities but does not resolve systemic dynamics in complex, mutable ecosystems.

— BIS, FSB, OECD, UNDRR, IPCC 2021-2023

02

Five Critical Displacements

How c-ECO differs from existing anticipatory regimes

1

From risk of harm to loss of reversibility

Existing (e.g., Basel III)

Probability of default × exposure = capital requirement

c-ECO

Lr < 1.0 → automatic execution suspension regardless of present solvency

2

From events/impacts to trajectories

Existing (e.g., CSDDD)

Identify → prevent → mitigate → remediate adverse impacts

c-ECO

ΔV sustained negative + P approaching boundary → juridical reclassification ex lege

3

From managerial to constitutive

Existing (e.g., macroprudential)

Supervisory discretion to impose additional requirements

c-ECO

Systemic incompatibility → automatic invalidity of execution rights (IEX)

4

From administrative cycles to immediate legal effects

Existing (e.g., AI Act)

Assessment → notification → compliance period → potential sanction

c-ECO

Certified sensor data → Γ score calculation → automatic trigger activation

5

From sectoral to transversal architecture

Existing

Finance: Basel | Supply chain: CSDDD | AI: AI Act | Disaster: SFDRR

c-ECO

Single grammar: P, ΔV, σ, Lr → applicable to any system with material limits

03

The c-ECO Response

Three interconnected innovations

1

Trajectory Illegality

Legality conditioned by systemic compatibility over time. Formal compliance ≠ systemic validity.

2

Systemic Proof

Certified sensor-based data, continuous monitoring. Immediate juridical effects, no declaratory delays.

3

Ex-Ante Unenforceability (IEX)

Automatic suspension of execution when trajectories become incompatible with stability. Ex lege, ex ante — not sanction, functional adjustment to biophysical reality.

"The preservation of systemic integrity becomes constitutive of juridical validity itself."

09

Governance Architecture

Layered accountability: separation of powers

01

Data & Verification Body (DVB)

Certified sensors, continuous monitoring, integrity assurance

LIMIT: No normative or decisional authority

02

Calibration Council

Sectoral parameters, methodological updates, threshold calibration

LIMIT: No intervention in individual assets or projects

03

Due Process Layer

Technical contestation: material error, fraud, sensor failure

LIMIT: No review of ecological, economic, or policy merit

04

Legal Interface

Contracts, bonds, insurance, automatic triggers

LIMIT: Courts verify integrity, not manage risk

Result: Non-interpretive, automatic, auditable — preserving execution speed while ensuring accountability.
