Exhibit E — Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement
MNDA Template | c-ECO Fellowship Program
Template Instrument — Authorized Use Only
This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("MNDA") is provided as Exhibit E to the Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA). It is a template for use by authorized c-ECO Fellows in preliminary discussions with prospective clients.
Execution Authority: This MNDA must be executed by Silvio Meira Institute LLC (operational entity), not by the Fellow individually. The Fellow may act as Authorized Representative for negotiations, but contractual capacity resides with the Institute.
Dual Protection: This MNDA protects confidential information of both Silvio Meira Institute LLC and the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation (collectively, the "Disclosing Parties").
PARTIES
Disclosing Parties (Protected Entities)
Primary: Silvio Meira Institute LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, with offices at 4215 Lindy Circle, Orlando, FL 32827, USA ("Institute")

Affiliated: Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, a non-profit organization, with offices at 2504 Grandview Circle, Columbia, MO 65203, USA ("Foundation")

The Institute and Foundation are collectively referred to as the "Disclosing Parties" or individually as a "Disclosing Party".

Fellow

Receiving Party (Counterparty)

PREAMBLE
WHEREAS, the Disclosing Parties possess confidential and proprietary information relating to the c-ECO Systemic Governance Framework, analytical methodologies, and related intellectual property;
WHEREAS, the Receiving Party desires to explore a potential business relationship involving the application of c-ECO methodologies to specific case environments;
WHEREAS, the Disclosing Parties are willing to disclose certain confidential information to the Receiving Party for the limited purpose of evaluating such potential relationship, subject to the protections herein;
WHEREAS, the Receiving Party possesses confidential information regarding its operations, systems, and strategic objectives that it may disclose to the Disclosing Parties for the same evaluative purpose;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:
ARTICLE I — DEFINITIONS
[MNDA-ART-001] Section 1.1 — Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether in oral, written, electronic, or other form, including but not limited to:
Category Description Primary Disclosing Party
c-ECO Framework Methodological architecture, TFP variables, sectoral structures, analytical protocols, pre-threshold logic, temporal validity protocols (−Στο) Foundation & Institute
Technical Information Software, algorithms, data models, platform architecture, signal processing systems Institute
Business Information Strategic plans, partnership structures, funding mechanisms, pricing methodologies, OCAP structures Foundation & Institute
Analytical Outputs Case assessments, threshold analyses, systemic compatibility evaluations, pre-threshold signals Institute (as operator)
Client Information Operational data, institutional structures, case-specific details, stakeholder information Receiving Party (Client)
§1 — Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this MNDA; (b) was rightfully in the receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure; (c) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed without use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information.
[MNDA-ART-002] Section 1.2 — Dual Protection Structure
The Parties acknowledge the specific roles and protection requirements of each Disclosing Party:
I — Foundation Protection: The Foundation's Confidential Information relates primarily to the c-ECO framework's methodological integrity, doctrinal coherence, and institutional reputation. Unauthorized disclosure may compromise systemic governance standards.
II — Institute Protection: The Institute's Confidential Information relates to operational implementation, commercial arrangements, analytical execution, and client relationships. Unauthorized disclosure may compromise competitive position and contractual obligations.
III — Cross-Protection: Each Disclosing Party is entitled to enforce this MNDA independently for breaches affecting its respective Confidential Information. The Institute may act on its own behalf and on behalf of the Foundation for operational breaches.
ARTICLE II — OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY
[MNDA-ART-003] Section 2.1 — Standard of Care
The Receiving Party shall hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Parties in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, but in no event less than reasonable care.
[MNDA-ART-004] Section 2.2 — Specific Protections for c-ECO Information
Given the unique nature of the c-ECO framework, the Receiving Party agrees to enhanced protections:
I — No Framework Extraction: The Receiving Party shall not extract, document, or reverse-engineer the c-ECO methodological architecture, TFP variables, or systemic logic for independent use or adaptation;
II — No Unauthorized Application: The Receiving Party shall not apply c-ECO concepts, terminology, or analytical structures outside the scope of any subsequent authorized engagement;
III — No Competitive Use: The Receiving Party shall not use c-ECO Confidential Information to develop competing analytical frameworks, governance methodologies, or systemic assessment tools;
IV — Pre-Threshold Signal Protection: Any disclosure of threshold-related or pre-threshold analytical concepts shall be subject to the highest level of protection, with no external communication without explicit written authorization from the Foundation.
[MNDA-ART-005] Section 2.3 — Disclosure Restrictions
The Receiving Party shall not disclose Confidential Information of the Disclosing Parties to any third parties except:
(a) To its directors, officers, employees, legal counsel, and professional advisors who have a strict need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein;
(b) To the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice (unless prohibited by law) and cooperates with the Disclosing Parties' efforts to protect the information.
ARTICLE III — OBLIGATIONS OF THE DISCLOSING PARTIES
[MNDA-ART-006] Section 3.1 — Reciprocal Protection
The Disclosing Parties (Institute and Foundation) shall hold the Receiving Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence with the same standard of care specified in Article II.
[MNDA-ART-007] Section 3.2 — Use Limitations
The Disclosing Parties shall use the Receiving Party's Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating the potential business relationship described in the Preamble, and not for any other purpose, including:
I — Independent investment or operational decisions regarding the Receiving Party's business without separate authorization;
II — Disclosure to third parties except as necessary for c-ECO analytical engagement under subsequent authorized instruments;
III — Use in unrelated research, publications, or methodological development without explicit consent.
[MNDA-ART-008] Section 3.3 — Fellow as Information Conduit
The Parties acknowledge that the Fellow, as Authorized Representative of the Institute, may be privy to Confidential Information of both Disclosing Parties and the Receiving Party in the course of preliminary discussions.
§1 — The Fellow is bound by the FPA (Exhibits A-D) and acts under the Institute's authority, not as an independent party.
§2 — The Receiving Party shall not attempt to circumvent the Institute or Foundation by seeking direct contractual relationships with the Fellow individually.
§3 — Any Confidential Information disclosed to the Fellow shall be deemed disclosed to the Institute and subject to this MNDA.
ARTICLE IV — TERM AND SURVIVAL
[MNDA-ART-009] Section 4.1 — Term
This MNDA shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of three (3) years, unless earlier terminated by either Party with thirty (30) days' written notice.
[MNDA-ART-010] Section 4.2 — Survival
The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this MNDA for a period of five (5) years with respect to general Confidential Information, and indefinitely with respect to:
I — The c-ECO framework architecture and core methodological elements (Foundation IP);
II — Pre-threshold signal concepts and threshold analytics (Joint IP);
III — Trade secrets and proprietary technical infrastructure (Institute IP).
ARTICLE V — REMEDIES
[MNDA-ART-011] Section 5.1 — Injunctive Relief
The Parties acknowledge that breach of this MNDA would cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are inadequate. Each Disclosing Party (Institute and Foundation independently) shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without the requirement of posting bond.
[MNDA-ART-012] Section 5.2 — Independent Enforcement
The Institute and Foundation may each enforce this MNDA independently for breaches affecting their respective interests:
(a) The Institute may seek remedies for operational, commercial, or competitive harm;
(b) The Foundation may seek remedies for methodological integrity, doctrinal coherence, or reputational harm;
(c) Either may seek joint remedies where interests overlap.
ARTICLE VI — RETURN OF INFORMATION
[MNDA-ART-013] Section 6.1 — Return or Destruction
Upon termination or upon written request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Parties and certify such return or destruction in writing.
[MNDA-ART-014] Section 6.2 — Retention Exception
The Receiving Party may retain Confidential Information to the extent: (a) required by applicable law or professional standards; (b) contained in automated backup systems; or (c) necessary for ongoing dispute resolution, provided all such retained information remains subject to this MNDA.
ARTICLE VII — GENERAL PROVISIONS
[MNDA-ART-015] Section 7.1 — No Binding Commitment
This MNDA creates no obligation to enter into any further agreement. The Disclosing Parties make no representation or warranty regarding the accuracy or completeness of any Confidential Information disclosed.
[MNDA-ART-016] Section 7.2 — No License or Transfer
Nothing in this MNDA grants any license, express or implied, to any intellectual property of either Party. No transfer of rights occurs by disclosure of Confidential Information.
[MNDA-ART-017] Section 7.3 — Governing Law and Dispute Resolution
This MNDA shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict of law principles. Disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration under ICC Rules, with seat in New York, consistent with the FPA.
[MNDA-ART-018] Section 7.4 — Entire Agreement
This MNDA constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding confidentiality for the specified purpose and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations.
[MNDA-ART-019] Section 7.5 — Counterparts
This MNDA may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as original signatures.
