The Threshold Function Protocol in Mining

Mining systems are threshold-sensitive because operational continuity can conceal progressive loss of reversibility. A dam, slope, aquifer, or contamination pathway may appear functional while its systemic trajectory moves toward irreversible failure.

Γ = f(P, ΔV, σ, Lr)

The Mining Trigger Function: systemic classification as a function of position, trajectory, uncertainty, and reversibility liquidity.

The Four TFP Variables in Mining

P
Position — Distance from Critical Mining Threshold

Definition: Position measures how close a mining subsystem is to a geotechnical, hydrological, toxicological, or restoration boundary.

P = (Critical Boundary − Current State) / Reference Range

Mining Translation: P may apply to tailings pressure, slope deformation, seepage rate, groundwater drawdown, or contaminant concentration.

Mining Application

If pore pressure in a tailings structure approaches the certified safety boundary, P decreases. A low P indicates that continued deposition may no longer be systemically compatible.

ΔV
Velocity — Rate of Deterioration

Definition: Velocity measures the direction and speed at which the mining subsystem is moving toward or away from a threshold.

ΔV = (Pfinal − Pinitial) / Tref

Mining Translation: Negative ΔV means the mine is approaching instability. In mining, sustained negative velocity may be more important than a single static measurement.

Mining Application

A slope that remains technically within tolerance but shows accelerating displacement may require escalation before visible failure.

σ
Uncertainty — Data Gaps and Measurement Limits

Definition: Uncertainty captures sensor error, incomplete monitoring, model limitations, and lack of observability.

σtotal = √(σ²sensor + σ²model + σ²coverage)

Critical Principle: In c-ECO, uncertainty does not enlarge the permission to continue extraction. It narrows the acceptable margin.

Mining Application

If groundwater monitoring is sparse or tailings instrumentation is incomplete, the system receives a prudential downgrade rather than the benefit of doubt.

Lr
Reversibility Liquidity — Capacity to Stabilize or Restore

Definition: Lr measures whether immediately mobilizable resources are sufficient to reverse or stabilize the deteriorating trajectory.

Lr = Rmi / Ct

Mining Translation: Rmi includes immediately available funds, equipment, technical teams, containment capacity, and enforceable guarantees. Ct is the projected cost of stabilization or restoration.

Mining Application

A mine with high restoration promises but low immediately accessible financial assurance may have low Lr and require Safe Mode or Restoration First logic.

Problem Set: Calculating TFP Variables from Mining Data

Problem Set A — Tailings Structure Case
1 Position Calculation: Tailings Pore Pressure Boundary

Scenario: A mining operator maintains a tailings facility. Certified technical review establishes a critical pore pressure boundary of 180 kPa. The current reported pore pressure is 150 kPa. The historical safe reference level is 90 kPa. Sensor uncertainty is ±10%.

ParameterValue
Critical boundary180 kPa
Current pore pressure150 kPa
Reference safe state90 kPa
Sensor uncertainty±10%

Tasks:

  1. Calculate nominal P using: P = (Boundary − Current) / (Boundary − Reference).
  2. Apply asymmetric uncertainty by treating current pressure as Current + σ.
  3. Classify the prudential band.
  4. Explain whether continued tailings deposition remains compatible with reversibility.

3 Reversibility Liquidity: Restoration Capacity

Scenario: The operator has $12M in immediately accessible emergency funds, $25M in insurance subject to litigation delay, and $40M in long-term restoration commitments. The projected technical cost of stabilization is $55M.

Tasks:

  2. Calculate Lr using only immediately mobilizable resources.
  3. Recalculate Lr if stabilization cost increases to $75M due to delayed intervention.
  4. Determine whether the system falls into Safe Mode or Restoration First.

4 Portfolio-Level Mining Classification

Walkthrough: Problem 1 Solution Structure

1
Calculate Nominal Position

P = (180 − 150) / (180 − 90) = 30 / 90 = 0.33

The structure is already close to the critical boundary.

2
Apply Asymmetric Uncertainty

σ = 150 × 10% = 15 kPa

Prudential current pressure = 165 kPa

Pprudential = (180 − 165) / 90 = 15 / 90 = 0.17

3
Classify Band

Nominal P = 0.33 suggests a critical zone.

Prudential P = 0.17 indicates Black-band proximity and Restoration First concern.

4
Governance Implication

Under c-ECO, uncertainty does not justify continued deposition. It strengthens the case for immediate Safe Mode or Restoration First review.
