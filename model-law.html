I

Legal Nature and Function

The Model Law constitutes the c-ΣCO Systemic Governance Statute — a transnational legal regime founded on three structural pillars: predictive governance, absolute primacy of biophysical boundaries, and binding legal automation. It does not create new substantive environmental obligations, nor does it replace existing regulatory regimes. Instead, it conditions the validity, enforceability, and continuity of legal acts upon the preservation of the Safe Operating Space (SOS).

Ex-Ante Unenforceability (IEX) Art. 15

Automatic suspension of obligation enforceability when predictive technical analysis indicates trajectory incompatible with systemic reversibility, irrespective of fault, material harm, or contractual breach. Operationalized via TFP Manual, Sec. 7-9.

Systemic Perclusion Art. 19-22

Immediate legal blocking of formally lawful acts whose isolated or cumulative continuation contributes to construction of technical trajectory incompatible with SOS or systemic reversibility.

Conditioned Immediate Object Art. 9-10

The immediate object of any legal relationship is conditioned upon preservation of biophysical stability. The obligation exists and produces effects only as long as systemic conditions ensuring its performance remain valid.

II

Structural Principles

Principle of Supremacy of the Safe Operating Space

Art. 1

The validity, enforceability, and continuity of any acts, contracts, or operations subject to the c-ΣCO regime are subordinated to the preservation of the Safe Operating Space (SOS). No legal effect may be recognized for acts or arrangements whose execution presupposes or entails violation of such limits. SOS boundaries defined by Calibration Council per TFP Manual, Sec. 17.

In Dubio Pro Systema Principle

Art. 2

Where material technical uncertainty exists regarding risk trajectories, impact reversibility, or systemic integrity, legal decision-making shall prioritize the protection of the system. The absence of absolute technical certainty shall not preclude immediate suspension of operations. Uncertainty treatment per TFP Manual, Sec. 4.5.

Precedence of Physical Time

Art. 3

Any administrative, judicial, or arbitral act permitting the continuation or consolidation of irreversible trajectories, loss of systemic reversibility, or aggravation of monitored risks during the pendency of proceedings shall be null and without legal effect.

Non-Disposability of Collapse Risk

Art. 4

The risk of systemic collapse, exhaustion of critical resources, and loss of habitability constitutes a non-disposable legal interest. Any waiver, settlement, consent, or administrative act admitting, tolerating, or normalizing transgression of monitored systemic limits is null.

III

Core Legal Mechanisms

The Model Law institutes structural legal mechanisms operating automatically, non-discretionarily, and ex ante, translating certified technical evidence into binding legal effects:

§15 IEX

Ex-Ante Unenforceability (IEX)

An autonomous legal category whereby the enforceability of obligations, execution rights, and operational acts are automatically suspended whenever predictive technical analysis indicates that continuation of performance would lead to trajectory incompatible with systemic reversibility or SOS stability. Triggered by TFP scores (SPS, TRS, RLS) per Manual Sec. 3, 7.

§19-22 Perclusion

Systemic and Trajectory Perclusion

Legal blocking of formally lawful acts whose continued execution predictably leads to exhaustion of critical resources, loss of habitability, or systemic collapse. Perclusion is opposable to successors and substitutes, and is not neutralized by corporate reorganization or asset transfer.

§44-47 Safe Mode

Safe Mode (Level 3)

A legal state of systemic containment whereby execution of contracts, operations, or flows is temporarily suspended or reconfigured to preserve system integrity and prevent propagation of critical risk. In Safe Mode, preservation of systemic stability prevails over individual performance targets, profit, or operational efficiency. Activated by Red Band (TRS < 60 or RLS < 0.8) per TFP Manual, Sec. 3.4.

§137 Asset Affection

Automatic Asset Affection (Systemic Slashing)

Violation of systemic limits or maintenance of trajectories incompatible with reversibility results in automatic affection of assets, guarantees, or linked reserves, mandatorily allocating them to containment, restoration, or safe decommissioning of the affected system. Execution by certified Restoration Providers per TFP Manual, Part VIII.

V

Governance Architecture and Legal Shielding

Distributed Intelligence Architecture

Art. 221

The c-ECO architecture recognizes three non-substitutable intelligence domains: Planetary Intelligence (biophysical signals), Algorithmic Intelligence (technical processing via TFP), and Human Intelligence (exclusive source of normativity, legal responsibility, and value judgment). The Model Law ensures no domain substitutes for or absorbs the functions of others. Human Intelligence stewarded by Hasse Foundation.

Anti-Forum Shopping Shield

Art. 222

All disputes related to trigger activation, prudential band classification, Safe Mode (Level 3), or Restoration First (Level 4) shall be submitted exclusively to specialized technical arbitration. Ordinary courts are expressly excluded from jurisdiction over such controversies, preventing tactical litigation and procedural delay. Technical Curatorship Chamber designated by Hasse Foundation per Art. 81.

Continuity of Execution During Review

Art. 81, 222

The filing of any contestation, appeal, or arbitral claim shall not suspend the automatic effects of a triggered prudential level. If Safe Mode or External Intervention is activated, all corresponding measures remain in force until final binding decision — prioritizing biophysical reversibility over procedural convenience. Solve et repete principle: execute first, argue later.
