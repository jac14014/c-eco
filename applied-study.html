Applied Study

Participate in the c-ECO Applied Study

The c-ECO Applied Study invites institutions to participate in an applied research collaboration on pre-threshold contractual governance — focusing on how long-horizon contracts can preserve governability and decision-space under systemic risk and potential irreversibility.

Important:
Participation is research-oriented and non-certifying. The study does not provide legal advice, ratings, or ESG assurances. It examines contractual architecture and internal governance procedures.

What participation involves

  • A structured intake on a selected contract type (or a representative template).
  • A scoping exercise to identify “licensed powers” that may create lock-in over time.
  • A governance mapping of reassessment procedures and internal decision records.
  • A pilot “adoption pathway” draft for procedural reassessment and recalibration.

The study can be adapted to your context. You may participate with a redacted contract, a template, or a contract-type description — depending on confidentiality needs.

c-ECO Adoption Pathway: 5 Steps

This process aims to ensure that contracts maintain adaptability and governability over time by embedding mechanisms for reassessment and recalibration.

Step 1
Scope the contract’s licensed powers

Identify contractual powers (e.g., expansion rights, automatic renewals, lock-in clauses) that could create irreversible outcomes or limit future decision-making space if exercised over time.

Step 2
Define credible pre-threshold signals

Establish clear, observable indicators that trigger a procedural reassessment. This avoids the need for external scientific or legal adjudication by focusing on observable signs of instability or loss of reversibility.

Step 3
Embed pre-threshold procedures through the c-ECO Clause

Translate the license into specific contractual language: a c-ECO Clause that conditions the effectiveness of the relevant powers on processes like reassessment, justification, documentation, and recalibration as circumstances evolve.

Step 4
Assign internal institutional roles

Determine which internal teams (legal, governance, risk management) own the process and how decisions are recorded. The goal is to demonstrate preserved governability through traceable procedures.

Step 5 (Optional)
Seek verification via the c-ECO Pre-Threshold Contract Seal

Organizations can optionally apply for a third-party “Seal” to verify that their contractual architecture incorporates these pre-threshold governance standards. The Seal verifies the design, not the outcomes.

Priority participation categories

Infrastructure & Energy
Long-horizon CAPEX, procurement, concessions

Supply Chains
Standards, audits, exclusivity, long-term sourcing

Finance & Insurance
Risk allocation, covenants, rollover structures

Digital & AI Systems
Platform dependence, model deployment, lock-in

If your sector is not listed, you may still participate. The study is designed around contractual architecture, not sector-specific regulation.
