ARTICLE I — CSAM REFERENCE
[SOW-ART-001] Section 1.1 — Internal Methodological Governance
This SOW is executed under the authority of internal Case-Specific Analytical Mandate (CSAM) reference [CSAM-YYYY-FELLOW-SEQ], which establishes:
I — Authorized analytical domains and c-ECO variable applications;
II — Pre-threshold signal protocols and temporal validity constraints;
III — Data governance requirements per CDGA (Exhibit B);
IV — Living Lab engagement protocols per LLEP (Exhibit C) if applicable;
V — Quality assurance and Foundation oversight checkpoints.
CSAM Confidentiality
The CSAM is an internal Program document and shall not be disclosed in full to the Client. Relevant methodological constraints and quality standards are incorporated into this SOW by reference.
[SOW-ART-002] Section 1.2 — Foundation Oversight Integration
For SOWs involving novel applications, sensitive sectors, or pre-threshold analysis, the Foundation may require:
I — Pre-execution review and approval of methodology;
II — Mid-project checkpoint review;
III — Final Deliverable doctrinal coherence review;
IV — Direct Client communication regarding c-ECO framework integrity (rare, reserved for material breaches).
ARTICLE II — CLIENT OBLIGATIONS
[SOW-ART-003] Section 2.1 — Data and Access
The Client shall provide timely access to:
I — Relevant operational data, contracts, and institutional documents;
II — Key personnel for interviews and clarification;
III — Physical or digital access to case environments as required;
IV — Third-party data permissions where applicable.
[SOW-ART-004] Section 2.2 — Cooperation and Timeliness
Client delays in providing required inputs may extend timelines proportionally. The Institute shall not be liable for delays caused by Client non-performance.
ARTICLE III — CHANGES AND MODIFICATIONS
[SOW-ART-005] Section 3.1 — Change Control
Changes to scope, timeline, or fees require written Change Order signed by both Parties. Changes affecting c-ECO methodology require Foundation approval.
[SOW-ART-006] Section 3.2 — No Oral Modifications
No oral agreements, email exchanges, or informal understandings shall modify this SOW. Only written Change Orders signed by authorized representatives are binding.
ARTICLE IV — ACCEPTANCE AND CLOSE-OUT
[SOW-ART-007] Section 4.1 — Acceptance Criteria
Deliverables are accepted when:
I — Submitted in agreed format and within scope;
II — Reviewed by Client within specified review period;
III — No material objections raised in writing;
IV — Formal acceptance sign-off provided (or deemed accepted if no response within review period).
[SOW-ART-008] Section 4.2 — Project Close-Out
Upon completion:
I — Final payment becomes due upon acceptance;
II — OCAP validation process commences (if applicable);
III — Data retention per CDGA and MSA Article VI;
IV — Case closed in Fellowship tracking systems.
