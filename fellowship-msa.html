Exhibit F — Master Service Agreement
MSA Template | c-ECO Fellowship Program
Template Instrument — Authorized Use Only
This Master Service Agreement ("MSA") is provided as Exhibit F to the Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA). It establishes the framework for ongoing service relationships between Silvio Meira Institute LLC and clients seeking c-ECO analytical services.
Execution Authority: This MSA must be executed by Silvio Meira Institute LLC as service provider, with Johann Christian Hasse Foundation as beneficiary of IP protections. The Fellow acts as Authorized Representative for negotiations and project execution.
SOW Integration: Specific projects are governed by Statements of Work (Exhibit G) that incorporate this MSA by reference and may reference internal Case-Specific Analytical Mandates (CSAM).
PARTIES
Service Provider (Signatory)
Silvio Meira Institute LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, with offices at 4215 Lindy Circle, Orlando, FL 32827, USA ("Institute" or "Service Provider")

Role: Primary contracting party for service delivery, project management, and client relations. Operational executor of c-ECO analytical services.
IP Beneficiary & Framework Steward (Third-Party Beneficiary)
Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, a non-profit organization, with offices at 2504 Grandview Circle, Columbia, MO 65203, USA ("Foundation")

Role: Steward of the c-ECO framework; beneficiary of all IP protections, methodological controls, and quality assurance provisions herein. Entitled to enforce provisions protecting c-ECO integrity.
Authorized Representative (Non-Party)
Fellow
[FELLOW NAME], c-ECO Fellow, authorized under FPA to represent the Institute in client negotiations, project scoping, and analytical execution. The Fellow is not a party to this MSA and assumes no contractual liability hereunder.
Client (Counterparty)
[CLIENT ENTITY NAME], a [jurisdiction] [entity type], with principal offices at [address] ("Client").
PREAMBLE
WHEREAS, the Institute provides systemic governance analysis and related professional services based on the c-ECO framework, as licensed and stewarded by the Foundation;
WHEREAS, the Client desires to engage the Institute for such services on a project-by-project basis, as specified in Statements of Work;
WHEREAS, the Foundation, as steward of the c-ECO framework, requires that all applications of its methodology adhere to strict standards of integrity, coherence, and systemic compatibility;
WHEREAS, this MSA establishes the general terms and conditions governing all such engagements, with specific scopes, deliverables, and fees defined in subsequent SOWs;
WHEREAS, the Client acknowledges that the Foundation is an intended third-party beneficiary of all provisions protecting the c-ECO framework and may enforce such provisions independently;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and conditions herein, the Parties agree as follows:
ARTICLE I — DEFINITIONS
[MSA-ART-001] Section 1.1 — Defined Terms
Term Definition
"c-ECO Framework" The systemic governance methodology, including TFP variables (Position, Velocity, Uncertainty, Reversibility), pre-threshold principles, temporal validity protocols (−Στο), sectoral architectures, and associated analytical tools, as stewarded by the Foundation.
"Confidential Information" All non-public information disclosed by any Party, including c-ECO methodology, client data, business strategies, and technical information, as further defined in Article VI.
"CSAM" Case-Specific Analytical Mandate — internal Program document defining scope, methodology, and governance for a specific c-ECO application. Not shared in full with Client but may be referenced in SOWs.
"Deliverables" All reports, analyses, models, data sets, presentations, and other work product produced by the Institute under an SOW.
"OCAP" Outcome-Conditioned Analytical Participation — discretionary value-sharing mechanism based on validated systemic effects, as defined in Article IV.
"Services" Professional services to be performed by the Institute under this MSA and applicable SOWs, including c-ECO-based analysis, advisory support, and related activities.
"SOW" Statement of Work — Exhibit G template executed for each specific project, incorporating this MSA by reference and defining project-specific scope, timeline, deliverables, and fees.
ARTICLE II — SCOPE OF SERVICES
[MSA-ART-002] Section 2.1 — General Description
The Institute shall provide the Client with systemic governance analysis and related professional services based on the c-ECO framework, as specified in individual SOWs executed under this MSA.
(a) Core Capabilities: Systemic structure analysis, threshold proximity assessment, reversibility evaluation (Lr), uncertainty mapping (σ), temporal dynamics analysis (ΔV), and sector-specific applications (water, energy, agriculture, mining, finance, AI/infrastructure, coastal systems).
(b) Service Forms: Diagnostic analysis, contractual architecture review, governance design support, signal monitoring, and strategic advisory — all within the bounds of c-ECO methodology and the Fellow's authorized scope under FPA.
[MSA-ART-003] Section 2.2 — SOW Process
Each engagement shall be governed by an SOW (Exhibit G) that:
I — Is authorized by the Institute prior to execution;
II — Incorporates this MSA by reference;
III — References the applicable internal CSAM (without disclosure of sensitive methodological details);
IV — Defines specific scope, Deliverables, timeline, fees, and any OCAP terms;
V — Requires Client acknowledgment of c-ECO Terms & Conditions (incorporated by reference).
SOW — Statement of Work (Exhibit G)
The SOW is the operational document that activates this MSA for each specific project. It bridges the internal CSAM (methodological governance) with the commercial client relationship. No Services shall commence without an executed SOW.
[MSA-ART-004] Section 2.3 — No Guaranteed Outcomes
The Client acknowledges that:
I — c-ECO analysis provides systemic assessment, not prediction or guarantee of specific outcomes;
II — Analytical outputs are conditional upon data quality, temporal validity, and scope boundaries defined in the SOW;
III — The Institute does not warrant that recommended actions will produce specific results;
IV — The Client retains sole responsibility for decisions based on analytical outputs.
ARTICLE III — c-ECO FRAMEWORK AUTHORIZATION
[MSA-ART-005] Section 3.1 — Conditional Authorization Structure
The Client's access to and use of the c-ECO framework is governed by a three-layer authorization:
Layer Instrument Function
Framework c-ECO Terms & Conditions (public) General conditions of framework authorization, incorporated by reference in this MSA
Framework This MSA (Exhibit F) Service relationship framework, IP protection, governance standards
Operational SOW (Exhibit G) + internal CSAM Case-specific scope, deliverables, analytical boundaries, OCAP terms
Critical Distinction
The Client receives conditional authorization to benefit from c-ECO analysis, not a license to use the framework independently. All c-ECO IP remains with the Foundation; analytical outputs are Deliverables under specific engagement terms.
[MSA-ART-006] Section 3.2 — No Framework License Granted
The Client expressly acknowledges that:
I — No general, perpetual, or transferable license to the c-ECO framework is granted;
II — Authorization is case-specific, time-bound, and contingent upon ongoing compliance;
III — The Client may not apply c-ECO concepts, terminology, or methodology outside the scope of the specific SOW without separate authorization;
IV — The Client may not train internal personnel in c-ECO methods, develop derivative frameworks, or represent itself as c-ECO-capable independent of the Institute;
V — All rights to the c-ECO framework, including improvements suggested by the engagement, remain with the Foundation.
[MSA-ART-007] Section 3.3 — Foundation Oversight Rights
The Foundation, as third-party beneficiary, retains the right to:
I — Review and approve any SOW involving novel or sensitive c-ECO applications;
II — Audit Deliverables for methodological integrity and doctrinal coherence;
III — Suspend or revoke framework authorization for breaches of c-ECO standards;
IV — Require modifications to analytical approach to protect framework integrity.
§1 — The Institute shall cooperate with Foundation oversight and implement required modifications.
§2 — Client shall not interfere with or resist Foundation oversight activities.
[MSA-ART-008] Section 3.4 — Incorporation of Public Terms
The c-ECO Terms & Conditions for Services, publicly available at https://c-eco.org/service-terms, are incorporated herein by reference. In case of conflict, the provisions of this MSA shall govern, except where the public Terms establish stricter protective standards.
ARTICLE IV — FEES AND OCAP
[MSA-ART-009] Section 4.1 — Fee Structure
Fees for Services shall be specified in each SOW and may include:
I — Fixed Fees: Agreed amount for defined scope and Deliverables;
II — Time-Based Fees: Hourly or daily rates for advisory services, subject to caps where specified;
III — Retainer: Monthly or periodic fixed payment for ongoing advisory access;
IV — Expenses: Pre-approved travel, accommodation, and out-of-pocket costs, documented and invoiced at cost.
[MSA-ART-010] Section 4.2 — OCAP — Outcome-Conditioned Analytical Participation
Where specified in an SOW, the Client and Institute may agree to OCAP terms, whereby a portion of Institute compensation is contingent upon validated systemic effects.
(a) Condition Precedent: OCAP payments require: (i) licensed c-ECO application; (ii) validated contribution to identified systemic outcome; (iii) independent verification where specified; (iv) no breach of methodological standards.
(b) Measurement: Systemic effects shall be measured against c-ECO-modeled thresholds, with methodology specified in the SOW.
(c) Discretionary Nature: OCAP participation remains discretionary and subject to Foundation validation of analytical integrity. No OCAP entitlement arises from Client's unilateral assessment of outcomes.
(d) Fellow Participation: Where OCAP is activated, the authorized Fellow may participate in value distribution per FPA terms, without creating employment or partnership with Client.
[MSA-ART-011] Section 4.3 — Payment Terms
Standard payment terms: Net 30 days from invoice date. Late payments subject to 1.5% monthly service charge. The Institute may suspend Services for material payment default.
ARTICLE V — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND DATA
[MSA-ART-012] Section 5.1 — c-ECO Framework Ownership
The c-ECO framework and all associated IP are and shall remain the exclusive property of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation. No transfer of rights occurs through service delivery, Client payment, or OCAP participation.
[MSA-ART-013] Section 5.2 — Deliverables Ownership
Deliverables produced under this MSA are governed as follows:
Deliverable Type Ownership Client Rights
c-ECO-based analysis, reports, assessments Joint (Institute + Foundation) Limited license for internal use per SOW scope; no modification, sublicensing, or external distribution
Client-specific data, case materials Client Full ownership; license to Institute for project execution
Methodological improvements suggested by engagement Foundation No rights; acknowledgment in publications where appropriate
[MSA-ART-014] Section 5.3 — Data Governance
All Client data shall be handled per the CDGA standards (Exhibit B) and any SOW-specific data governance addendum. Cross-border transfers shall comply with applicable data protection laws (LGPD, GDPR, etc.).
ARTICLE VI — CONFIDENTIALITY
[MSA-ART-015] Section 6.1 — MNDA Integration
This MSA incorporates by reference the MNDA (Exhibit E) executed between the Parties (or to be executed prior to disclosure). In case of conflict, the stricter standard shall apply.
[MSA-ART-016] Section 6.2 — Enhanced c-ECO Protections
The Client acknowledges that c-ECO Confidential Information requires enhanced protection:
I — Pre-threshold signal concepts, threshold analytics, and systemic instability indicators shall not be disclosed to third parties without Foundation authorization;
II — TFP variable applications, sectoral architectures, and methodological combinations shall not be documented or reverse-engineered for independent use;
III — All Deliverables containing c-ECO analysis shall be marked with appropriate attribution and confidentiality designations.
ARTICLE VII — TERM AND RENEWAL
[MSA-ART-017] Section 7.1 — Initial Term
This MSA shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for one (1) year, unless earlier terminated.
[MSA-ART-018] Section 7.2 — Renewal
This MSA shall automatically renew for successive one-year periods unless either Party gives 60 days' prior written notice of non-renewal. All executed SOWs shall survive MSA termination or non-renewal until completed or terminated per their terms.
ARTICLE VIII — TERMINATION
[MSA-ART-019] Section 8.1 — Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this MSA or any SOW with 30 days' written notice. Client remains liable for all fees incurred prior to termination and for non-cancellable commitments.
[MSA-ART-020] Section 8.2 — Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate immediately for material breach uncured within 15 days of notice. The Institute may terminate immediately for:
I — Client's unauthorized use or disclosure of c-ECO Confidential Information;
II — Client's attempt to apply c-ECO methodology outside SOW scope;
III — Client's interference with Foundation oversight rights;
IV — Material payment default.
ARTICLE IX — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
[MSA-ART-021] Section 9.1 — General Limitation
The Institute's aggregate liability under this MSA and all SOWs shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the 12 months preceding the claim.
[MSA-ART-022] Section 9.2 — Excluded Damages
Neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages, including lost profits, lost data, or business interruption, even if advised of the possibility.
[MSA-ART-023] Section 9.3 — No Liability for Client Decisions
The Institute shall not be liable for any decisions, actions, or omissions taken by Client or third parties based on Deliverables. The Client assumes full responsibility for operational implementation of analytical insights.
ARTICLE X — GOVERNANCE AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
[MSA-ART-024] Section 10.1 — Governing Law
This MSA shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict of law principles.
[MSA-ART-025] Section 10.2 — Arbitration
Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under ICC Rules, with seat in New York, consistent with the FPA. The Foundation may intervene as third-party beneficiary in disputes affecting c-ECO interests.
[MSA-ART-026] Section 10.3 — Injunctive Relief
The Institute and Foundation (independently) may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm to c-ECO IP or Confidential Information.
ARTICLE XI — GENERAL PROVISIONS
[MSA-ART-027] Section 11.1 — Entire Agreement
This MSA, together with executed SOWs, the incorporated c-ECO Terms & Conditions, and applicable MNDA, constitutes the entire agreement regarding Services.
[MSA-ART-028] Section 11.2 — Amendment
This MSA may only be amended by written instrument signed by authorized representatives of the Institute and Client. The Foundation's consent is required for amendments affecting c-ECO IP protections.
[MSA-ART-029] Section 11.3 — Assignment
The Client may not assign this MSA without Institute consent. The Institute may assign to successor entities or affiliates with Foundation notification.
[MSA-ART-030] Section 11.4 — Third-Party Beneficiary
The Foundation is an express third-party beneficiary of all provisions protecting c-ECO IP, methodological integrity, and quality standards, with independent right of enforcement.
[MSA-ART-031] Section 11.5 — Counterparts
This MSA may be executed in counterparts, with electronic signatures having the same legal effect as originals.
