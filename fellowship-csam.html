Exhibit D — Case-Specific Analytical Mandate
CSAM | c-ECO Fellowship Program Contractual Framework
Instrument Context
This Case-Specific Analytical Mandate (CSAM) constitutes Exhibit D to the c-ECO Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA). It transforms c-ECO theory into operational reality by establishing precise parameters, scope limitations, signal architecture, and output requirements for individual analytical engagement.
This instrument operates in conjunction with the Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI), the Confidentiality & Data Governance Agreement (CDGA), the Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP), and, where applicable, the Outcome-Conditioned Analytical Participation Framework (OCAP). This Mandate structures analytical engagement only. It does not, by itself, authorize licensed operational use of c-ECO in real-world environments. In case of conflict, the FPA governs.
PREAMBLE
WHEREAS, the c-ECO Fellowship Program requires precise operationalization of analytical work through concrete case environments;
WHEREAS, this Mandate establishes the specific parameters, scope limitations, signal architecture, and output requirements for an individual Fellow's authorized analytical engagement;
WHEREAS, the CSAM functions as the bridge between the Fellowship's general governance architecture and the particularities of real-world systemic analysis;
NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE II — ANALYTICAL SCOPE AND BOUNDARIES
[CSAM-ART-004] Section 2.1 — Purpose of This Mandate
The purpose of this Mandate is to authorize bounded analytical engagement with the designated case environment in order to produce c-ECO-consistent observation, variable application, signal assessment, and structured outputs within the limits defined herein.
[CSAM-ART-005] Section 2.2 — Authorized Analytical Domain
The Fellow is authorized to analyze the following aspects of the case environment:

Temporal Dynamics ☐ ☐ Velocity vectors, trend trajectories
Threshold Proximity ☐ ☐ Pre-threshold signal identification
Reversibility Assessment ☐ ☐ Lr (liquidity of reversibility) analysis
Uncertainty Mapping ☐ ☐ σ (uncertainty) variable application
Contractual Architecture ☐ ☐ Existing agreements, guarantee structures
Regulatory Coherence ☐ ☐ Compliance gaps, enforcement patterns
Financial Transmission ☐ ☐ Risk propagation to credit/guarantee systems
[CSAM-ART-006] Section 2.3 — Explicit Scope Exclusions
Without written amendment to this Mandate, the Fellow shall NOT:
I — Analyze systems or jurisdictions outside the Geographic Scope
II — Apply c-ECO methodologies to cases not specified herein
III — Extend temporal projections beyond the specified horizon
IV — Make comparative assessments with other Fellows' cases without authorization
V — Develop prescriptive recommendations for case subjects
VI — Generate outputs suggesting causation where only correlation is established
VII — Engage in unsupported generalization or unwarranted transferability of findings
VIII — Create any implied certification or advisory overreach
[CSAM-ART-007] Section 2.4 — Cross-Case Prohibitions
The Fellow is prohibited from:
I — Merging data from this case with other Fellows' cases
II — Creating composite indices or benchmarks using unauthorized case comparisons
III — Referencing this case in publications or presentations without de-identification and approval
[CSAM-ART-007A] Section 2.5 — License Dependence for Real-World Use
This Mandate structures the analytical scope of the approved case environment. It does not, by itself, authorize operational, contractual, regulatory, or institutional application of the c-ECO framework in real-world settings.
I — Where implementation or real-case use is intended, a separate licensing pathway may be required
II — This CSAM may support, but does not automatically generate, license activation
III — No presumption of enforceable, operational, or institutional authority shall arise from the existence of a CSAM alone
IV — Any licensed use, where applicable, remains subject to separate institutional authorization and additional conditions
License Clarification
Fellowship admission and case authorization permit bounded analytical work. Licensed real-world use of c-ECO remains a distinct institutional layer.

[CSAM-ART-010A] Section 3.4 — Potential Effect Categories
Where relevant, this Mandate may identify potential effect categories capable of later institutional review under the Outcome-Conditioned Analytical Participation Framework (OCAP). Such identification does not create entitlement, automatic recognition, or economic participation by itself.
I — Reversibility Preservation (Lr): measurable increase, protection, or preservation of system reversibility capacity
II — Threshold Avoidance: demonstrable prevention of transition into critical or irreversible system states
III — Safe Mode Activation: timely classification and institutional transition under Red or Black band conditions
IV — Contractual Integration: incorporation of c-ECO-compatible logic, clauses, or structures into enforceable instruments
V — Institutional Adoption: integration of c-ECO logic into governance, regulatory, financial, or operational frameworks
§1. Identification of a potential effect category within this Mandate is analytical only and does not, by itself, certify that such effect has occurred.
§2. Any later recognition of effect for institutional or allocation purposes remains subject to separate validation by the competent c-ECO authority.

ARTICLE VI — LIMITATION OF INTERPRETATION
[CSAM-ART-017] Section 6.1 — Analytical Boundaries
The Fellow is explicitly prohibited from:
I — Causal Claims: Stating that c-ECO analysis "proves" causation between variables
II — Predictive Assertions: Presenting projections as certain outcomes rather than conditional scenarios
III — Prescriptive Conclusions: Recommending specific actions for case subjects
IV — Comparative Generalization: Extending findings from this case to other contexts without authorization
V — Unsupported Generalization: Applying findings beyond the evidence base
VI — Unwarranted Transferability: Assuming applicability to different jurisdictions or systems
VII — Implied Certification: Suggesting Foundation endorsement of findings
VIII — Advisory Overreach: Providing guidance outside analytical scope
[CSAM-ART-018] Section 6.2 — Mandatory Disclaimers
All outputs shall include the following standard disclaimer:
"This analysis applies the c-ECO systemic governance framework [version] to the specific case environment designated in CSAM-[identifier]. Findings are valid within the temporal and scope parameters defined in the Mandate. Application to other cases, jurisdictions, or time periods requires separate validation. This analysis does not constitute legal, financial, or operational advice. No certification, endorsement, or institutional adoption inference shall be drawn from this output."
[CSAM-ART-019] Section 6.3 — No Seal / No Certification / No Adoption Inference
The Fellow shall not imply, state, or allow to be inferred that:
I — The case has been "certified" by c-ECO or the Foundation
II — The output constitutes Foundation approval of the case or its subjects
III — The case is endorsed by the Foundation, ISM, or any partner institution
IV — The analysis triggers or enables formal institutional adoption of c-ECO
V — The Fellow's engagement represents institutional partnership with case subjects
[CSAM-ART-020] Section 6.4 — Interpretive Authority
Only the Foundation's designated methodological review authority may authorize:
I — Upgrade of analytical findings to broader applicability
II — Use of c-ECO seal or certification on case outputs
III — Reference to this case in Foundation publications or communications

ARTICLE VIII — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND ATTRIBUTION
[CSAM-ART-024] Section 8.1 — Ownership and Use Rights
Ownership and use rights relating to deliverables produced under this Mandate shall be governed by the Fellowship Participation Agreement and any applicable project-specific written determination of the Foundation.
[CSAM-ART-025] Section 8.2 — Attribution
Where attribution is appropriate, the Fellow shall be credited in accordance with Foundation attribution protocols and any applicable confidentiality limitations:
"Analytical contribution by [Fellow Name], c-ECO Fellow, under CSAM-[identifier], © [Year] Johann Christian Hasse Foundation."
[CSAM-ART-026] Section 8.3 — Publication and External Use
No deliverable produced under this Mandate may be published, externally circulated, certified, or represented as institutional output without prior written approval from the Foundation.
[CSAM-ART-027] Section 8.4 — Case Anonymization
Publication or dissemination of case materials requires:
I — De-identification of case subjects unless explicit authorization obtained
II — Removal of location-specific details that could enable re-identification
III — Foundation approval of all public references to the case
ARTICLE IX — AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION
[CSAM-ART-028] Section 9.1 — Mandate Amendment
This CSAM may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties and attached as a Schedule to this Mandate.
[CSAM-ART-029] Section 9.2 — Grounds for Termination
The Foundation may terminate this Mandate upon:
I — Material breach of MAI, CDGA, or LLEP provisions
II — Failure to meet review gate requirements
III — Discovery of scope violations or unauthorized activities
IV — Safety, legal, or ethical concerns arising from case engagement
V — Mutual agreement of the parties
[CSAM-ART-030] Section 9.3 — Post-Termination Deliverables
Upon termination, the Fellow shall submit:
I — All work product completed to date
II — Data inventory and classification documentation
III — Case closure memo describing status at termination
ARTICLE X — MANDATE EXHAUSTIVENESS
[CSAM-ART-031] Section 10.1 — Exhaustiveness Clause
The Fellow is authorized to act only within the analytical, territorial, temporal, and procedural boundaries expressly stated in this Mandate. No silence in this Mandate shall be interpreted as authorization.
[CSAM-ART-032] Section 10.2 — Integration with Other Instruments
This Mandate constitutes the complete operational specification for the Fellow's case engagement, integrating with:
I — Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA): General Fellowship terms, admission conditions, and governing hierarchy
II — Exhibit A — MAI: Methodological adherence
III — Exhibit B — CDGA: Data governance and confidentiality
IV — Exhibit C — LLEP: Field engagement and Living Lab conduct
V — Exhibit E — OCAP (where applicable): Outcome-conditioned analytical participation linked to validated systemic effects in licensed cases
[CSAM-ART-033] Section 10.3 — Governing Law and Dispute Resolution
This Mandate shall be governed by the laws and dispute resolution mechanisms specified in the Fellowship Participation Agreement.
