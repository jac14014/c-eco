The Mining Signal Detection Problem

The central challenge in mining governance is that failure often becomes visible only after reversibility has already contracted. The c-ECO approach does not wait for collapse. It looks for converging signals that indicate when a mining system is losing its capacity to stabilize, contain, or restore itself.

Critical Slowing Down in Geotechnical Systems
λ → 0

As a tailings structure, slope, or waste pile approaches instability, its recovery from stress events becomes slower. Small disturbances produce longer deformation response, delayed drainage, or repeated displacement.

Mining Application: A tailings wall that previously stabilized after rainfall in 48 hours now requires several weeks to return to baseline pressure.

Rising Variance and Flickering
σ² ↑

Mining systems near thresholds may show irregular pressure spikes, seepage fluctuations, turbidity changes, crack expansion, or oscillation between apparently safe and unsafe readings.

Mining Application: Turbidity and seepage data that were historically stable begin showing sharp swings after rainfall or blasting cycles.

Spatial Correlation and Coupled Failure
r ↑

When multiple monitoring points begin moving together, local anomalies may indicate system-wide coupling. What looked isolated becomes a shared trajectory.

Mining Application: Pore pressure, slope displacement, seepage, and downstream turbidity begin deteriorating together across the same drainage basin.

Decision Points

Decision Point 1 — 2022: Pore pressure and seepage variance are rising, but the facility remains formally compliant. Do you:

  1. Maintain ordinary monitoring because no collapse has occurred.
  2. Trigger Amber protocol: increase monitoring, require stabilization plan, restrict new tailings loading.
  3. Trigger Red protocol immediately and suspend deposition.

Decision Point 2 — 2024: Displacement accelerates, recovery time after rainfall reaches 21 days, and Lr falls below 0.5. The operator argues that a shutdown would be financially damaging. Do you:

  1. Wait for a formal regulatory order.
  2. Activate Safe Mode: suspend new deposition, redirect cash to stabilization, notify governance actors.
  3. Activate Restoration First: treat continuation as systemically incompatible and prioritize containment/restoration.
