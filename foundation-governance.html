Governance Principles

The Foundation’s governance model is designed to preserve independence, prevent conflicts of interest, and support the long-term integrity of the c-ECO initiative.

Collective Fiduciary Oversight.
Board members exercise fiduciary oversight collectively, in accordance with the Foundation’s bylaws, conflict-of-interest standards, and nonprofit governance obligations.
Separation of Roles.
Executive officers administer the Foundation. Directors provide oversight. Technical and social-impact portfolios support the integrity of the Foundation’s systemic governance mission.
Institutional Independence.
Board and officer roles do not imply representation of any external employer, university, public agency, law firm, institute, or affiliated organization unless expressly authorized.
