Pre-threshold contractual governance

Systemic risk · temporal authority · reversibility

Framework

c-eco is a research framework and institutional platform focused on how contracts allocate authority over time under conditions of systemic risk and potential irreversibility.

It treats contracts as temporal governance architectures: they do not merely unfold over time; they stabilize trajectories, distribute tolerances, and shape what future interventions remain legally and materially possible.

The core focus is preserving reversibility before thresholds consolidate — where ex post remedies arrive too late.

For institutional partnerships and research inquiries, a detailed Concept Note is available upon request.
