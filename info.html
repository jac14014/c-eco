c-ECO

Contractual Equity & Ecological Co-Responsibility

c-ECO is an original research framework and institutional platform focused on pre-threshold contractual governance under conditions of systemic risk and potential irreversibility. It examines how contracts allocate authority over time — and how legality can remain compliant while decision-space quietly collapses.

The framework reconceptualizes contracts as temporal governance architectures: they do not merely unfold over time; they stabilize trajectories, distribute tolerances, and shape what future interventions remain legally and materially possible. c-ECO focuses on preserving reversibility before thresholds consolidate — where ex post remedies arrive too late.

c-ECO engages with foundations, research institutions, policy labs, and development actors through applied research partnerships, Concept Notes, pilot implementation pathways, and institutional collaborations focused on pre-threshold decision-making and systemic risk governance.

For institutional partnerships and research inquiries, a detailed Concept Note is available upon request.

Founded by Jacqueline A. Ennis.
