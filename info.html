c-ECO

Contractual Equity
Framework

An original research framework and institutional platform focused on pre-threshold contractual governance under conditions of systemic risk and potential irreversibility. It examines how contracts allocate authority over time — and how legality can remain formally compliant while decision-space quietly collapses.

Contracts are treated as temporal governance architectures: they do not merely unfold over time; they stabilize trajectories, distribute tolerances, and shape what future interventions remain legally and materially possible. The focus is preserving reversibility before thresholds consolidate — where ex post remedies arrive too late.

For institutional partnerships and research inquiries, a detailed Concept Note is available upon request.
