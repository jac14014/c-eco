CECO-GOV-001
Governance Charter for Economic Claims — EGS v1.2
Preamble

The c-ECO Doctrine was built on the principle of "pre-threshold responsibility" — the obligation to act before decisions become irreversible. This principle applies, meta-recursively, to the Foundation's own economic claims about its value.

Six-Authority Rule

Every economic claim passes through 6 distinct authorities sequentially, without exception:

Authority Function Actor Document
CREATE Originate (hypothesis or benchmark) VP Research + Lead Architect Working Paper (CECO-WP-NNNN)
REVIEW Classify (🟢🟡🔴) IVC (3 members) Evidence Review (IVC-REV-001)
APPROVE Authorize (for external use) Board of Trustees Board Resolution + CEP-LOG
USE Apply (appropriate channel) Business Owner (Accountable) CEP-LOG entry
MONITORING Verify (permanence of validity) VP Research + IVC Analytics Monitoring Report (IVC-MON-001)
RETIRE Retire (shelf life) IVC + Board Degradation Notice + Archive
Non-Delegable Powers

□ Approval of Methodology (CECO-DOC-001)

□ Approval of Commercial Evidence Policy (CECO-CEP-001)

□ Approval of IVC Terms of Reference (CECO-IVC-TOR-001)

□ Approval of this Charter (CECO-GOV-001)

□ Institutional Upgrade: 🟡→🟢 for grants and due diligence

□ Degradation of 🟢→🟡 impacting projections >$500K

□ Retirement of metrics in active contracts

□ Suspension or revocation of the EGS (sunset clause)

Section 5A — Monitoring Authority

Purpose: Monitor economic claims after use to verify permanence of validity. MONITORING is not passive. It implies measurement, detection, updating, and trigger.

Triggers (7 types):

□ Benchmark reached 75% of shelf life

□ Deviation >20% between projected and observed

□ Contradictory pilot result

□ Relevant regulatory change

□ New external evidence

□ Board request

□ Whistleblower report received

Outputs:

Result Definition Consequence
MAINTAIN Claim remains valid within tolerance No action; update CEP-LOG
ADAPT Claim remains valid but requires adjustment Replace benchmark; update Working Paper; notify Business Owner
DEGRADE Claim loses empirical validity 🟢→🟡 or 🟡→🔴; update all materials; suspend external use
ESCALATE Claim questioned with significant institutional impact Submit to Board; suspend use until decision
RETIRE Claim obsolete or irreversibly invalidated Retire; archive; notify all affected Business Owners
Handoff Rule

USE does not end responsibility.
Responsibility only ends when:
MONITORING → RETIRE
or
MONITORING → revalidation

Return Path

MONITORING → REVIEW: When monitoring detects that a 🟢 claim has lost empirical support but still has hypothetical value, the output is not RETIRE. It is DEGRADE → REVIEW.

The IVC reopens classification, reevaluates the causal chain, and issues a new classification (🟡 or 🔴). The cycle restarts at REVIEW, allowing the claim to be recovered if new evidence emerges — preserving the reversibility of the EGS itself.

Prohibition on Resurrection

No retired metric may be reused without complete revalidation.

Complete revalidation = new Working Paper + new peer review + new IVC classification + new CEP-LOG registration.

This prevents: cherry-picking, reviving old benchmarks, "resurrecting" ROI, reusing without updating assumptions.

Sunset Clause

If within 24 months of approval:

□ <50% of 🟢 metrics are revalidated, OR

□ <3 🟢 evidences are generated by pilots, OR

□ IVC is not constituted, OR

□ <80% shelf-life compliance

Then this Charter is suspended and the Board reverts to direct governance of all economic claims.

The Foundation will not operate with ungoverned economic claims.

Economic Impact Methodology
CECO-DOC-001 — How We Calculate
Evidence Classification

🟢 DEMONSTRATED — Published benchmark by independent third party. Citable source; reproducible method; age ≤3 years.

Example: Predictive maintenance reduces emergency costs by 15-25% (Oxmaint 2025)

🟡 MODELLED — c-ECO hypothesis sustained by framework architecture. Internal peer review; explicit causal chain; requires pilot validation.

Example: Pre-threshold governance reduces litigation by 15-30%

🔴 CONCEPTUAL — c-ECO concept without direct quantitative validation. NEVER in due diligence or grant application. Presented as STRATEGIC CAPABILITY enabled.

Example: Reversibility liquidity (NOT quantifiable in cash)

Mandatory Disclaimers

🟢 DEMONSTRATED: "Based on [SOURCE], published [DATE]. See Economic Impact Methodology (CECO-DOC-001) for full citation and methodology."

🟡 MODELLED: "Based on c-ECO internal hypothesis, not yet validated by independent study. See Working Paper [REF] for assumptions and limitations."

🔴 CONCEPTUAL: "This represents a conceptual capability of the c-ECO framework, not a guaranteed outcome. No empirical validation exists. See Commercial Evidence Policy (CECO-CEP-001) for classification."

Prohibited Combinations

□ Summing 🟢 + 🟡 + 🔴 in a single "ROI" or "total savings" number

□ Presenting 🔴 as 🟢 in any channel

□ Omitting 🟡 disclaimer in grant or due diligence

□ Using 🟢 benchmark >3 years without revalidation

□ Citing "internal studies" without published hypothesis document

□ Promising specific savings ("you will save $X") without performance contract or completed pilot

Board Resolution
CECO-BRD-001 — How We Decide
Proposed Resolution

BE IT RESOLVED THAT

the Board of Trustees of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation approves the conditional launch of the c-ECO Pilot Program with the following parameters:

1. SCOPE: Maximum of 5 (five) pilot engagements in Year 1, selected from priority sectors per the Sector Allocation Framework.

2. BUDGET: Total allocation of $150,000 for pilot execution, inclusive of fellow time, technical setup, and Impact Report production.

3. GOVERNANCE:

• Annual review of pilot outcomes by the Independent Validation Committee

• Quarterly reporting to the Board on pilot progress, conversion rates, and evidence quality

• No external claims of ROI, savings, or economic impact prior to completion of at least 3 (three) pilots with validated Impact Reports

4. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT:

• Approval of the Commercial Evidence Policy (CECO-CEP-001)

• Constitution of the Independent Validation Committee

• Execution of standard Pilot Agreement templates by all participating clients

5. SUNSET CLAUSE:

If conversion rate falls below 40% after 5 pilots, or if no 🟢 DEMONSTRATED evidence is generated within 12 months, the Board shall review continuation of the program.

Vote Required

Simple majority of Trustees present.
