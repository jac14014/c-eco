Governance Charter for Economic Claims — EGS v1.2
Preamble

The c-ECO Doctrine was built on the principle of pre-threshold responsibility — the obligation to act before decisions become irreversible. This principle applies, meta-recursively, to the Foundation's own economic claims about its value.

Six-Authority Rule

Every economic claim passes through six distinct authorities sequentially, without exception.

Authority Function Actor Document
Create Originate hypothesis, benchmark, or economic claim VP Research + Lead Architect Working Paper CECO-WP-NNNN
Review Classify evidence as DEMO, MODEL, or CONCEPT Independent Validation Committee Evidence Review IVC-REV-001
Approve Authorize external use Board of Trustees Board Resolution + CEP-LOG
Use Apply claim in the appropriate channel Business Owner CEP-LOG entry
Monitoring Verify permanence of validity VP Research + IVC Analytics Monitoring Report IVC-MON-001
Retire Remove obsolete or invalid claims from use IVC + Board Degradation Notice + Archive
Non-Delegable Powers

☐ Approval of Methodology CECO-DOC-001

☐ Approval of Commercial Evidence Policy CECO-CEP-001

☐ Approval of IVC Terms of Reference CECO-IVC-TOR-001

☐ Approval of this Charter CECO-GOV-001

☐ Institutional upgrade from MODEL to DEMO for grants and due diligence

☐ Degradation from DEMO to MODEL affecting projections above $500,000

☐ Retirement of metrics used in active contracts

☐ Suspension or revocation of the EGS under the sunset clause

Section 5A — Monitoring Authority

Purpose: Monitoring verifies economic claims after use to confirm the permanence of validity. Monitoring is not passive. It implies measurement, detection, updating, and trigger.

Seven monitoring triggers:

☐ Benchmark reaches 75% of shelf life

☐ Deviation exceeds 20% between projected and observed result

☐ Contradictory pilot result

☐ Relevant regulatory change

☐ New external evidence

☐ Board request

☐ Whistleblower report received

Monitoring outputs:

Result Definition Consequence
Maintain Claim remains valid within tolerance No action; update CEP-LOG
Adapt Claim remains valid but requires adjustment Replace benchmark; update Working Paper; notify Business Owner
Degrade Claim loses empirical validity DEMO to MODEL or MODEL to CONCEPT; update materials; suspend external use
Escalate Claim is questioned with significant institutional impact Submit to Board; suspend use until decision
Retire Claim becomes obsolete or irreversibly invalidated Retire; archive; notify all affected Business Owners
Handoff Rule

USE does not end responsibility.
Responsibility only ends when:
MONITORING → RETIRE
or
MONITORING → REVALIDATION

Return Path

Monitoring returns to Review when a DEMO claim loses empirical support but still has hypothetical or operational value. In that case, the correct output is not immediate retirement. It is degradation followed by review.

The Independent Validation Committee reopens classification, reevaluates the causal chain, and issues a new classification as MODEL or CONCEPT. The cycle restarts at Review, allowing the claim to be recovered only if new evidence later emerges.

Prohibition on Resurrection

No retired metric may be reused without complete revalidation.

Complete revalidation requires a new Working Paper, new peer review, new IVC classification, and new CEP-LOG registration.

This prevents cherry-picking, reviving old benchmarks, resurrecting ROI, or reusing assumptions without updated validation.

Sunset Clause

If within 24 months of approval:

☐ Less than 50% of DEMO metrics are revalidated; or

☐ Fewer than three DEMO evidences are generated by pilots; or

☐ The Independent Validation Committee is not constituted; or

☐ Shelf-life compliance falls below 80%.

Then this Charter is suspended, and the Board reverts to direct governance of all economic claims.

The Foundation will not operate with ungoverned economic claims.

CECO-DOC-001 — How We Calculate
Evidence Classification

✓ Demonstrated Published Benchmark — Source citable; reproducible method; age ≤ 3 years.

Example: Predictive maintenance reduces emergency costs by 15–25%.

~ Modelled c-ECO Hypothesis — Internal peer review; explicit causal chain; requires pilot validation.

Example: Pre-threshold governance reduces litigation exposure by 15–30%.

◊ Conceptual Strategic Capability — No empirical validation. Never used as quantified ROI.

Example: Reversibility liquidity as a governance capability, not as a cash projection.

Mandatory Disclaimers

DEMO Demonstrated: “Based on published or externally verifiable evidence. See Economic Impact Methodology CECO-DOC-001 for citation, assumptions, and methodology.”

MODEL Modelled: “Based on c-ECO internal hypothesis and architecture, not yet validated by independent study. See the corresponding Working Paper for assumptions and limitations.”

CONCEPT Conceptual: “This represents a conceptual capability of the c-ECO framework, not a guaranteed outcome. No empirical validation exists. See Commercial Evidence Policy CECO-CEP-001 for classification.”

Prohibited Combinations

☐ Summing DEMO + MODEL + CONCEPT into a single ROI or total savings number.

☐ Presenting CONCEPT as DEMO in any external or institutional channel.

☐ Omitting MODEL disclaimers in grant, diligence, or board materials.

☐ Using DEMO benchmarks older than three years without revalidation.

☐ Citing internal studies without a published hypothesis document.

☐ Promising specific savings without a performance contract or completed pilot.

Board Resolution
CECO-BRD-001 — How We Decide
Proposed Resolution

Be It Resolved That

The Board of Trustees of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation approves the conditional launch of the c-ECO Pilot Program with the following parameters.

1. Scope: Maximum of five pilot engagements in Year 1, selected from priority sectors under the Sector Allocation Framework.

2. Budget: Total allocation of $150,000 for pilot execution, inclusive of fellow time, technical setup, and Impact Report production.

3. Governance:

• Annual review of pilot outcomes by the Independent Validation Committee.

• Quarterly reporting to the Board on pilot progress, conversion rates, and evidence quality.

• No external claims of ROI, savings, or economic impact before completion of at least three pilots with validated Impact Reports.

4. Conditions Precedent:

• Approval of the Commercial Evidence Policy CECO-CEP-001.

• Constitution of the Independent Validation Committee.

• Execution of standard Pilot Agreement templates by all participating clients.

5. Sunset Clause:

If conversion rate falls below 40% after five pilots, or if no DEMO evidence is generated within 12 months, the Board shall review continuation of the program.

Vote Required

Simple majority of Trustees present, unless bylaws require a higher threshold.

Board Decision Package

☐ Approve CECO-GOV-001 before approving economic claim use.

☐ Approve CECO-CEP-001 before publishing ROI, savings, or economic impact language.

☐ Approve CECO-IVC-TOR-001 before upgrading MODEL evidence to DEMO evidence.

☐ Approve pilot launch only as conditional and evidence-generating, not as validated commercial proof.
