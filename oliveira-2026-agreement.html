1. Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement:

“c-ECO Framework” means the Contractual Equity & Ecological Co-Responsibility doctrinal and methodological system developed by Jacqueline A. Ennis, including the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP), Safe Operating Space (SOS), sectoral workbooks, and associated analytical instruments.

“CSAM” means the Case-Specific Analytical Mandate (Exhibit D to the FPA) or equivalent institutional review pathway adopted by HF for case validation and analytical scope authorization.

“Cycle” means a twelve (12)-month period commencing on the Effective Date or any anniversary thereof, unless otherwise specified by HF in writing.

“Fellowship Admission” means entry into the c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab, granting access to the controlled methodological environment and supervised analytical formation under the FPA, but not authorizing independent real-world deployment of the c-ECO framework.

“FPA” means the Fellowship Participation Agreement executed between HF and each admitted Fellow, governing admission conditions, contractual adherence, methodological obligations, and standing categories.

“HF” means Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, the sole authority for Fellowship admission, certification, fee structures, and doctrinal custody of the c-ECO framework.

“ISM” means Instituto Silvio Meira, the Brazil-based operational partner providing Living Lab logistics, field-linked integration, and local articulation under separate institutional instruments.

“License” means a Case-Based c-ECO License — separate institutional authorization for operational, contractual, regulatory, or commercial use of the c-ECO framework in real-world environments, contingent upon platform availability and case-specific review.

“Standing” means the current institutional validity status of a Fellow under the FPA: Active, Conditional, Lapsed, Suspended, or Revoked, as defined in the Fellowship Portal.

“TFP” means the Threshold Function Protocol, the trajectory-based analytical framework monitoring systemic position (P), velocity (ΔV), uncertainty (σ), and reversibility liquidity (Lr) to determine governance interventions before irreversible thresholds are crossed.

2. Purpose and Scope

2.1 Program Operation

HF operates the c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab as a controlled applied system for systemic governance formation, governed by the TFP, SOS, and current methodological architecture published at c-eco.org/fellowship-portal.html.

2.2 Partner Role

Partner shall act solely as an independent referral and collaboration channel. Partner is not an employee, agent, or representative of HF. Partner does not exercise doctrinal, technical, or certification authority.

2.3 Institutional Discretion

All admissions decisions, certification determinations, license authorizations, and methodological updates remain exclusively within HF’s institutional discretion. Partner may not override, influence, or commit HF to any admission, certification, or licensing outcome.

2.4 Non-Exclusive Structure

This Agreement is non-exclusive. HF may engage additional referral partners, channels, or institutional collaborators without restriction.

2.5 Framework Deployment Prohibition

Nothing herein authorizes Partner to independently operationalize, sublicense, commercialize, or otherwise deploy the c-ECO framework outside the scope expressly approved by HF in writing.

Critical Distinction — Admission vs. License
Fellowship Admission grants access to the methodological environment only. Fellowship Admission does NOT authorize independent operational, contractual, regulatory, or commercial deployment of the c-ECO framework. Case-Based c-ECO License requires separate institutional authorization, active monitoring platform infrastructure, and case-specific review under applicable CSAM and licensing pathway. Platform infrastructure required for License activation is not yet operationally available as of the Effective Date.

3. Fellowship Participation Structure

3.1 Standard Admission Range

Standard Fellowship Admission may ordinarily range from USD $1,500–2,500, depending on case complexity, institutional exposure, sector sensitivity, participant profile, analytical scope, and operational structure applicable to the relevant Fellowship cycle.

3.2 Institutional Support

Referred candidates may receive institutionally supported, scholarship-supported, invited, or substantially reduced participation conditions at HF’s sole discretion. Such determinations are not subject to Partner influence or appeal.

3.3 Cohort Balancing

HF may establish cohort balancing criteria, participation caps, sectoral allocation limits, or other institutional composition mechanisms without Partner consultation.

3.4 Partner Participation

Partner’s own participation for the first applicable cycle may be institutionally supported by HF, subject to separate FPA execution and HF’s determination of case viability and fit.

3.5 Separate FPA Requirement

Every admitted Fellow — including Partner, if admitted — shall execute the FPA and adhere to its associated Exhibits (MAI, CDGA, LLEP, CSAM). This Agreement does not substitute for, modify, or override any FPA obligation.

4. Partner Obligations

4.1 Candidate Pre-Qualification

Partner shall pre-qualify prospective candidates by assessing basic eligibility criteria: professional trajectory, sectoral relevance, analytical capacity, and alignment with the c-ECO applied architecture. Pre-qualification does not guarantee admission. Partner shall document pre-qualification activities using the template set forth in Exhibit B.

4.2 Mentorship Support

Mentorship, preparatory guidance, and communication assistance are encouraged but not legally required under this Agreement. Any such services provided by Partner to candidates constitute independent professional arrangements exclusively between Partner and the candidate.

4.3 Tracking and Attribution

Partner shall instruct candidates to use tracking code “oliveirawang” in all application materials and communications to enable proper referral attribution.

4.4 Public Materials Approval

All public-facing materials referencing HF, c-ECO, the Fellowship Program, or associated methodologies require prior written approval by HF. HF may require modification or immediate removal of any public materials inconsistent with institutional standards, doctrinal accuracy, or reputational integrity.

4.5 Prohibited Conduct

Partner shall not:

  1. represent himself as an official spokesperson, employee, or agent of HF;
  2. promise, guarantee, or imply admission, certification, licensing authorization, or any specific outcome;
  3. imply that Case-Based c-ECO License activation is currently available or imminent;
  4. reproduce, sublicense, commercialize, or create derivative works from the c-ECO framework without prior written authorization;
  5. misrepresent the scope, authority, or institutional status of the Fellowship Program;
  6. engage in conduct that may damage HF’s reputation, doctrinal integrity, or institutional standing.

4.6 Confidentiality

Partner shall maintain strict confidentiality regarding HF proprietary materials, methodologies, TFP protocols, sectoral workbooks, candidate data, and institutional communications. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

4.7 Independent Contractor Status

Partner acts solely as an independent contractor. No employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship is created by this Agreement. Partner is solely responsible for taxes, regulatory compliance, and professional liability arising from Partner’s activities.

5. HF Obligations

5.1 Guidance Materials

HF shall provide approved Fellowship guidance materials, public descriptions, and institutional positioning documents for Partner’s use in referral activities, subject to §4.4 approval requirements.

5.2 Application Processing

HF shall process referred applications through internal review procedures. HF is not obligated to admit any referred candidate, regardless of Partner pre-qualification.

5.3 Certification

HF may issue certificates upon completion of Fellowship requirements, subject to the FPA, standing categories, and current methodological conditions. Certificate issuance is an institutional determination, not an automatic entitlement.

5.4 Status Updates

HF shall provide monthly referral status updates to Partner, including: number of referrals received, applications under review, admissions determined, and administrative fee status.

5.5 License Infrastructure Notice

HF shall notify Partner if and when platform infrastructure becomes operationally available for License referral activities. Until such notice, Partner shall not engage in License solicitation, negotiation, or representation.

6. Administrative Support Fees

Administrative Support Fee — Not Commission
Fees payable under this Section constitute compensation for documented administrative support activities only. They are not commissions, profit-sharing arrangements, revenue participation mechanisms, or commercial origination incentives.

6.1 Eligibility for Fee

HF may compensate Partner for documented administrative services related to candidate processing, outreach coordination, and Fellowship onboarding support. Fee eligibility requires:

  1. confirmed Fellowship participation payment received by HF;
  2. Fellow in Active standing under the FPA;
  3. submission of applicable administrative documentation by Partner using Exhibit B;
  4. referral attribution verified through tracking code “oliveirawang”.

6.2 Fee Structure

Fee Category Amount Conditions
Fellowship Admission Administrative Support Fee USD $200 per fellow Per approved, enrolled, revenue-generating fellow in Active standing. Initial admission only.
Additional Administrative Services USD $50–90/hour Services beyond standard pre-qualification; prior written approval required; documented by time records; invoice itemization.
Annual Cap USD $2,000 per Cycle Maximum aggregate administrative support fees per Partner per Cycle, inclusive of all fee categories.

6.3 Non-Revenue Admissions — No Fee

No administrative support fee shall apply to:

  1. scholarship-supported admissions;
  2. invited participation;
  3. waived admissions;
  4. fully sponsored admissions;
  5. other non-revenue-generating participation structures;
  6. renewal, reinstatement, or progression review.

Partner may nevertheless refer candidates under non-revenue structures at HF’s sole institutional discretion.

6.4 HF Discretion

HF retains sole discretion regarding:

  1. scholarship allocation and institutional support determinations;
  2. admission classification — revenue-generating or non-revenue;
  3. standing classification and standing transitions;
  4. fee approval, calculation, and payment timing.

6.5 Payment Mechanics

Administrative support fees shall be paid within fifteen (15) days following:

  1. confirmed receipt of the applicable Fellowship participation payment by HF;
  2. verification of Fellow’s Active standing;
  3. submission of complete administrative documentation by Partner using Exhibit B.

6.6 Fee Separation

Administrative support fees payable by HF to Partner are distinct from and independent of:

  1. Fellowship participation fees paid by fellows to HF;
  2. payments made directly by candidates to Partner for independent mentorship or professional services;
  3. future licensing structures, case-based license fees, or outcome-conditioned participation.

6.7 Tax Responsibility

Partner remains solely responsible for all taxes, social contributions, regulatory compliance, and professional liability arising from payments received under this Agreement. HF shall issue applicable documentation as required by law.

7. Safe Mode and TFP Integration

7.1 Automatic Suspension

In the event of TFP band classification Red — near-threshold condition — or Black — irreversibility initiated — HF may suspend, partially or fully, the following without penalty or liability:

  1. referral activities and new admissions;
  2. administrative support fee obligations;
  3. Partner obligations under this Agreement;
  4. Fellowship cycle operations.

7.2 Safe Mode Non-Discretionary

Safe Mode activation reflects certified systemic conditions, not discretionary preference. Partner acknowledges that TFP classification triggers are technical and non-negotiable.

7.3 Resumption

Upon TFP band reclassification to Green or Amber, suspended activities and obligations shall resume in accordance with written notice from HF.

8. Intellectual Property

8.1 Proprietary Framework

The c-ECO framework, including the TFP, SOS, sectoral workbooks, methodological instruments, and associated concepts, remains proprietary intellectual property developed by Jacqueline A. Ennis and held under HF’s doctrinal custody.

8.2 Use Restrictions

Partner may not reproduce, distribute, sublicense, commercialize, create derivative works from, or otherwise exploit the c-ECO framework without prior written authorization from HF.

8.3 Fellowship Use Limitation

Fellowship participation — whether by Partner or referred candidates — does not authorize independent operational, contractual, regulatory, or commercial use of c-ECO outside the scope expressly approved by HF.

9. Data Governance and Confidentiality

9.1 Candidate Data

Partner shall handle all candidate data in accordance with the CDGA and applicable data protection laws. Partner shall not retain, transfer, or process candidate data beyond the scope necessary for referral activities.

9.2 Institutional Communications

All communications regarding HF institutional decisions, methodological updates, TFP protocols, or internal operations are confidential and shall not be disclosed without HF authorization.

9.3 Survival

Confidentiality and data governance obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years, or longer if required by applicable law or institutional policy.

10. Term and Termination

10.1 Initial Term

The initial term of this Agreement is twelve (12) months from the Effective Date.

10.2 Renewal

This Agreement may renew for additional twelve (12)-month terms subject to satisfactory collaboration performance, as determined by HF in its sole discretion. Renewal requires written confirmation by both parties.

10.3 Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice to the other party.

10.4 Immediate Termination by HF

HF may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Partner:

  1. breaches confidentiality or data governance obligations;
  2. engages in unauthorized use of HF intellectual property;
  3. commits reputational misconduct or misrepresentation regarding HF, c-ECO, or License availability;
  4. violates the prohibited conduct provisions of §4.5;
  5. fails to maintain Active standing if admitted as a Fellow.

10.5 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

  1. Partner shall immediately cease all referral activities and public representation of HF;
  2. HF shall process any pending administrative support fees for eligible referrals received prior to termination date;
  3. survival clauses — confidentiality, IP, indemnification, limitation of liability — remain in full force;
  4. Partner shall return or destroy all HF proprietary materials within fifteen (15) days.

11. Assignment and Transfer

Partner may not assign, transfer, or subcontract any rights or obligations under this Agreement without prior written authorization from HF. Any purported assignment without authorization is void.

12. Limitation of Liability

12.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages

HF shall not be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages arising from or related to this Agreement, including lost profits, lost data, or reputational harm.

12.2 Cap on Liability

HF’s aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total administrative support fees paid or payable to Partner in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

12.3 Exceptions

Nothing in this Section excludes or limits liability arising from fraud, willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breaches of confidentiality or IP obligations.

13. Indemnification

Partner shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless HF, its directors, officers, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, arising from:

  1. Partner’s independent acts, representations, or omissions;
  2. Partner’s violations of this Agreement;
  3. Partner’s unauthorized use of HF intellectual property;
  4. any claim by a referred candidate or third party arising from Partner’s referral activities.

14. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

14.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

14.2 Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising from or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in Columbia, Missouri, under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitrator’s decision shall be final and binding. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

14.3 Injunctive Relief

Notwithstanding §14.2, either party may seek injunctive or equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction for breaches of confidentiality, IP, or prohibited conduct provisions.

15. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, including Schedule A and Exhibit B appended hereto, constitutes the complete and exclusive understanding between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof, superseding all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements. No amendment or modification shall be effective unless in writing signed by both parties.

16. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The parties shall negotiate in good faith to replace the invalid provision with a valid provision achieving the nearest equivalent economic and institutional effect.

17. Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email with read receipt or certified mail to the addresses set forth in the parties block, or to such other address as either party may designate by written notice.

Schedule A

Participation & Administrative Support Fee Schedule

Appended to the Institutional Referral & Collaboration Agreement

A.1 Institutionally Supported Participation

Candidates referred by Partner and approved by HF may receive institutionally supported or substantially reduced participation conditions at HF’s sole discretion. Such support does not alter the FPA obligations, methodological requirements, or standing conditions applicable to the admitted Fellow.

A.2 Costs Payable Directly to HF

Applicable institutional participation costs may include:

  1. Fellowship module access fees;
  2. certificate production and shipping costs;
  3. operational administrative costs; and/or
  4. other costs as specified in the FPA or HF’s current fee schedule.

All such costs are payable directly to HF through approved institutional channels. Partner shall not collect, hold, or intermediate any Fellowship participation fees.

A.3 Independent Services by Partner

Partner may independently charge candidates for mentorship, preparatory support, communication or logistical assistance, and related outreach activities. Such arrangements:

  1. are exclusively between Partner and the candidate;
  2. are not administered, supervised, invoiced, or guaranteed by HF;
  3. do not constitute Fellowship participation fees; and
  4. shall not be represented as HF-sponsored, HF-approved, or linked to admission outcomes.

A.4 Administrative Support Fee Structure

Category Amount Conditions & Limitations
Revenue-Generating Admissions USD $200 per fellow Per approved, enrolled fellow generating confirmed payment to HF; Fellow in Active standing; initial admission only; referral attribution verified.
Non-Revenue Admissions USD $0 Scholarship-supported, invited, waived, sponsored, or other non-revenue-generating admissions generate no fee.
Additional Administrative Services USD $50–90/hour Beyond standard pre-qualification; prior written HF approval; documented by time records; invoice itemization required.
Annual Cap USD $2,000 per Cycle Maximum aggregate per Partner per 12-month Cycle, inclusive of all categories.

A.5 Payment Terms

Administrative support fees shall be paid within fifteen (15) days following:

  1. confirmed Fellowship participation payment received by HF;
  2. verification of Fellow’s Active standing under the FPA;
  3. submission of complete administrative documentation by Partner using Exhibit B; and
  4. referral attribution confirmed through tracking code “oliveirawang”.

A.6 License Activation Condition

Case-Based c-ECO License referral activities remain unavailable until:

  1. operational platform activation confirmed by HF in writing;
  2. separate licensing pathway established under CSAM and applicable institutional instruments; and
  3. HF issues written authorization for Partner to engage in License referral activities.

Until such conditions are met, Partner shall not solicit, negotiate, or represent License opportunities.

A.7 Indicative License Ranges — Informational Only

The following ranges are illustrative and subject to case-specific review:

  • Small-scale applications: USD $5,000–10,000
  • Mid-scale applications: USD $10,000–25,000
  • Institutional or high-sensitivity applications: USD $25,000–50,000+

Final license conditions depend on project complexity, data sensitivity, institutional exposure, operational monitoring requirements, and applicable CSAM and licensing pathway. No Partner fee or participation is linked to License activation unless expressly authorized by HF in a separate instrument.

A.8 Modification

HF may update this Schedule A upon thirty (30) days written notice to Partner. Continued referral activities after such notice constitute acceptance of updated terms.
