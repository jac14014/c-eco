IV

Risk Mitigation Architecture

How the three-layer structure neutralizes specific systemic risks:

Capture of Technical Bodies

Risk: Calibration Council or Data Custodians captured by economic operators.

Mitigation: Hasse Foundation supervises functional independence, conflict disclosure, member rotation. Prohibition of "capture" (Model Law, Art. 77).

Data Manipulation

Risk: Data Custodians falsify sensor readings to avoid triggers.

Mitigation: Rigorous certification, mandatory training, chain-of-custody with hashing, immutable ledger. Permanent revocation for fraud (TFP Manual, Sec. 5.4.3).

Tactical Litigation (Forum Shopping)

Risk: Operators seek injunctions in ordinary courts to suspend Safe Mode.

Mitigation: Exclusive technical arbitration (Model Law, Art. 222). Ordinary courts expressly excluded. *Solve et repete* principle: execute first, discuss later.

Methodological Drift

Risk: Silent changes to methodologies alter thresholds without validation.

Mitigation: Calibration Council updates parameters with technical public consultation (Art. 229). Hasse Foundation validates doctrinal coherence. Immutable archives preserve history.

Science vs. Normativity Confusion

Risk: Algorithm determines values; or law ignores technical evidence.

Mitigation: Strict separation: Algorithmic Intelligence = processing; Human Intelligence (Hasse) = normativity. No domain substitutes for another (TFP Manual, Sec. 16).

Institutional Memory Failure

Risk: Loss of knowledge about past decisions, precedents, methodological evolution.

Mitigation: Hasse Foundation maintains Systemic Proof archives, state transition records, intellectual genealogy (Weber-Hasse). Continuity assurance.
