Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

1. Domain Overview

1.1
Definition & Scope

Definition: The institutions, instruments, observatories, datasets, validation protocols, and knowledge systems that detect, verify, preserve, and communicate systemic evidence across environmental, technological, health, security, financial, and governance domains.

Scope: Earth observation, scientific monitoring networks, metrology institutes, public statistics, research infrastructures, verification bodies, knowledge repositories, standards organizations, sensor networks, audit trails, and the institutional arrangements that keep evidence usable under stress.

1.2
Systemic Relevance

SOVK is the evidentiary nervous system of c-ECO. Without trusted observation and verification, P cannot be located, ΔV cannot be interpreted, σ cannot be bounded, and Lr cannot be assessed in time for pre-threshold action.

2. Historical Evolution

This SIL treats the domain as an evolving systemic infrastructure rather than a static sector. Historical analysis tracks how technical complexity, interdependence, and governance dependence have increased the cost of late intervention.
