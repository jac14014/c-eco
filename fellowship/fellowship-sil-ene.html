Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

1. Domain Overview

1.1
Definition & Scope

Definition: The generation, transmission, distribution, storage, and balancing of electrical power and primary energy carriers (gas, oil products, coal, hydrogen) that energize every other sector of the economy.

Scope: Generation (thermal, nuclear, hydro, wind, solar, geothermal); high-voltage transmission and HVDC interconnectors; distribution networks and substations; system operation and balancing (TSO/ISO); fuel supply chains (LNG terminals, pipelines, strategic reserves); grid-scale and distributed storage; and the SCADA/EMS control layer that coordinates it all.

1.2
Systemic Relevance

Energy is the master enabling domain: a sustained loss of electricity collapses Digital infrastructure within hours, halts Water pumping and treatment, diverts Healthcare to backup power, and freezes Financial settlement. No other domain can substitute for it, and most have only hours-to-days of autonomous backup.

The grid is a real-time machine: supply must equal demand every second, frequency must hold within a fraction of a hertz, and a single uncontrolled disturbance can propagate across a continent in seconds (2003 Northeast and Italy; 2025 Iberia). This combination of universality and second-by-second fragility makes Energy the highest-leverage sector in the c-ECO model.

1.3
Key Infrastructures
HV transmission grid
400/500/765 kV backbone + HVDC interconnectors; the system whose collapse cascades fastest
HV transformers (500 kV+)
Long-lead, hard-to-replace assets (<200 spares globally); GIC- and overload-vulnerable

ENTSO-E members, NERC/ISOs (PJM, ERCOT, CAISO) — real-time balancing authorities
Generation fleet
Nuclear, gas, hydro, wind, solar; inertia and dispatchability profile drives stability
Fuel supply
LNG terminals, gas pipelines, strategic petroleum/coal reserves; days-of-supply buffer
Storage & black start
Pumped hydro, grid batteries (BESS), and certified black-start units for restoration

2. Historical Evolution (ΔV Calibration)

Milestones anchoring the velocity (ΔV) calibration — each shows how fast an energy disturbance can become systemic:

  • 1859 Carrington Event — geomagnetic storm benchmark for GIC threat to transformers.
  • 1965 / 1977 Northeast blackouts — early demonstrations of cascade and load-restoration challenges.
  • 1989 Quebec (GIC) — solar storm collapsed Hydro-Québec in 90 seconds; 6M without power.
  • 2003 Northeast (55M) & Italy (56M) — tree contact + control blind spots cascaded continent-scale.
  • 2011 Fukushima — natural hazard → nuclear + grid compound failure.
  • 2015–2016 Ukraine — first confirmed cyber-induced blackouts (BlackEnergy, Industroyer).
  • 2021 Texas/ERCOT (Uri) — cold snap + gas freeze-off; 4.5M out, 246 deaths.
  • 2025 Iberia — cross-domain Energy → Digital cascade; 10h blackout (ENTSO-E final report, Mar 2026).

The full 60-event historical library is in Part IX, Section 34.

3. Global Institutional Landscape

Key institutions with TFP relevance: IEA (markets, security of supply); IRENA (renewables); ENTSO-E (EU TSO coordination, frequency); NERC (North American reliability standards, CIP); FERC (US regulation); CIGRE and IEEE PES (technical standards); IAEA (nuclear safety); NOAA SWPC and ESA (space weather / GIC); WMO (meteorology); national TSOs/ISOs (National Grid ESO, RTE, PJM, ERCOT, CAISO, Hydro-Québec, POSOCO).

Glossary, Sources & Classification

Glossary of TFP & Domain Terms

P — Position
Proximity to a failure threshold; higher P = closer to systemic breach.
ΔV — Velocity
Rate of change of system state toward (or away from) breach.
σ — Uncertainty
Confidence interval / governance gaps around an estimate.
Lr — Reversibility Liquidity
Capacity to reverse/recover from failure (0 = irreversible, 1 = fully reversible).
SPS
Systemic Proximity Score — composite breach-proximity indicator driving response levels.
TRS / RLS
Systemic Trend and Restoration/Liquidity sub-scores used in cross-domain calibration.
GIC
Geomagnetically Induced Currents — solar-storm currents that saturate and damage HV transformers.
RoCoF
Rate of Change of Frequency — key low-inertia stability metric.
Inertia
Stored rotational energy (GW·s) that slows frequency change after a disturbance.
TSO / ISO / RTO
Transmission System Operator / Independent System Operator / Regional Transmission Org — real-time balancing authorities.
Black start
Restarting the grid from a fully de-energized state without outside power.
N-1 security
The system can lose any single element without violating limits.
IBR
Inverter-Based Resources (wind, solar, batteries) — non-synchronous generation.
Dunkelflaute
Prolonged period of low wind and low solar output.
PMU
Phasor Measurement Unit — GPS-timed wide-area grid sensor.
