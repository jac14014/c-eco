Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

1. Domain Overview

1.1
Definition & Scope

Definition: The extraction, processing, and beneficiation of solid mineral resources from Earth's crust—including metallic ores (iron, copper, lithium, cobalt, nickel, rare earth elements), non-metallic minerals (phosphate, potash, limestone), and fossil fuels (coal, uranium)—together with the tailings storage, waste management, and rehabilitation infrastructure that constitute the sector's operational footprint.

Scope: Surface mining (open-pit, strip, mountaintop removal), underground mining (shaft, drift, longwall), solution mining (in-situ leaching, brine extraction), deep-sea mining (polymetallic nodules, seafloor massive sulfides), and artisanal/small-scale mining (ASM). Includes upstream exploration, midstream processing (crushing, milling, flotation, smelting, refining), and downstream tailings management, closure, and post-closure monitoring.

1.2
Systemic Relevance

The Mining & Mineral Extraction sector is the material foundation of the global economy. Without mineral inputs, Energy Systems collapse (no copper for grids, no lithium for batteries), Digital Infrastructure halts (no rare earths for semiconductors, no cobalt for magnets), Healthcare fails (no lithium for psychiatric medication, no platinum for chemotherapy), and Defense becomes inoperable (no uranium for nuclear deterrence, no rare earths for guidance systems).

Failure in this domain causes cascading disruption across all 18 other c-ECO sectors. A single tailings dam failure can contaminate water supplies for millions (Water & Sanitation), destroy agricultural land (Agribusiness), trigger sovereign debt crises (Financial Systems), and generate cross-border migration (Governance).

1.3
Key Infrastructures
Tailings Storage Facilities (TSFs)
~18,000 active TSFs globally; 3,500 classified as "high consequence" (ICMM, 2024). Single-point failure can release >40M m³ of toxic slurry (Mariana: 43.7M m³; Brumadinho: 12M m³).
Mine Waste Dumps
~200B tonnes of mine waste stored globally. Acid mine drainage (AMD) from sulfide waste can persist for centuries, contaminating watersheds permanently.
Processing Plants
Crushing, grinding, flotation, leaching, SX-EW, smelting, refining. Concentration risk: 70% of cobalt refining in China; 60% of lithium processing in China.
Haul Roads & Logistics
Single-haul road dependency common in remote operations. Road failure isolates mine for weeks (Lr degradation).
Power & Water Infrastructure
Mines consume 2-5% of global electricity; water consumption 1-2% of global freshwater withdrawals. Grid failure → mine flooding; water failure → processing halt.
Port & Rail Export Corridors
Iron ore: 1.6B tonnes/year via dedicated rail-port systems (Carajás-Itaqui, Pilbara-Port Hedland). Single-corridor failure = global steel supply shock.

Glossary

AMD (Acid Mine Drainage): The outflow of acidic water from metal mines or coal mines, formed by the reaction of water with sulfide minerals (pyrite) exposed to air and water.

ASM (Artisanal and Small-Scale Mining): Mining activities carried out by individuals, families, or groups using minimal machinery and technology, often informal and unregulated.

CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism): EU policy imposing carbon costs on imports of carbon-intensive goods (steel, aluminum, cement, fertilizers, electricity).

DLE (Direct Lithium Extraction): Emerging technology extracting lithium from brine without evaporation ponds, using ion exchange, adsorption, or electrochemical methods.

FPIC (Free, Prior, and Informed Consent): Principle that indigenous peoples must give consent before projects affecting their lands and resources proceed.

GTR (Global Tailings Review): Independent review established after Brumadinho to develop international standard for tailings facility management.

HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index): Measure of market concentration; higher values indicate greater concentration and reduced competition.

InSAR (Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar): Satellite-based radar technique measuring ground deformation with millimeter precision; used for TSF monitoring.

ISA (International Seabed Authority): UN body regulating mineral-related activities in the international seabed area (the "Area" beyond national jurisdiction).

Lr (Reversibility Liquidity): TFP variable measuring the capacity to recover from systemic disruption; 0 = irreversible, 1 = fully reversible.

LTIFR (Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate): Standard safety metric measuring lost-time injuries per million hours worked.

P (Position): TFP variable measuring proximity to failure threshold; higher values indicate closer to systemic breach.

SPS (Systemic Proximity Score): Composite score (1-5) indicating how close a system is to systemic failure; L1 = collapse, L5 = normal.

STP (Submarine Tailings Placement): Disposal of mine tailings into the ocean via pipeline; practiced in PNG, Indonesia, Norway.

σ (Uncertainty): TFP variable measuring confidence in risk assessment and governance gaps; higher values indicate greater uncertainty.

ΔV (Velocity): TFP variable measuring rate of change toward or away from failure; higher values indicate faster approach to threshold.

TSF (Tailings Storage Facility): Structure for storing mine waste (tailings) after ore processing; includes dams, impoundments, in-pit deposits.

TFP (Total Friction Profile): c-ECO framework combining P, ΔV, σ, Lr, SPS, TRS, and RLS for systemic risk assessment.
