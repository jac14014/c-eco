Module 03 of 06 — Agribusiness & Intensive Land Use

Threshold Logic: Agribusiness Pre-Threshold Signals and Early Warning

3 Hours Preparation 90-Minute Simulation Early Warning Design

Learning Objectives

  • Identify weak signals and compound signals in agribusiness and intensive land-use.
  • Differentiate ordinary risk indicators from pre-threshold governance triggers.
  • Design a sector-specific early warning ladder for Agribusiness.
  • Translate signal deterioration into disciplined institutional escalation.

Module Function

Module 3 teaches Fellows to recognize the moment when a case stops being merely risky and begins to move toward threshold incompatibility. In the Agribusiness & Intensive Land Use track, early warning is not a dashboard exercise. It is the institutional capacity to convert signal deterioration into timely, lawful, and reversible governance action.

Pre-Threshold Logic

A signal becomes legally and institutionally meaningful when it indicates movement toward a boundary where ordinary continuity assumptions no longer hold. The purpose of early warning is not prediction for its own sake; it is the preservation of intervention capacity.

Signal Classes

Early Warning Ladder

Watch

Signals are visible but not yet trajectory-defining. Fellows document evidence quality and monitoring frequency.

Concern

Signals begin to align across data sources or actors. Fellows test alternative explanations and update σ.

Intervention

Trajectory and reversibility exposure justify institutional action. Fellows draft options for CSAM escalation.

Safe Mode

Red or Black band conditions require mandatory conduct under the applicable Fellowship and institutional protocols.

Simulation: The Delayed Signal

Fellows receive a staged packet based on a vertically integrated soy, cattle, or commodity-processing operation expanding across water-stressed or biodiversity-sensitive territory. Each round introduces new evidence, including soil organic matter loss and compaction, groundwater drawdown and irrigation stress, nutrient runoff, eutrophication, and pesticide persistence. The exercise tests whether Fellows can identify the point at which continued observation becomes a failure of governance.

Simulation Deliverables
1
Signal Register

Classify each signal as physical, institutional, contractual, or systemic.

2
Trigger Memo

Identify the earliest justified pre-threshold intervention point and explain the cost of waiting.

3
Institutional Escalation

Draft the escalation pathway for producers and landholders, commodity traders and processors, banks, insurers, and offtakers, environmental agencies and land registries.
