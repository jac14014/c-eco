Comprehensive Examination Structure

c-ECO Mining Practitioner Certification Examination
Part I: Written Examination — 2.5 Hours

Format: Closed-book, individual assessment.

Components:

  • Section A — Conceptual: Short-answer questions on pre-threshold mining governance, reversibility, systemic incompatibility, Safe Mode, and Restoration First.
  • Section B — Quantitative: Problems requiring P, ΔV, σ, Lr calculations from mining data such as pore pressure, displacement, seepage, groundwater drawdown, and restoration capital.
  • Section C — Applied: Mining case analysis requiring integrated use of TFP classification, temporal validity, and contractual governance.
Part II: Oral Examination — 1 Hour

Format: Socratic dialogue with two examiners.

Components:

  • Case Defense — 30 min: Present and defend your Module 5 Mining Field Project.
  • Professional Scenario — 20 min: Respond to a real-time mining scenario involving conflicting technical, legal, financial, and community pressures.
  • Reflection — 10 min: Articulate how c-ECO changes your professional judgment in mining governance.

Sample Examination Questions

Section A — Conceptual Example

Question: A mine remains formally permitted and no collapse has occurred. However, pore pressure, seepage variance, slope displacement, and downstream turbidity have all deteriorated for three monitoring cycles. Explain why c-ECO treats this as a legal-operational problem before accident occurrence. Address the distinction between incident response and pre-threshold governance.

Section B — Quantitative Example

Question: A tailings facility reports current pore pressure of 160 kPa. The critical boundary is 180 kPa. The reference safe state is 90 kPa. Measurement uncertainty is ±10%. Displacement increased from 2 mm/month to 8 mm/month over 24 months. Immediately mobilizable stabilization capital is $18M. Projected technical stabilization cost is $45M. Calculate: (a) P with asymmetric uncertainty, (b) ΔV, (c) Lr, and (d) likely prudential band.

Section C — Applied Example

Question: You are advising a board that wants to continue production at a high-value mine while commissioning additional studies. Community groups allege downstream contamination. The operator argues that all permits are current and closure bonds are in place. Draft a two-page memorandum explaining whether continuation remains c-ECO-compatible, what data must be verified, and which contractual or operational measures should be triggered immediately.

  • Re-read Mining Modules 1–5 and consolidate feedback.
  • Review TFP variables, Safe Mode, Restoration First, and temporal validity.
  • Create a one-page formula and trigger summary for personal preparation.

Phase 2 — Practice:

  • Complete timed quantitative problems using mining data.
  • Conduct mock oral examination with a peer or mentor.
  • Prepare answers to the 10 hardest questions about your Module 5 mining project.

Phase 3 — Integration:

  • Synthesize your professional theory of mining systemic governance.
  • Prepare a 2-minute, 5-minute, and 10-minute explanation of your case.
  • Practice translating technical risk into board-level and community-facing language.
