Field Project: The c-ECO Forests Stability Assessment

Your Role: c-ECO Fellow assigned to conduct a supervised Systemic Stability Assessment for a forest concession, carbon project, biodiversity asset, restoration portfolio, or natural-capital transaction exposed to integrity, permanence, and community-risk failure. This is a Fellowship analytical exercise. It does not authorize independent real-world deployment of c-ECO.

CSAM Structure

SectionRequired ContentForests Focus
Case BoundaryDefine geography, assets, activities, actors, and time horizon.forest integrity, carbon stocks, biodiversity, ecosystem services, restoration, permanence, and natural-asset governance
Signal ArchitectureIdentify measurable indicators and evidence sources.deforestation and degradation acceleration; carbon stock instability; biodiversity indicator decline; fire, drought, and pest exposure
Threshold MapIdentify relevant SOS boundaries and failure conditions.forest integrity and tipping-point boundaries; carbon permanence limits; biodiversity loss thresholds; restoration viability limits
Actor MapAllocate observation, response, and coordination responsibilities.project developers and landholders; Indigenous and local communities; carbon buyers and registries; conservation and forestry agencies; investors, insurers, and auditors
Instrument OptionsTranslate analysis into possible contractual or institutional clauses.permanence and reversal clauses; biodiversity integrity covenants; community benefit and consent protocols; restoration-first triggers

Deliverables

Deliverable 1 — Technical Assessment Report (40%)

A 2,000-word report explaining P, ΔV, σ, Lr, signal quality, and threshold exposure.

Deliverable 2 — CSAM Draft (35%)

A structured Case-Specific Analytical Mandate defining scope, evidence, actors, intervention logic, and methodological limitations.

Deliverable 3 — Governance Translation Memo (25%)

A concise memo translating the assessment into possible institutional or contractual response pathways without claiming operational authority.

Seven-Day Workplan

  • Days 1–2: Confirm case boundary and actor map.
  • Days 3–4: Build signal architecture and threshold map.
  • Days 5–6: Draft CSAM and governance translation memo.
  • Day 7: Peer review, revision, and submission through cohort coordination.
