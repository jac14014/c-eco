Module Function

Module 2 converts the common doctrine of Module 1 into sector-specific measurement logic. In the Logistics & Transportation track, the Threshold Function Protocol is not treated as a generic scoring device. It is a disciplined method for locating a concrete case inside its systemic stability space, measuring movement toward or away from threshold conditions, weighting uncertainty, and assessing remaining reversibility.

Sector Calibration Principle

The same TFP variables apply across all Fellowship tracks, but their empirical content changes by domain. For Logistics, calibration begins by asking which system is being protected, which thresholds matter, which signals can be observed, and which decisions remain reversible.

Core Variables

P
Position

The current state of an activity, asset, environment, or system within its systemic stability space, measured relative to relevant thresholds, Safe Operating Space boundaries, and potential failure conditions.

Logistics application: position is assessed through critical node continuity limits, corridor redundancy boundaries, emissions and fuel-transition compatibility limits, and the proximity of the case to operational or ecological failure.

ΔV
Velocity

The rate and direction of systemic change toward or away from a threshold. A slow deterioration may be manageable; a rapid deterioration may require immediate Safe Mode escalation.

Logistics application: Fellows examine signals such as port congestion and dwell-time escalation, corridor closure frequency, fleet fuel transition mismatch.

σ
Uncertainty

The degree of uncertainty associated with evidence, measurement quality, data continuity, model assumptions, and institutional visibility. In c-ECO, uncertainty does not excuse delay when reversibility is shrinking.

Logistics application: uncertainty is mapped across observation gaps, institutional blind spots, and contested evidence.

Lr
Reversibility Liquidity

The remaining capacity of the system to stabilize, recover, redesign, or reconfigure before damage becomes irreversible or response windows close.

Logistics application: Lr is evaluated through technical redundancy, governance capacity, restoration options, financing flexibility, and time-sensitive intervention pathways.

2
