PART I — DOMAIN INTELLIGENCE PROFILE
1. Domain Overview
1.1 — Definition & Scope

Definition: The defense, strategic security, civil protection, critical infrastructure protection, continuity-of-government, and strategic logistics systems that preserve organized collective security under systemic environmental, technological, geopolitical, and infrastructure stress.

Scope: Defense institutions, strategic bases, command and control, dual-use logistics, critical supply chains, cyber and space security, continuity planning, emergency mobilization, strategic reserves, and institutional mechanisms for lawful security response.

1.2 — Systemic Relevance

DSSS becomes systemic when security capacity depends on the same energy, water, digital, space, financial, health, and industrial systems exposed to threshold deterioration. The domain is not exempt from c-ECO; it requires stricter boundary discipline because security necessity can mask physical incompatibility.

2. Historical Evolution
This SIL treats the domain as an evolving systemic infrastructure rather than a static sector. Historical analysis tracks how technical complexity, interdependence, and governance dependence have increased the cost of late intervention.
Analytical Lens

Historical cases are evaluated not for military outcomes but for their governance implications: what threshold signals were available, which institutions held response authority, and whether reversibility was preserved or foreclosed by the decisions made.
