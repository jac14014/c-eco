Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

1. Domain Overview

1.1
Definition & Scope

Definition: The institutions, markets, and infrastructures that intermediate capital, process payments, and clear and settle obligations — banking, payments, securities and derivatives markets, insurance, central banking, and the financial market infrastructures (FMIs) that underpin them.

Scope: Commercial & investment banking; payment systems (RTGS, retail, cards); clearing (CCPs) and settlement (CSDs); securities exchanges and OTC markets; money markets and repo; insurance & reinsurance; central banks and monetary operations; messaging (SWIFT); and the growing crypto/stablecoin and digital-asset layer.

1.2
Systemic Relevance

Finance is the circulatory system of the economy: it allocates capital, prices risk, and settles the obligations every other sector relies on. It runs on confidence and liquidity — both of which can evaporate in hours, turning a solvent institution insolvent through a run.

The domain is densely interconnected (interbank, CCP, and collateral linkages) and now sits atop the Digital substrate (payments, timing, cloud). A failure can propagate through contagion, fire sales, and margin spirals faster than any other domain — and can feed back into Governance via sovereign-bank "doom loops."

2. Historical Evolution (ΔV Calibration)

Milestones anchoring the velocity (ΔV) calibration — finance produces some of the fastest systemic moves on record:

  • 1929 Wall Street Crash — benchmark for market collapse + depression cascade.
  • 1987 Black Monday — Dow −22.6% in a day; program-trading velocity.
  • 1998 LTCM — leverage + correlation blow-up; Fed-orchestrated wind-down.
  • 2008 GFC (Lehman) — interconnectedness + shadow banking; global contagion.
  • 2010 Flash Crash — ~$1T intraday evaporation in minutes (HFT/liquidity).
  • 2012 Knight Capital — $440M lost in 45 minutes from a software bug.
  • 2020 COVID "dash for cash" — even Treasuries seized; central-bank backstops.
  • 2022 UK LDI / 2023 SVB & Credit Suisse — rates shock + digital bank-run velocity.

The full 60-event historical library is in Part IX, Section 34.

3. Global Institutional Landscape

Key institutions with TFP relevance: BIS (central-bank cooperation, research); FSB (systemic-risk coordination); IMF (surveillance, crisis lending); Basel Committee/BCBS (bank capital & liquidity); IOSCO (securities); CPMI (payments & FMIs, PFMI); central banks (Fed, ECB, BoE, PBoC, BoJ); macroprudential bodies (FSOC, ESRB); market regulators (SEC, CFTC, ESMA, FCA); FMIs (DTCC, CLS, SWIFT, LCH, CME).

Glossary, Sources & Classification

Glossary of TFP & Domain Terms

P — Position
Proximity to a failure threshold; higher P = closer to systemic breach.
ΔV — Velocity
Rate of change of system state toward (or away from) breach.
σ — Uncertainty
Confidence interval / governance gaps around an estimate.
Lr — Reversibility Liquidity
Capacity to reverse/recover from failure (0 = irreversible, 1 = fully reversible).
SPS
Systemic Proximity Score — composite breach-proximity indicator driving response levels.
TRS / RLS
Systemic Trend and Restoration/Liquidity sub-scores used in cross-domain calibration.
FMI
Financial Market Infrastructure — payment systems, CCPs, CSDs, trade repositories.
CCP
Central Counterparty — clears trades and mutualizes default risk.
RTGS
Real-Time Gross Settlement — large-value payment system (Fedwire, TARGET2).
LOLR
Lender of Last Resort — central-bank emergency liquidity.
LCR / NSFR
Liquidity Coverage Ratio / Net Stable Funding Ratio (Basel liquidity rules).
TLAC / MREL
Loss-absorbing capacity for orderly resolution of large banks.
Bail-in
Imposing losses on shareholders/creditors instead of taxpayers.
NBFI
Non-Bank Financial Intermediation ("shadow banking").
Doom loop
Mutual fragility between banks and their sovereign.
