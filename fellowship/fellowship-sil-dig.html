Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

1. Domain Overview

1.1
Definition & Scope

Definition: Physical and virtual assets—fiber optic cables, data centers, satellite constellations, radio frequency spectrum, cloud platforms—that process, store, and transmit digital information.

Scope: Terrestrial (fiber, 5G/6G), submarine (cable landing stations), orbital (LEO, MEO, GEO), plus the power grids and cooling systems on which all of these depend. Spans the full chain from intercontinental backbone to last-mile access and the timing/positioning layer (GNSS) that synchronizes it.

1.2
Systemic Relevance

Digital infrastructure is the central nervous system for Finance, Energy, and Health. Failure causes cascading collapses across dependent sectors: payment settlement, grid control, hospital operations, and logistics all assume continuous connectivity and sub-microsecond timing.

Because 99% of intercontinental traffic transits a small number of submarine cables, and because cloud compute is concentrated in a handful of hyperscale providers, the domain combines extreme criticality with extreme concentration—a structural fragility that the TFP model is designed to quantify.

1.3
Key Infrastructures
Submarine cables
~99% of intercontinental traffic; concentrated chokepoints (Luzon Strait, Suez, Panama)

Starlink, OneWeb, Kuiper — emerging redundancy and emerging debris risk
IXPs
Internet Exchange Points — peering fabric of the global routing system

2. Historical Evolution (ΔV Calibration)

Selected milestones anchor the velocity (ΔV) calibration—each marks a step-change in how fast the domain can move toward systemic breach:

  • 1859 Carrington Event — telegraphs fail globally; benchmark SPS threshold for geomagnetically induced currents (GIC).
  • 1989 Quebec blackout — GIC, 6M without power.
  • 2006 Hengchun earthquake — 8 cables severed; concentration risk.
  • 2012 Carrington-class CME near miss — missed Earth by 9 days (σ).
  • 2021 Log4Shell — 90% of Java applications vulnerable.
  • 2022 Tonga eruption — 5-week national blackout, Lr = 0.
  • 2025 Spain/Portugal blackout — cross-domain Energy → Digital cascade, 60–90% traffic drop.

The full 60-event historical library is provided in Part IX, Section 34.

Glossary, Sources & Classification

Glossary of TFP & Domain Terms

P — Position
Proximity to a failure threshold; higher P means closer to systemic breach.
ΔV — Velocity
Rate of change of system state toward (or away from) breach.
σ — Uncertainty
Confidence interval / governance gaps around an estimate.
Lr — Reversibility Liquidity
Available capacity to reverse or recover from a failure (0 = irreversible, 1 = fully reversible).
SPS
Systemic Proximity Score — composite breach-proximity indicator driving response levels.
TRS / RLS
Systemic Trend and Restoration/Liquidity sub-scores used in cross-domain calibration.
GIC
Geomagnetically Induced Currents — solar-storm currents that damage HV transformers.
GNSS
Global Navigation Satellite System (GPS, Galileo, GLONASS, BeiDou) — positioning and timing.
BGP
Border Gateway Protocol — the Internet's inter-domain routing protocol.
IXP
Internet Exchange Point — physical peering fabric between networks.
PUE
Power Usage Effectiveness — data center energy-efficiency ratio.
RTO
Recovery Time Objective — target duration to restore a system after failure.
Kessler Syndrome
Self-sustaining orbital debris collision cascade that can render LEO unusable.
