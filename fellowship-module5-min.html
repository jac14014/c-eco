Field Project: The c-ECO Mining Stability Assessment

Mining Engagement Simulation

Your Role: c-ECO Fellow assigned to conduct a Systemic Stability Assessment for a mining asset, mining company, concession, tailings facility, or restoration obligation.

You may select either:

  • Option A: Real mining case with appropriate confidentiality protections.
  • Option B: Realistic composite case based on public disclosures, technical reports, sustainability reports, or litigation materials.
  • Option C: Assigned case study provided by the instructor.

Project Scope: Conduct a comprehensive c-ECO mining analysis including:

  1. System boundary identification: mine, tailings facility, aquifer, waste pile, slope, river basin, or downstream receptor zone.
  2. Safe Operating Space boundary identification for the selected mining subsystem.
  3. Current Position assessment with uncertainty quantification.
  4. Trajectory analysis using historical monitoring or reported data.
  5. Reversibility Liquidity evaluation of restoration, stabilization, and emergency-response capacity.
  6. Band classification: Green, Amber, Red/Safe Mode, or Black/Restoration First.
  7. Contractual and governance recommendations for pre-threshold intervention.
  8. Implementation roadmap for mining-specific c-ECO integration.

Required Deliverables

Deliverable 1: Mining Technical Assessment Report — 40%

Format: 15–20 page professional report.

Contents:

  • Executive Summary: key findings and recommendations for board or institutional review.
  • Mining System Boundary: asset, concession, tailings facility, aquifer, waste pile, slope, or receptor zone.
  • Material Risk Context: geotechnical, hydrological, toxicological, social, and restoration dimensions.
  • Data Audit: sources, coverage, gaps, uncertainty, and monitoring frequency.
  • TFP Variable Calculations: P, ΔV, σ, and Lr with full methodological transparency.
  • Prudential Classification: current state, projected trajectory, and escalation conditions.
  • Safe Mode / Restoration First Analysis: whether and when intervention becomes mandatory.
  • Contractual and Governance Recommendations: clauses, triggers, reserves, reporting, and accountability.
  • Implementation Roadmap: immediate, 90-day, 12-month, and 36-month milestones.
Deliverable 2: Client / Board Presentation — 30%

Format: 20-minute presentation + 10-minute Q&A.

Audience: Simulated mining board, regulator, lender, insurer, or affected stakeholder committee.

Requirements:

  • Translate technical instability into legal-operational consequences.
  • Explain why absence of collapse is not evidence of systemic safety.
  • Clarify what triggers Amber, Safe Mode, or Restoration First.
  • Anticipate skeptical questions from operators, investors, regulators, and communities.
  • Use clear visuals: maximum 12 slides, minimal jargon.
Deliverable 3: Mining Implementation Toolkit — 20%

Format: Operational documents for mining governance use.

Contents:

  • Monitoring Protocol: specific indicators, sources, frequency, and validation rules.
  • Threshold Dashboard: P, ΔV, σ, Lr indicators and band classification logic.
  • Escalation Procedure: who must act when Amber, Red, or Black conditions are detected.
  • Safe Mode Checklist: operational restrictions, stabilization actions, notification duties.
  • Contractual Template Language: monitoring covenants, suspension rights, restoration-priority clauses, and guarantee conversion.
Deliverable 4: Reflection Memo — 10%

Format: 3-page personal reflection.

Topics: What made this case difficult? Where did conventional mining governance arrive too late? How did c-ECO change your interpretation of operational continuity, permit validity, and restoration responsibility?

Assessment Rubric

Criterion Excellent Good Satisfactory Needs Work
Technical Accuracy Precise mining logic, correct TFP calculations, strong uncertainty treatment. Mostly correct methodology with minor gaps. Basic calculations, limited sensitivity analysis. Significant technical errors or weak mining fit.
Systemic Interpretation Clear distinction between accident, failure pattern, and pre-threshold trajectory. Good systemic analysis with some generic elements. Identifies risk but does not fully connect trajectory to reversibility. Treats the case as ordinary compliance or incident response.
Contractual Translation Specific Safe Mode, IEX, monitoring, and restoration clauses proposed. Good contractual recommendations, some missing operational detail. General legal recommendations with limited enforceability structure. No clear legal-operational translation.
Actionability Prioritized, resourced, time-bound recommendations with clear ownership. Mostly specific and implementable. Some useful recommendations, but generic or incomplete. Vague or unrealistic recommendations.
Professional Communication Board-ready, clear, persuasive, and suitable for controlled institutional use. Professional with minor polish needed. Understandable but not client-ready. Unclear, overly technical, or poorly structured.
