Instrument Architecture
  • This Agreement governs institutional collaboration and regional coordination support for the c-ECO Fellowship Program.
  • It does not create a franchise, educational licensing arrangement, joint venture, partnership, or technology transfer mechanism.
  • Fellowship participation by individual candidates is governed by the separate Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA) and associated Exhibits.
  • Data governance for admitted Fellows is governed by the Confidentiality & Data Governance Agreement (CDGA).
  • Academic credit, curricular equivalency, or continuing education recognition shall require a separate written agreement.
  • All restricted disclosures remain subject to the NDA, TFP Safe Mode, and role-based access controls.

This Institutional Collaboration and Fellowship Administration Support Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") between the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation ("Foundation"), acting through its c-ECO Framework and c-ECO Fellowship Program, and [Regional Institutional Collaborator] ("Collaborator").

This Agreement establishes the terms under which the Collaborator shall function as a regional institutional collaborator and supporting institutional node for the c-ECO Fellowship Program, providing regional coordination support, candidate referral assistance, institutional outreach, and operational facilitation within the Designated Territory defined herein.

Important: The Collaborator does not operate the c-ECO Fellowship Program. The Collaborator provides institutional support, regional coordination, and operational facilitation under the standards, authorization, and program governance of the Foundation. The fellow's primary contractual and methodological relationship is with the Foundation, not with the Collaborator.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this collaboration is to foster applied institutional collaboration in the field of contractual equity and ecological co-responsibility, with particular emphasis on designated Living Lab territories and regional coordination nodes, including, where applicable, the Amazon biome and associated territories.

Specifically, this Agreement enables:

  • Regional coordination support for the identification, referral, and local facilitation of fellowship candidates;
  • Institutional outreach to strengthen networks advancing systemic environmental governance, pre-damage risk analysis, and contractual frameworks for ecological co-responsibility;
  • Operational facilitation for fellowship activities conducted within the Designated Territory;
  • Support for the methodological integrity of the c-ECO framework without conferring upon the Collaborator any status as franchisor, sublicensor, independent certifying authority, or educational provider.
Clarification: This Agreement does not create a franchise relationship, educational licensing arrangement, technology transfer mechanism, or institutional merger. The Collaborator's role is strictly limited to institutional coordination, regional outreach, and operational support as defined herein.

2. Nature of Institutional Collaboration

The relationship between the Foundation and the Collaborator is defined as an applied institutional collaboration within a distributed research-extension network. This relationship is characterized by the following attributes:

2.1 No Educational Provider Representation

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as authorizing the Collaborator to act as an educational provider, accredited institution, degree-granting body, diploma-issuing entity, or independent operator of the c-ECO Fellowship Program. The Collaborator shall not:

  • Offer, market, or represent itself as providing c-ECO-certified education, training, or degree programs independently of the Foundation;
  • Issue credentials, certificates, diplomas, or academic transcripts in the name of c-ECO or the Foundation;
  • Represent that participation in the Collaborator's own programs (separate from this fellowship) confers c-ECO recognition, endorsement, or certification;
  • Apply for accreditation, licensing, or governmental authorization as an educational institution based on this Agreement or c-ECO affiliation.

2.2 No Fee Collection

The Collaborator shall not collect application fees, tuition, enrollment fees, certification fees, access fees, processing fees, administrative fees, or any similar payment from candidates or fellows in connection with the Fellowship Program, unless expressly authorized in a separate written agreement approved by the Foundation.

This prohibition includes, without limitation:

  • Application or referral processing fees;
  • Enrollment or registration fees;
  • Tuition or instructional fees;
  • Certification or credentialing fees;
  • Access fees for materials, platforms, or events;
  • Administrative or service fees of any nature.

2.3 Designated Territory

The Collaborator's coordination support activities shall be limited to the designated territory identified as [City, State/Province, Country] and, where applicable, associated Living Lab territories or regional coordination nodes identified by the Foundation in writing.

Nothing herein shall be construed as granting territorial exclusivity unless expressly stated in a separate written addendum. The Collaborator may be granted a preferential right to discuss future expansion opportunities within the designated territory, subject to performance, strategic alignment, and separate written agreement.

The Collaborator shall not represent itself as authorized to operate c-ECO Fellowship activities outside the Designated Territory without express written authorization from the Foundation.

2.4 Independent Institutional Status

Each Party retains full independent institutional status. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating a joint venture, partnership, merger, acquisition, or any other form of corporate integration. The Collaborator remains an autonomous legal entity, governed by its own charter, bylaws, and applicable domestic law.

No Party shall represent the collaboration as creating an institutional branch, chapter, office, licensee, franchise, or territorial mandate.

2.5 Non-Exclusive Arrangement

This collaboration is non-exclusive. The Foundation reserves the right to establish similar institutional collaborations with other organizations, institutions, or entities, whether within the same geographic region or elsewhere. The Collaborator may engage in other activities and partnerships, provided such activities do not conflict with the terms of this Agreement.

2.6 No Authority to Bind

The Collaborator shall not have authority to enter into contracts, make representations, incur obligations, or otherwise bind the Foundation, except as expressly authorized in writing by the Foundation's designated executive authority. The Collaborator may not represent itself as an "official branch," "licensed operator," or "exclusive representative" of c-ECO.

2.7 Methodological Integrity Protection

The Collaborator expressly acknowledges that:

  • It does not possess, and shall not claim, any independent certification authority over the c-ECO methodology;
  • Participation in this fellowship program does not confer any permanent authorization to teach, replicate, sublicense, or commercially exploit the c-ECO methodological framework independently of the Foundation;
  • All materials, curricula, analytical instruments, and proprietary frameworks provided under this Agreement remain the exclusive intellectual property of the Foundation, subject to the terms of Section 9;
  • Any unauthorized representation that the Collaborator offers independent c-ECO certification or training shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement.

3. Fellowship Structure

The c-ECO Fellowship Program is structured as a systemic governance and applied research-extension program, not as a conventional academic degree program or commercial training course.

3.1 Program Components

  • Core Modules: Guided methodological participation in contractual equity, ecological co-responsibility, pre-damage risk analysis, and systemic environmental governance;
  • Living Lab Engagement: Supervised field-based application within designated Living Lab territories, coordinated through c-ECO institutional structures or regional coordination nodes, where applicable;
  • Case-Specific Analytical Mandate (CSAM): Directed analytical work on specific governance challenges, producing deliverables under Foundation supervision;
  • Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI): Structured compliance and quality assurance framework ensuring fidelity to c-ECO analytical standards.

3.2 Fellowship Participation Support

Fellowship participation support shall be administered directly by the Foundation. The Collaborator's role is limited to regional fellowship coordination support, including:

  • Coordination of sponsored fellowship participation within the designated region;
  • Local logistical facilitation (venue, scheduling, community liaison);
  • Communication support between fellows and local stakeholders;
  • Documentation and reporting on regional program activities.
Important: The Collaborator shall not engage in the direct disbursement of fellowship funds to fellows, nor shall it hold itself out as a funding entity. All financial flows shall follow the structure set forth in Section 11.

3.3 Duration and Intensity

Standard fellowship engagements shall follow the annual calendar established by the Foundation's Program Administration, including application periods, module schedules, Living Lab deployment windows, and evaluation milestones.

4. Regional Coordination Support

The Collaborator shall designate a Regional Coordination Liaison ("RCL") to serve as the primary institutional contact for fellowship activities within the Designated Territory.

4.1 RCL Qualifications and Designation

The RCL shall be designated by the Collaborator and acknowledged by the Foundation/c-ECO Program Administration. The RCL should possess:

  • Demonstrated institutional standing within the Collaborator's organization;
  • Regional knowledge and community networks relevant to the fellowship's thematic focus;
  • Capacity to coordinate logistical, communicational, and reporting activities;
  • Proficiency in the working language(s) of the fellowship program.

4.2 Scope of Coordination Activities

The RCL and Collaborator personnel may engage in the following coordination activities:

  • Stakeholder Mapping: Identification of relevant local institutions, communities, and potential fellowship hosts;
  • Logistical Facilitation: Assistance with venue arrangements, transportation, accommodation, and field access;
  • Cultural and Linguistic Mediation: Support for fellows engaging with local populations, particularly indigenous and traditional communities;
  • Safety and Compliance Monitoring: Reporting on local conditions, risk factors, and regulatory requirements affecting fellowship activities;
  • Event and Workshop Support: On-site coordination for Foundation-directed training sessions, seminars, and public events.

4.3 Limitations on Coordination Authority

The RCL and Collaborator personnel shall not:

  • Modify, adapt, or reinterpret c-ECO methodological frameworks without written authorization;
  • Make binding commitments on behalf of the Foundation to third parties;
  • Collect fees or payments from fellows for c-ECO program access;
  • Establish parallel certification or credentialing programs using c-ECO-derived materials.

5. Candidate Referral & Institutional Outreach

The Collaborator may engage in candidate referral and institutional outreach activities to support the fellowship program's visibility and applicant pipeline.

5.1 Referral Process

The Collaborator may refer candidates who meet the eligibility criteria established by the Foundation. Referral does not guarantee selection. The Collaborator shall:

  • Provide accurate information about the fellowship structure, requirements, and expectations;
  • Direct candidates to official c-ECO application channels (c-eco.org/fellowship-portal.html);
  • Refrain from making representations about selection probability, funding amounts, or post-fellowship employment;
  • Not charge candidates any application, referral, processing, or access fee in connection with the Fellowship Program;
  • Maintain records of referred candidates for reporting purposes.

5.2 Outreach and Dissemination

The Collaborator may disseminate fellowship information through:

  • Institutional communications (newsletters, websites, social media);
  • Professional networks and association channels;
  • Academic and community events;
  • Direct engagement with potential host institutions and community leaders.

All outreach materials must be pre-approved by the Foundation or drawn from the official communications repository provided by c-ECO.

5.3 Non-Discrimination

Candidate referral and outreach shall be conducted without discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, religion, national origin, disability, age, or any other protected characteristic. The Foundation and the Collaborator are committed to diversity, equity, and inclusion in fellowship access.

6. Independent Selection Authority

Core Principle: The Collaborator does not possess, and shall not exercise, independent authority to select, reject, or conditionally admit fellowship candidates. All selection decisions reside exclusively with the Foundation, through the c-ECO Fellowship Program.

6.1 Selection Process Ownership

The Foundation, through the c-ECO Fellowship Program, retains sole and exclusive authority over:

  • Establishment and publication of eligibility criteria;
  • Application review and evaluation methodology;
  • Interview and assessment procedures;
  • Final selection and placement decisions;
  • Waitlist management and alternative candidate identification.

6.2 Collaborator Input

The Collaborator may provide non-binding contextual information regarding referred candidates, including:

  • Verification of institutional affiliation or professional standing;
  • Regional relevance of the candidate's proposed focus area;
  • Community or stakeholder endorsement (where appropriate and consensual);
  • Logistical feasibility of proposed Living Lab engagement.

Such input shall be advisory only and shall not substitute for the Foundation's independent evaluation.

6.3 Transparency and Accountability

The Foundation shall maintain documented selection records and shall provide the Collaborator with anonymized, aggregated data on candidate outcomes from the Collaborator's referral pool, subject to data protection obligations under Section 13.

7. Regional Administrative and Coordination Support

The Collaborator shall provide administrative and coordination support for fellowship operations within its Designated Territory, in coordination with and subject to program standards established by the Foundation/c-ECO.

7.1 Administrative Functions

  • Documentation Management: Collection, organization, and secure transmission of fellowship-related documents (applications, agreements, reports, evaluations);
  • Scheduling Coordination: Alignment of fellowship activities with local calendars, institutional schedules, and seasonal considerations;
  • Communication Relay: Timely transmission of Foundation directives, announcements, and inquiries to fellows and local stakeholders;
  • Regional Activity Reporting: Non-evaluative reporting on attendance, logistical participation, local conditions, and implementation challenges.

7.2 Data and Reporting

The Collaborator shall submit periodic reports to the Foundation in the format and frequency specified by the Foundation's Program Administration. Reports shall include:

  • Number of candidates referred and their disposition status;
  • Summary of regional coordination activities undertaken;
  • Challenges, risks, or incidents affecting fellowship operations;
  • Recommendations for program improvement from a regional perspective.

7.3 No Financial Intermediation

The Collaborator shall not serve as an intermediary for fellowship funds, stipends, or financial benefits. All financial administration shall follow the structure described in Section 11.

8. Institutional Recognition and Completion Records

8.1 Foundation Recognition Authority

All institutional recognition, completion records, and formal acknowledgment of fellowship engagement shall be issued exclusively by the Foundation. The Collaborator does not possess independent recognition authority and shall not issue records that could be construed as c-ECO-endorsed without explicit written authorization.

8.2 Nature of Recognition

Fellowship completion recognition may include:

  • Record of Fellowship Completion (Foundation-issued);
  • Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI) compliance attestation;
  • Case-Specific Analytical Mandate (CSAM) completion record;
  • Letter confirming participation, completion, or institutional contribution, where appropriate;
  • Listing in the c-ECO Fellowship Alumni Registry.

8.3 No Operational License or Deployment Authorization

Participation in the Fellowship Program, including successful completion, recognition, or issuance of completion records, does not confer any operational license, deployment authorization, commercial implementation right, sector authorization, or independent methodological accreditation with respect to the c-ECO framework, TFP systems, or related analytical instruments.

Any operational deployment, institutional implementation, sector-specific authorization, monitoring engagement, or licensed methodological use shall require a separate written agreement, license instrument, or deployment authorization issued exclusively by the Foundation.

8.4 Academic Recognition

Any academic credit, curricular equivalency, or continuing education recognition shall require a separate written agreement between the Foundation and the relevant academic institution. No automatic academic credit is conferred by participation in this Agreement.

8.5 Prohibition on Independent Credentialing

The Collaborator expressly covenants that it shall not:

  • Create, offer, or market independent certification programs derived from c-ECO materials;
  • Use c-ECO trademarks, symbols (including any registered or unregistered symbols, logos, names, and marks associated with c-ECO), or nomenclature in connection with non-c-ECO training or educational offerings;
  • Represent that completion of the Collaborator's own programs (separate from this fellowship) confers c-ECO recognition or endorsement.

9. Intellectual Property & Restricted Materials

9.1 Ownership Framework

All intellectual property rights in and to the c-ECO methodology, analytical instruments, curricula, training materials, software platforms, data frameworks, and derivative works ("c-ECO IP") shall remain the exclusive property of the Foundation. No transfer of ownership, license (except as expressly granted herein), or joint ownership is conferred by this Agreement.

9.2 Limited Use License

The Foundation grants the Collaborator a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to use c-ECO IP solely for the purposes of performing its obligations under this Agreement. This license permits:

  • Internal review and study of c-ECO materials by designated Collaborator personnel;
  • Distribution of c-ECO-approved materials to enrolled fellows, strictly within the fellowship context;
  • Reference to c-ECO concepts and frameworks in reports and communications, with proper attribution.

9.3 Prohibited Uses

The Collaborator shall not:

  • Reproduce, modify, adapt, translate, or create derivative works from c-ECO IP without prior written consent;
  • Reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any c-ECO analytical instruments or software;
  • Remove or alter copyright notices, trademarks, or proprietary markings from c-ECO materials;
  • Sub-license, sell, rent, lease, or otherwise commercialize c-ECO IP to third parties;
  • Use c-ECO IP for purposes outside the scope of this Agreement.

9.4 Restricted Methodological Access

Access to restricted analytical instruments, TFP structures, advanced methodological components, deployment protocols, or sensitive governance materials may be conditioned upon:

  • Role-based authorization;
  • Completion status;
  • Jurisdictional restrictions;
  • Sector-specific approval;
  • Additional confidentiality obligations;
  • Separate licensing or deployment agreements.

9.5 Fellow-Generated Content

Works, analyses, reports, and creative materials produced by fellows during their fellowship engagement shall be governed by the Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA) executed between each fellow and the Foundation. The Collaborator acknowledges that it has no ownership claim to fellow-generated content, except where the Collaborator itself contributes original material under a separate written agreement.

9.6 Trademark Protection

The Collaborator shall use c-ECO trademarks (including "c-ECO," "Contractual Equity & Ecological Co-responsibility," any registered or unregistered symbols, logos, names, and marks associated with c-ECO) only in accordance with the Brand Guidelines and any supplementary written directives from the Foundation.

10. Branding, Communications & Public Statements

10.1 Joint Branding Protocol

In public communications, the Collaborator may identify itself as a "Regional Institutional Collaborator" or "Supporting Institutional Node" of the c-ECO Fellowship Program. All such references must:

  • Be accurate and not misleading as to the nature or scope of the collaboration;
  • Include the c-ECO logo and the Collaborator's logo in equal visual prominence, unless otherwise approved by the Foundation in a manner proportionate to the nature of each communication;
  • Include a disclaimer where appropriate: "This collaboration represents institutional coordination support and does not imply exclusive representation, franchising, or operational integration."

10.2 Pre-Approval of Public Statements

The Collaborator shall obtain prior written approval from the Foundation for:

  • Press releases or media statements referencing c-ECO or the fellowship program;
  • Social media campaigns or promotional materials using c-ECO branding;
  • Academic publications or conference presentations describing the collaboration;
  • Any statement that could reasonably be construed as representing c-ECO's official position.

10.3 Website and Digital Presence

The Collaborator may maintain a dedicated webpage describing its role in the c-ECO Fellowship Program, provided such webpage:

  • Links prominently to c-eco.org as the authoritative program source;
  • Does not replicate c-ECO application or enrollment functions;
  • Is kept current and accurate;
  • Is subject to the Foundation's periodic review and approval.

11. Financial Administration

Structural Principle: The fellow's primary financial and contractual relationship is with the Foundation. The Collaborator provides regional coordination support and may receive operational support funding, but does not function as a financial intermediary between the Foundation and fellows.

11.1 Fellowship Stipend and Support

Fellowship participation support, including stipends, travel allowances, and material support, shall be determined by the Foundation and disbursed directly to fellows or through the Foundation's designated financial infrastructure. The Collaborator shall not receive or disburse funds on behalf of individual fellows, unless expressly authorized under a separate written funding schedule.

11.2 Operational Support Funding

The Foundation may provide the Collaborator with operational support funding to defray costs associated with regional coordination activities. Such funding shall be:

  • Documented through a separate funding schedule or budget annex;
  • Used exclusively for agreed-upon coordination activities;
  • Accounted for through periodic financial reports submitted to the Foundation;
  • Subject to audit by the Foundation or its designated representatives.

This does not prohibit reimbursement of approved local logistical expenses incurred by the Collaborator under a separate funding schedule.

11.3 No Revenue Sharing or Commission

This Agreement does not create any revenue-sharing, commission, franchise fee, royalty, finder's fee, tuition sharing, enrollment-based compensation, or similar arrangement. Any compensation to the Collaborator shall be structured as cost reimbursement or service support, not commission, tuition sharing, or revenue participation.

11.4 Financial Transparency

Both Parties shall maintain accurate financial records pertaining to this Agreement. The Foundation shall have the right to request and review such records upon reasonable notice. The Collaborator shall promptly report any suspected financial irregularity, fraud, or misappropriation affecting fellowship funds.

12. Compliance, Ethics & Regulatory Integrity

12.1 Applicable Law

Each Party shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and regulatory requirements in its respective jurisdiction, including but not limited to:

  • Anti-corruption and anti-bribery laws (e.g., U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, UK Bribery Act, Brazilian Clean Company Act);
  • Data protection and privacy laws (e.g., LGPD, GDPR);
  • Labor and employment laws affecting fellows and staff;
  • Environmental and indigenous rights protections applicable to field activities, where applicable;
  • Tax and financial reporting obligations.

12.2 Ethics and Integrity

The Collaborator shall adhere to the highest standards of ethical conduct in all fellowship-related activities. This includes:

  • Transparency in candidate referral and selection support;
  • Respect for intellectual property and academic integrity;
  • Protection of vulnerable populations and community rights;
  • Avoidance of conflicts of interest in program administration;
  • Prompt reporting of ethical concerns or misconduct to the Foundation.

12.3 Indigenous and Community Protocols

Where applicable, and where fellowship activities involve indigenous territories, traditional communities, or protected areas, the Collaborator shall ensure compliance with:

  • Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC) protocols;
  • Community-established research and engagement guidelines;
  • Applicable territorial protection instruments and management plans;
  • c-ECO's Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP), as set forth in Exhibit C to the Fellowship Participation Agreement.

12.4 Anti-Corruption Representation

The Collaborator represents and warrants that neither it nor any of its personnel involved in this Agreement has been convicted of, or is currently under investigation for, any corruption-related offense. The Collaborator shall immediately notify the Foundation of any change in this representation.

13. Confidentiality

13.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other ("Receiving Party") in connection with this Agreement, whether oral, written, electronic, or in any other form, including but not limited to:

  • c-ECO methodological frameworks, analytical instruments, and training materials not publicly available;
  • Fellow personal data, application materials, and evaluation records;
  • Financial terms, budgets, and funding arrangements;
  • Strategic plans, partnership negotiations, and institutional intelligence;
  • Community data and territorial information subject to protective protocols.

13.2 Obligations of Confidentiality

The Receiving Party shall:

  • Maintain Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  • Use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of this Agreement;
  • Disclose Confidential Information only to personnel with a need-to-know, bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations;
  • Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

13.3 Exceptions

Confidentiality obligations shall not apply to information that:

  • Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
  • Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure;
  • Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;
  • Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt notice and cooperates in seeking protective measures.

13.4 Data Protection

The Parties shall process personal data in accordance with applicable data protection laws. The Collaborator shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect fellow and stakeholder personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

13.5 Survival

Confidentiality obligations shall survive for five (5) years after termination of this Agreement. Trade secrets, proprietary methodologies, non-public analytical frameworks, and TFP core logic shall remain protected for so long as such information remains non-public.

14. Independent Institutional Status

14.1 Affirmation of Independence

The Parties affirm that this Agreement does not create any employment, agency, partnership, joint venture, or franchise relationship. Each Party is an independent institution, responsible for its own governance, personnel, finances, and legal compliance.

14.2 No Control or Supervision

The Foundation shall not control or supervise the Collaborator's internal operations, personnel decisions, or institutional governance, except as necessary to ensure compliance with this Agreement and protection of the Foundation's intellectual property and methodological integrity. The Collaborator shall not control or supervise the Foundation's fellowship administration, selection processes, or strategic decisions.

14.3 Liability Separation

Each Party shall be solely responsible for its own acts and omissions. Neither Party shall be liable for the debts, obligations, or wrongful acts of the other, except as expressly assumed under this Agreement.

14.4 No Integration

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as authorizing either Party to act as the legal representative of the other, to bind the other contractually, or to create any appearance of institutional merger, acquisition, or consolidation.

15. Term, Suspension & Termination

15.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of twelve (12) months, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section.

15.2 Renewal

The Agreement may be renewed for successive one-year terms by mutual written consent of the Parties, subject to performance review and strategic alignment assessment.

15.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

15.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

  • Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice;
  • Engages in fraudulent, corrupt, or illegal conduct affecting the fellowship program;
  • Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases operations;
  • Misrepresents its institutional status, credentials, or authority in a manner that damages the Foundation's reputation or methodological integrity.

15.5 Suspension

The Foundation may suspend the Collaborator's participation in the fellowship program, pending investigation and resolution, if:

  • Serious allegations of misconduct, ethical violation, or regulatory non-compliance are received;
  • The Collaborator's actions create imminent risk of harm to fellows, communities, or the program's integrity;
  • Emergency circumstances (political instability, natural disaster, public health crisis) render regional operations unsafe or impracticable.

15.6 Post-Termination Obligations

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Collaborator shall:

  • Immediately cease using c-ECO trademarks, materials, and intellectual property;
  • Return or destroy all Confidential Information and c-ECO materials in its possession;
  • Complete transition of ongoing regional coordination records and pending logistical matters to the Foundation or a designated successor collaborator;
  • Submit final reports and financial accountings as requested by the Foundation;
  • Continue to honor confidentiality obligations under Section 13.

16. Governing Law & Order of Precedence

16.1 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of the State of Missouri, unless the Parties mutually agree to alternative dispute resolution (mediation or arbitration) in writing.

16.2 Dispute Resolution

Before initiating litigation, the Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through direct negotiation between their respective designated executives. If direct negotiation fails within sixty (60) days, the Parties may escalate to non-binding mediation administered by a mutually agreed mediation institution.

16.3 Order of Precedence

In case of conflict between this Agreement and any appendices, schedules, promotional materials, or informal communications, this Agreement shall prevail. The following order of precedence shall apply:

  1. This Agreement (including Section 16);
  2. Duly executed written amendments to this Agreement;
  3. Appendices and schedules specifically referenced and incorporated herein;
  4. Other written instruments executed by both Parties (e.g., funding schedules, academic cooperation annexes);
  5. Operational guidelines and brand directives issued by the Foundation.

16.4 Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, or if modification is not possible, severed from this Agreement, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

16.5 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with its Appendices and any duly executed amendments, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral.

16.6 Amendments

No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

16.7 Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other, except that the Foundation may assign this Agreement to an affiliate or successor entity in connection with a merger, acquisition, or corporate reorganization.

16.8 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures and PDF executions shall be deemed valid and binding.

16.9 Language

This Agreement is executed in English. In the event of any conflict between the English version and any translation, the English version shall prevail.
