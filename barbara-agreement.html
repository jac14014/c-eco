WHEREAS, JCHF is a Missouri nonprofit organization established for nonprofit, educational, research, governance, and institutional development purposes;

WHEREAS, JCHF supports the development of the c-ECO Global System, including systemic governance, institutional programs, international collaboration, and related nonprofit initiatives;

WHEREAS, the Appointee has been invited to serve in a voluntary institutional capacity as Secretary of JCHF and to support c-ECO as International Programs Officer;

WHEREAS, the parties intend to clarify that the present appointment is voluntary, institutional, unpaid, and non-employment in nature;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

Instrument Architecture

  • This Agreement governs the JCHF ↔ Appointee voluntary institutional relationship
  • The Secretary appointment is voluntary and institutional in nature
  • The International Programs Officer role is connected to c-ECO project development
  • No current employment or salary is created
  • Any future paid allocation requires separate written agreement
  • Confidentiality and governance obligations apply immediately

1. Definitions

“Appointee” means Barbara Oliva, the individual appointed under this Agreement to serve in voluntary institutional capacities for JCHF and c-ECO-related initiatives.

“c-ECO” means the Contractual Equity & Ecological Co-Responsibility systemic governance framework and associated institutional programs.

“Foundation” or “JCHF” means Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, a Missouri nonprofit corporation.

“Volunteer Service” means unpaid, voluntary institutional participation without employment relationship, salary, wages, benefits, or guaranteed compensation.

2. Purpose and Scope

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to formalize the Appointee’s voluntary institutional service to JCHF and c-ECO-related initiatives, define the scope of the appointment, clarify authority limitations, and protect confidential institutional information.

2.2 Scope

This Agreement governs only the current voluntary institutional relationship between the parties. It does not govern future employment, consulting, grant-funded allocation, or paid contractor arrangements unless separately documented in a future written agreement signed by both parties.

Important Status Clarification:
This Agreement documents a voluntary institutional appointment only and shall not be interpreted as an employment contract, compensation plan, or guarantee of future paid work.

3. Appointment

3.1 Foundation Secretary

JCHF hereby appoints Barbara Oliva to serve as Secretary of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation in a voluntary institutional capacity, subject to applicable bylaws, board oversight, and nonprofit governance policies.

3.2 International Programs Officer

JCHF further recognizes Barbara Oliva as International Programs Officer for c-ECO, in a voluntary project-support capacity related to international program development, institutional coordination, and cross-border initiatives connected to the c-ECO Global System.

3.3 Acceptance

The Appointee accepts the foregoing appointments subject to the limitations and conditions set forth in this Agreement.

4. Voluntary Nature of Service

4.1 Voluntary Participation

The Appointee’s participation is voluntary, institutional, unpaid, and nonprofit-supportive in nature.

4.2 No Current Compensation

No salary, wages, employee benefits, health insurance, retirement benefits, stipend, bonus, commission, or guaranteed compensation is provided under this Agreement.

4.3 No Required Work Schedule

The parties acknowledge that the Appointee is not subject to a fixed employment schedule, mandatory hours, payroll supervision, or employment-style control under this Agreement.

Funding Contingency:
Any future compensated role, project allocation, stipend, contractor arrangement, or paid program officer position is expressly contingent upon funding availability, organizational approval, legal compliance, and a separate written agreement.

5. Duties and Responsibilities

5.1 Secretary Duties

As Secretary, the Appointee may support JCHF in the following areas:

  1. institutional records and organizational documentation;
  2. meeting coordination and agenda preparation;
  3. minutes, resolutions, and governance files;
  4. internal communication and administrative coordination;
  5. document retention and nonprofit governance support.

5.2 International Programs Officer Duties

As International Programs Officer for c-ECO, the Appointee may support:

  1. international program coordination and operational support;
  2. Brazil–United States institutional communication workflows;
  3. partner outreach logistics and administrative preparation;
  4. support for c-ECO Living Labs and fellowship initiatives;
  5. future grant-funded or partnership-based program structures.

6. Future Funding and Potential Paid Allocation

6.1 Future Allocation Condition

The parties acknowledge that the Appointee may be considered for future compensated allocation within JCHF, c-ECO, international programs, grants, operational projects, or institutional initiatives only if and when funding becomes available.

6.2 Separate Written Agreement Required

Any future compensated arrangement shall require a separate written agreement executed by the parties, specifying title, scope of work, compensation, payment mechanism, tax treatment, reporting structure, duration, and applicable legal classification.

6.3 No Promise of Future Compensation

Nothing in this Agreement shall be interpreted as a promise, guarantee, expectation, implied contract, or binding commitment to provide future employment, compensation, stipend, salary, consulting fees, grant-funded payment, or any other financial benefit.

6.4 Future Terms to Be Negotiated

If funding becomes available, the amount, position, scope, classification, and terms of any future compensated role shall be negotiated and agreed separately at that future time.

7. No Employment Relationship

7.1 No Employment

The parties expressly agree that this Agreement does not create an employment relationship between the Appointee and JCHF under Missouri law, United States federal law, Brazilian law, or any other applicable labor or employment framework.

7.2 No Agency or Partnership

This Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, agency relationship, franchise, commercial representation, or legal mandate authorizing the Appointee to bind JCHF or c-ECO.

7.3 Tax and Labor Classification

Because no compensation is paid under this Agreement, the Appointee is not placed on payroll and no employment tax withholding, benefits administration, or contractor payment reporting is created by this Agreement.

7.4 Future Reclassification

If the parties later enter into a paid arrangement, classification shall be determined at that time through a separate written instrument and in accordance with applicable law.

8. Limitation of Authority

8.1 No Binding Authority

Unless expressly authorized in writing by JCHF, the Appointee shall not:

  1. execute contracts or agreements on behalf of JCHF or c-ECO;
  2. incur debts, obligations, or expenses in the name of JCHF or c-ECO;
  3. open bank accounts or manage financial accounts on behalf of JCHF;
  4. represent that she has authority to approve grants, employment, compensation, partnerships, licenses, or institutional commitments;
  5. make public statements that bind JCHF, c-ECO, or their leadership;
  6. commit JCHF or c-ECO to any partnership, program, transaction, or legal obligation.

8.2 Authorized Representation

The Appointee may identify herself as Secretary of JCHF and International Programs Officer for c-ECO only in a manner consistent with this Agreement, JCHF instructions, and approved institutional materials.

8.3 Public Materials

Any public-facing materials, biographies, posts, institutional announcements, or communications using JCHF or c-ECO names, marks, seals, logos, or official titles should remain consistent with approved institutional language.

9. Confidentiality

9.1 Confidential Information

The Appointee shall maintain strict confidentiality regarding all non-public information received, accessed, created, or discussed in connection with JCHF or c-ECO, including:

  1. internal governance documents and board communications;
  2. donor, partner, volunteer, advisor, and applicant information;
  3. strategic plans, fundraising plans, grant strategies, budgets, and operational roadmaps;
  4. unpublished c-ECO materials, methods, program structures, and implementation documents;
  5. legal, financial, compliance, tax, and institutional records;
  6. communications involving potential partners, funders, advisors, fellows, collaborators, or institutions.

9.2 Non-Disclosure

The Appointee shall not disclose confidential information to any third party without prior written authorization from JCHF, unless required by law.

9.3 Return or Destruction

Upon request or termination, the Appointee shall return, delete, or destroy confidential documents and materials in her possession, except where retention is required by law or expressly authorized by JCHF.

9.4 Survival

The confidentiality obligations under this Section survive termination of this Agreement.

10. Intellectual Property

10.1 JCHF and c-ECO Materials

All JCHF and c-ECO documents, frameworks, methodologies, institutional designs, governance structures, program materials, strategic content, presentations, templates, manuals, websites, and related work products remain the property of their respective owner or custodian.

10.2 Work Product

Any documents, drafts, organizational materials, communications, summaries, templates, program support materials, or other work product prepared by the Appointee specifically for JCHF or c-ECO within the scope of this Agreement shall be treated as institutional materials of JCHF unless otherwise agreed in writing.

10.3 Use Restrictions

The Appointee may not reproduce, publish, sublicense, commercialize, distribute, or create derivative works from JCHF or c-ECO proprietary materials outside the scope authorized by JCHF.

11. Expenses and Reimbursement

11.1 Pre-Approval Required

JCHF may, in its sole discretion and subject to available resources, reimburse reasonable expenses incurred by the Appointee for authorized institutional activities, provided such expenses are approved in advance in writing.

11.2 No Wages

Reimbursement of approved expenses shall not be interpreted as wages, salary, compensation, stipend, benefits, or evidence of employment.

11.3 Documentation

Any approved reimbursement request must be supported by receipts, descriptions, dates, purpose, and other documentation requested by JCHF.

12. Compliance and Conduct

12.1 Compliance Standards

The Appointee agrees to act in accordance with applicable laws, nonprofit governance standards, JCHF policies, confidentiality rules, conflict of interest principles, and ethical conduct expectations.

12.2 Institutional Integrity

The Appointee shall avoid any action that could reasonably damage JCHF’s reputation, undermine c-ECO’s institutional credibility, misrepresent authority, or create legal, financial, governance, or reputational risk.

12.3 No Unauthorized Commitments

The Appointee shall not make unauthorized commitments, promises, representations, or assurances regarding funding, employment, program admission, partnership status, certification, grants, or compensation.

13. Conflict of Interest

13.1 Disclosure

The Appointee shall disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest that may affect her service to JCHF or c-ECO, including financial interests, family relationships, institutional affiliations, paid engagements, or relationships with prospective partners, vendors, donors, or collaborators.

13.2 Annual Statement

JCHF may require the Appointee to complete an annual conflict of interest disclosure statement and related governance documentation.

13.3 Recusal

Where appropriate, JCHF may require the Appointee to recuse herself from discussions, communications, or decisions involving a conflict of interest.

14. Term and Termination

14.1 Term

This Agreement shall begin on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until terminated by either party or replaced by a subsequent written agreement.

14.2 Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by written notice to the other party.

14.3 Immediate Termination by JCHF

JCHF may terminate this Agreement immediately if the Appointee:

  1. breaches confidentiality or intellectual property obligations;
  2. engages in unauthorized representation or unauthorized commitments;
  3. misuses JCHF or c-ECO names, marks, materials, or institutional status;
  4. fails to comply with applicable governance or conduct expectations;
  5. engages in conduct creating legal, financial, governance, reputational, or institutional risk.

14.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, the Appointee shall cease using the relevant titles, stop representing any active role with JCHF or c-ECO, return or destroy confidential materials upon request, and cooperate in reasonable transition of institutional records or responsibilities.

15. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

15.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

15.2 Good-Faith Resolution

The parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising from or relating to this Agreement through informal written communication.

15.3 Venue

If informal resolution fails, any legal proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be brought in a court of competent jurisdiction in Missouri, United States, unless the parties agree in writing to another dispute resolution process.

15.4 Equitable Relief

JCHF may seek injunctive or equitable relief for breaches or threatened breaches of confidentiality, intellectual property, authority limitations, or misuse of institutional materials.

16. Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email, courier, certified mail, or other verifiable written method to the contact information listed in the parties block, or to such other address as either party may designate by written notice.

17. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the complete understanding between the parties regarding the voluntary institutional appointment described herein and supersedes prior oral or written understandings regarding the same subject matter.

No amendment or modification shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

A.2 Permitted Support Activities

  1. organizing institutional documents and records;
  2. supporting meeting logistics, agenda preparation, and minutes;

  4. assisting with Brazil–United States coordination workflows;

  1. available budget;
  2. organizational approval;

Pre-approved expenses may be reimbursed if authorized in writing by JCHF and supported by proper documentation. Expense reimbursement is not compensation.
