Instrument Architecture
  • This Agreement governs mutual institutional confidentiality between HF and ISM
  • It is distinct from the CDGA, which governs data governance for admitted Fellows
  • Institutional operations are governed by the SDA No. 1 and Institutional Operations Addendum
  • Fellowship participation is governed by the FPA and associated Exhibits
  • All restricted disclosures may be subject to TFP, Safe Mode, and role-based access controls

This Institutional Confidentiality and Information Protection Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") for the purpose of enabling institutional cooperation, strategic coordination, and protected information sharing between HF and ISM in connection with the c-ECO Framework, the Threshold Function Protocol ("TFP"), the c-ECO Fellowship Program, the Amazon Living Lab, and associated governance structures.

This Agreement operates as a mutual institutional confidentiality and intellectual-property protection instrument. It protects both Parties' proprietary, strategic, and operational information shared in the course of institutional collaboration. It does not replace any MOU, SDA, FPA, CDGA, data-access protocol, licensing instrument, or other written instrument that may later be required by either Party.

Important: Access to c-ECO or TFP materials does not create any license, ownership right, implementation right, teaching right, commercial right, derivative-framework right, or authority to represent, reproduce, adapt, disclose, or operationalize the c-ECO Framework without HF's prior written authorization.

1. Confidential Information

For purposes of this Agreement, "Confidential Information" includes, without limitation, all non-public information disclosed by either Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other (the "Receiving Party"), whether written, oral, visual, digital, technical, operational, strategic, institutional, legal, scientific, financial, educational, or otherwise.

  • HF Confidential Information: TFP operational structures, variables, indicators, formulas, trigger logic, threshold logic, Safe Mode procedures, analytical architecture, band classification logic, implementation design; c-ECO governance methodologies, legal theories, institutional architecture, model clauses, sectoral workbooks, protocols, draft statutes, manuals, papers, templates, and strategic materials; Fellowship structures, candidate materials, partner discussions, institutional negotiations, Living Lab plans, implementation roadmaps, funding strategies, partner contacts, and referral opportunities; technical, legal, scientific, commercial, educational, operational, or financial information disclosed in connection with HF, c-ECO, TFP, the Fellowship Program, or the Amazon Living Lab;
  • ISM Confidential Information: Institutional governance structure, academic research protocols, local partner networks, community engagement methodologies, territorial data, public-interest project strategies, funding applications, and operational information not publicly disclosed;
  • Any information marked or identified as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or "Trade Secret," or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the disclosure or the context in which it is provided.

1.1 TFP Integration and Disclosure Cessation

In the event of TFP band classification Red, Black, Safe Mode activation, emergency review, or any comparable heightened-risk disclosure condition determined by HF, all disclosure, discussion, dissemination, and further processing of HF Confidential Information shall immediately cease pending institutional review.

Such cessation is non-discretionary and reflects internal governance, systemic risk, legal risk, reputational risk, data-governance risk, or operational-risk conditions as assessed by HF.

1.2 Trade Secret Definition

"Trade Secrets" include any proprietary methodology, unpublished framework, formula, pattern, compilation, program, device, method, technique, process, threshold system, analytical model, know-how, implementation architecture, strategic process, sectoral mapping, confidential compilation, or operational logic related to c-ECO or TFP that derives actual or potential value from not being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy.

The TFP core logic, Safe Operating Space calibration parameters, sectoral threshold mappings, proprietary analytical instruments, and unpublished c-ECO methodological materials constitute Trade Secrets to the extent permitted by applicable law.

2. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

Each Party agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all Confidential Information of the other Party and to use such information solely for the limited purpose of institutional cooperation expressly authorized under the MOU, SDA, and associated instruments.

  • Each Party shall use at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care;
  • Each Party shall not disclose the other Party's Confidential Information to any third party without prior written approval;
  • Each Party shall not copy, reproduce, distribute, publish, upload, teach, present, summarize, circulate, or transmit Confidential Information except as expressly authorized in writing;
  • Each Party shall not use Confidential Information for any purpose detrimental to the other Party, the c-ECO Framework, the TFP, or associated institutional interests;
  • Each Party shall adopt reasonable physical, electronic, and procedural security measures to prevent unauthorized access, copying, disclosure, or misuse;
  • Each Party shall promptly notify the other of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, access, loss, or misuse of Confidential Information.

2.1 Authorized Disclosure

Either Party may disclose the other Party's Confidential Information only to individuals or entities expressly approved in writing on a case-by-case basis before disclosure.

General authorization, professional status, institutional affiliation, participation in discussions, or involvement in a related matter shall not constitute authorization to receive Confidential Information. The Receiving Party shall ensure that any authorized person is bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those contained herein, and shall remain responsible for any breach by such person.

2.2 Return and Destruction

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return, delete, or destroy all Confidential Information and related copies, notes, summaries, extracts, working files, digital files, printed materials, derivative materials, and tangible embodiments in its possession or control.

The Receiving Party shall certify such return, deletion, or destruction in writing within fifteen (15) days of the Disclosing Party's request. One archival copy may be retained solely to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement, provided such copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations herein.

3. Intellectual Property and No License

All rights, title, and interests in and to the c-ECO Framework, TFP, sectoral workbooks, methodological instruments, legal architecture, operational logic, drafts, models, protocols, publications, visual materials, training materials, institutional strategies, and associated concepts remain exclusively with HF and/or their respective authors, contributors, or affiliated entities, including Jacqueline A. Ennis as lead architect and doctrinal custodian.

No license, assignment, transfer, waiver, commercial right, implementation right, teaching right, sublicensing right, publication right, derivative-work right, ownership interest, or implied authorization is granted under this Agreement or by virtue of any disclosure of Confidential Information.

ISM shall not apply for, register, claim, or assist any third party in applying for, registering, or claiming any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, domain name, methodology, framework, program, protocol, or other intellectual-property protection based on or derived from HF Confidential Information.

4. No Reverse Engineering or Derivative Frameworks

ISM shall not reverse engineer, reconstruct, imitate, repackage, adapt, translate, teach, commercialize, implement, operationalize, or create derivative frameworks, methodologies, protocols, models, clauses, training programs, consulting services, institutional structures, or governance architectures based on the c-ECO Framework, TFP, or any HF Confidential Information.

ISM shall not use HF Confidential Information to develop, support, advise, promote, or participate in any competing or substantially similar framework, initiative, fellowship, governance model, threshold protocol, consulting model, academic program, or institutional program without HF's prior written authorization.

5. Non-Circumvention and Institutional Coordination

Neither Party shall use Confidential Information, institutional contacts, strategic opportunities, partner introductions, candidate pipelines, funding discussions, implementation opportunities, licensing discussions, or related institutional initiatives disclosed through the collaboration to bypass, exclude, undermine, or prejudice the other Party’s role, rights, authorship, institutional position, or strategic interests.

ISM shall not independently represent, negotiate, contract, license, commercialize, or operationalize the c-ECO Framework, TFP, Fellowship Program, Amazon Living Lab, or related institutional opportunities without HF’s prior written authorization.

Nothing in this Section restricts ISM from pursuing its own independent academic, public-interest, educational, research, civic, institutional, or funding activities that are not based on HF Confidential Information, do not use the c-ECO Framework, do not create confusion regarding c-ECO ownership or authorization, and do not conflict with the MOU, SDA, or this Agreement.

For a period of twenty-four (24) months from the Effective Date, opportunities specifically introduced, developed, or disclosed through the HF–ISM collaboration in relation to c-ECO shall be coordinated through HF unless otherwise authorized in writing.

6. Relationship to CDGA and Data Governance

This Agreement does not authorize ISM to access, process, store, transfer, analyze, govern, export, disclose, or otherwise handle candidate data, fellow data, institutional data, sensitive data, technical datasets, operational system data, or any other data subject to data-governance controls, except as expressly authorized under a separate CDGA or data-processing instrument.

In the event of inconsistency between this Agreement and any later CDGA concerning data governance, the more specific data-governance instrument shall control.

Institutional Scope Limitation
This Agreement protects institutional information shared between HF and ISM. It does not grant ISM access to individual candidate data, fellow records, or personal information governed by the CDGA. Any such access requires separate written authorization and compliance with applicable data protection laws (LGPD, GDPR, etc.).

7. Exceptions

The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate through written records:

  1. Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;
  2. Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;
  3. Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without restriction and without breach of any confidentiality obligation;
  4. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
  5. Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order or confidential treatment.

8. Remedies

Each Party acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure, misuse, circumvention, reverse engineering, derivative use, or unauthorized implementation of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone may be insufficient.

Each Party shall therefore be entitled to seek injunctive relief, temporary restraining orders, preliminary and permanent injunctions, specific performance, equitable remedies, accounting, damages, attorneys' fees where available, and any other remedies available under applicable law, in equity, or under this Agreement.

9. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States, without regard to conflict-of-law principles, consistent with the MOU and applicable SDA instruments between the Parties.

The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute arising from or relating to this Agreement through good-faith institutional consultation between their authorized representatives.

If the dispute is not resolved through institutional consultation within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be resolved in accordance with the dispute-resolution framework established in the MOU, unless the Parties agree in writing to arbitration or another appropriate mechanism.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive, equitable, or emergency relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent or remedy unauthorized disclosure, misuse, circumvention, reverse engineering, or infringement of Confidential Information or intellectual-property rights.

10. Term and Survival

This Agreement shall remain in effect for five (5) years from the Effective Date.

Obligations relating to Trade Secrets, proprietary methodologies, TFP logic, c-ECO architecture, non-circumvention, no-license obligations, no-reverse-engineering obligations, unauthorized derivative use, and unauthorized implementation shall survive indefinitely to the extent permitted by law.

11. Notices

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email with confirmation of receipt, certified mail, courier, or other traceable delivery method to the addresses set forth in the parties block, or to such other address as either party may designate by written notice.

HF: contact@c-eco.org

12. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court or arbitral tribunal of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

13. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding the confidentiality and protection of Confidential Information disclosed in connection with the matters described herein and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral, concerning such subject matter.

No amendment, modification, or waiver shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.
