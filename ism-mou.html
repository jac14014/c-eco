Memorandum of Understanding
Institutional Cooperation Agreement
Johann Christian Hasse Foundation & Instituto Silvio Meira
Executed February 27, 2026
c-ECO Framework — Institutional Cooperation, Research, Technology Development & Public-Interest Initiatives
Party A — Foundation
Johann Christian Hasse Foundation
A nonprofit corporation organized under the laws of the State of Missouri and recognized under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, with principal place of business at 2504 Grandview Cir, Columbia, MO 65203, United States of America.
Party B — Institute
Instituto Silvio Meira
A nonprofit legal entity organized under the laws of the Federative Republic of Brazil, registered under CNPJ No. 31.441.787/0001-50, formally recognized as an entity of public interest under State Law No. 10.226/2023 and Municipal Law No. 9.946/2023, with principal place of business at Rua Dom Romualdo Coelho, 609 – Cidade Universitária – Belém, PA, CEP 66.055-190, Brazil.

Instrument Architecture

  • This MOU establishes the baseline institutional cooperation framework between the Parties.
  • Project implementation requires separate Subsequent Definitive Agreements for approved initiatives.
  • Financial obligations arise only when expressly established in a duly executed SDA.
  • c-ECO intellectual property remains exclusively with Jacqueline A. Ennis or her designated entity.
  • The Institute may act as an administrative and institutional proponent in Brazil only for projects approved in writing.

1. Parties

This Memorandum of Understanding (“MOU”) is entered into by and between:

Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, a nonprofit corporation organized under the laws of the State of Missouri and recognized under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, with principal place of business at 2504 Grandview Cir, Columbia, MO 65203, United States of America (the “Foundation”);

and

Instituto Silvio Meira, a nonprofit legal entity organized under the laws of the Federative Republic of Brazil, registered under CNPJ No. 31.441.787/0001-50, formally recognized as an entity of public interest under State Law No. 10.226/2023 (State of Pará) and Municipal Law No. 9.946/2023 (Municipality of Belém), acting in the fields of education and research, including the organization of legal research chairs, with principal place of business at Rua Dom Romualdo Coelho, 609 – Cidade Universitária – Belém, PA, CEP 66.055-190, Brazil (the “Institute”).

The Foundation and the Institute may hereinafter be referred to individually as a “Party” and collectively as the “Parties.”

2. Purpose

The purpose of this MOU is to establish a framework for institutional cooperation in connection with research, technological development, and public-interest initiatives involving the c-ECO framework.

The Institute may act as an administrative and institutional proponent in Brazil for grant applications, research calls, public funding opportunities, and collaborative projects aligned with the c-ECO framework, subject to prior written approval by the Foundation.

Institutional Cooperation Scope
This MOU creates a framework for cooperation only. It does not itself activate funding obligations, licensing authority, ownership rights, exclusivity, or independent authority to operationalize c-ECO.

3. Nature of Relationship

3.1 Cooperative Relationship Only

This MOU establishes a cooperative relationship only and does not create a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment relationship, fiduciary relationship, or legal representation between the Parties.

3.2 No Authority to Bind

Neither Party shall have authority to bind, obligate, or commit the other Party to any financial, legal, or contractual obligation without prior written authorization.

3.3 Administrative and Institutional Proponent Role

The Institute shall act solely as an administrative and institutional proponent for projects expressly approved in writing and shall not represent itself as owner, exclusive representative, strategic controller, or governing authority of the c-ECO framework.

4. Intellectual Property

4.1 Exclusive Ownership

The c-ECO framework, including but not limited to its methodology, governance architecture, analytical systems, documentation, conceptual models, algorithms, protocols, decision architecture, and all derivative intellectual components, remains the sole and exclusive intellectual property of Jacqueline A. Ennis or her designated entity.

4.2 No Transfer of Rights

Nothing in this MOU shall be construed as transferring, assigning, licensing except as expressly limited herein, granting exclusivity, or otherwise conveying any ownership interest.

4.3 Limited Authorization

The Institute is granted a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable, and revocable authorization to reference and apply the c-ECO framework solely within expressly approved projects.

4.4 Prohibited Uses

The Institute shall not:

  1. register trademarks, copyrights, translations, or derivative designations incorporating “c-ECO”;
  2. sub-license, assign, or transfer the framework;
  3. modify, commercialize, reverse-engineer, replicate, or independently develop derivative governance systems based on c-ECO;
  4. represent c-ECO as institutional property or co-owned property.

4.5 Attribution

All public references to c-ECO must include clear attribution to Jacqueline A. Ennis.

IP Protection Notice
The MOU preserves c-ECO as exclusively owned intellectual property. Institutional cooperation does not create co-authorship, co-ownership, licensing independence, or derivative governance authority.

5. Project-Specific Agreements

5.1 Subsequent Definitive Agreements

Each approved grant, funded initiative, or collaborative project shall require a separate written Subsequent Definitive Agreement (“SDA”).

5.2 SDA Content

The SDA shall define scope, budget, data governance, publication rights, compensation if applicable, reporting obligations, and allocation of liability.

5.3 No Automatic Obligation

No financial or operational obligation arises under this MOU unless expressly established in a duly executed SDA.

6. Financial Principles

6.1 Formal Applicant Role

The Institute may act as formal applicant where required by Brazilian law.

6.2 Approved Budgets

Funding allocation shall strictly follow approved budgets and the applicable SDA.

6.3 No Success Fees

No commission-based, percentage-based, or success-fee compensation for grant procurement shall be established.

6.4 Administrative Costs

Administrative costs must be transparently disclosed and approved in writing.

Financial Control Principle
The MOU permits the Institute to serve as a formal applicant when legally required in Brazil, but financial allocation, cost approval, and operational obligations must be controlled by approved budgets and separate SDAs.

7. Confidentiality and Protection of Proprietary Information

7.1 Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” includes all non-public technical, structural, operational, strategic, and methodological information relating to c-ECO.

7.2 Included Materials

Confidential Information includes governance architecture, algorithms, scoring systems, protocols, internal documentation, roadmaps, and proprietary analytical logic.

7.3 Protection Standard

The Institute shall protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar nature, and in no event less than reasonable care. The Institute shall not disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent and shall not use it outside the scope of expressly approved projects.

7.4 No Reverse Engineering

The Institute shall not reverse-engineer, replicate, adapt, or derive independent governance systems from Confidential Information.

7.5 Survival

These obligations survive termination for five (5) years, and for trade secrets, for as long as they remain confidential.

7.6 Equitable Relief

Unauthorized disclosure may cause irreparable harm. The Foundation may seek injunctive or equitable relief in addition to other remedies.

8. Non-Circumvention

The Institute shall not, directly or indirectly, engage, solicit, contract, or collaborate with governmental entities, funding bodies, academic institutions, or private organizations in relation to the c-ECO framework independently of the Foundation, without prior written consent.

9. Marketing and Public Communication

9.1 Prior Approval

Public reference to cooperation requires prior written approval of language.

9.2 Accurate Authorship and Ownership

All communications must accurately reflect authorship and ownership.

9.3 No Exclusivity or Territorial Control

No representation shall imply exclusivity or territorial control.

10. Non-Exclusivity

No exclusivity is granted. The Foundation retains full discretion to collaborate with other entities.

11. Term and Termination

11.1 Term

This MOU shall remain effective for three (3) years from signature.

11.2 Termination Notice

Either Party may terminate upon thirty (30) days written notice.

11.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, all authorization to use or reference c-ECO shall immediately cease unless otherwise agreed in writing in a valid SDA.

12. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This MOU shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States of America, without regard to conflict of law principles.

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this MOU shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in the State of Missouri, United States of America.

Each Party irrevocably submits to such jurisdiction and waives any objection based on forum non conveniens or lack of personal jurisdiction.

13. Entire Understanding

This MOU constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, or representations regarding its subject matter.
