Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA)
c-ECO Fellowship Program | Johann Christian Hasse Foundation | Silvio Meira Institute LLC
Binding Contractual Instrument
This Fellowship Participation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation ("Foundation"), acting as institutional steward of the c-ECO framework, and Silvio Meira Institute LLC ("ISM"), acting as operational and implementation partner, collectively the "Program Administrators"; and the individual Fellow identified below.
This Agreement establishes a non-employment relationship governed by New York law and creates binding legal obligations regarding participation in the c-ECO Fellowship Program. Execution of this Agreement is required for Fellowship entry.
PARTIES AND PREAMBLE
[FPA-PARTIES-001] Parties
THE JOHANN CHRISTIAN HASSE FOUNDATION, a non-profit organization organized and existing under the laws of [Jurisdiction], with its principal offices at 2504 Grandview Circle. Columbia, MO. 65203 United Stated Of America ("Foundation"), acting as institutional steward of the c-ECO framework; and
SILVIO MEIRA INSTITUTE LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of Delaware, with its principal offices at 4215 Lindy Circle. Orlando, Fl. 32827 United States Of America ("ISM"), acting as operational and implementation partner for applied studies and project execution;
The Foundation and ISM are collectively referred to as the "Program Administrators" (individually or collectively, "Program");
and [FELLOW NAME], an individual residing at [Address] ("Fellow").
The Program Administrators and Fellow may be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."
[FPA-PREAMBLE-001] Preamble
WHEREAS, the Program operates the c-ECO Fellowship Program ("Fellowship"), a structured analytical initiative designed to operationalize the c-ECO governance architecture through collaborative research and systemic analysis;
WHEREAS, the Fellowship operates as a globally accessible program designed to identify and engage high-capacity analytical talent independent of geographic or institutional origin;
WHEREAS, c-ECO constitutes the governing methodological authority for all analytical activity conducted under the Fellowship, including but not limited to the pre-threshold principle, systemic compatibility analysis, temporal validity protocols (−Στο), and non-retrospective logic;
WHEREAS, in the event of interpretative conflict, c-ECO methodological principles shall prevail over individual analytical discretion;
WHEREAS, the Fellow seeks to participate in the Fellowship as an Analytical Agent within a Systemic Governance Architecture, contributing to the Program's objectives while adhering to the governance, methodological, and ethical standards established herein;
WHEREAS, the Parties recognize that this Agreement establishes a non-employment relationship governed by specific contractual parameters designed to ensure institutional integrity, methodological coherence, and systemic compatibility;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:
CONTRACTUAL PROVISIONS
[FPA-ART-001] Article I — Nature of Relationship
Section 1.1 Non-Employment Status
The Parties expressly acknowledge and agree that:
(a) No Employment Relationship: This Agreement does not create an employment relationship between the Fellow and the Program, the Foundation, ISM, or any affiliated entity. The Fellow is not an employee, worker, or staff member for any purpose, including but not limited to labor law, tax law, social security, or benefits eligibility.
(b) Independent Participation: The Fellow participates in the Fellowship as an independent analytical contributor, retaining autonomy over their analytical methodology within the constraints of c-ECO compliance and this Agreement.
(c) No Integration: The Fellow shall not be integrated into the operational command structure of the Foundation, ISM, or any partner institution. Participation does not confer membership, partnership, or equity interest in any entity.
Section 1.2 No Agency Authority
The Fellow has no authority to:
(a) Enter into contracts, agreements, or binding commitments on behalf of the Program, Foundation, ISM, or any third party;
(b) Make representations, warranties, or statements that may be construed as official positions of the Program or its affiliated entities;
(c) Incur expenses, obligations, or liabilities chargeable to the Program without prior written authorization.
Section 1.3 No Representation Rights
Unless expressly authorized in writing by the Program Director or designated authorized representative:
(a) The Fellow shall not represent themselves as a spokesperson, official representative, or decision-maker for the Program;
(b) The Fellow shall not use titles, designations, or affiliations that imply employment or institutional authority beyond "c-ECO Fellow" or such other designation as the Program may expressly approve.
[FPA-ART-002] Article II — Status as Analytical Agent
Section 2.1 Definition of Role
The Fellow serves as an Analytical Agent within a Systemic Governance Architecture, defined as a participant who:
(a) Conducts research and analysis within the scope of assigned or approved projects;
(b) Produces structured outputs consistent with c-ECO methodological standards;
(c) Operates under the systemic governance framework established by the Program.
Section 2.2 Non-Decision-Maker Status
The Fellow acknowledges that:
(a) No Decision Authority: The Fellow does not possess decision-making authority regarding Program strategy, institutional partnerships, funding allocations, or operational directives;
(b) Advisory Function: Analytical contributions may inform decision-making processes but do not constitute binding recommendations or directives;
(c) Hierarchical Position: The Fellow operates within a structured analytical hierarchy, with escalation protocols defined in the Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP) and Case-Specific Analytical Mandates (CSAM).
Section 2.3 Institutional Non-Representation
The Fellow's participation does not confer:
(a) Standing to represent the Foundation, ISM, or c-ECO Program in legal, regulatory, or formal institutional proceedings;
(b) Authority to negotiate, approve, or commit to partnerships, collaborations, or data-sharing arrangements;
(c) Capacity to bind the Program to any course of action, analytical conclusion, or policy position.
[FPA-ART-003] Article III — Scope of Participation
Section 3.1 Authorized Activities
Subject to compliance with this Agreement and all attached instruments, the Fellow may engage in:
(a) Research Activities: Systematic investigation, data collection, and analysis within approved project parameters;
(b) Analytical Contributions: Development of analytical frameworks, models, and assessments consistent with c-ECO methodology;
(c) Structured Outputs: Production of reports, briefings, datasets, and other deliverables as specified in relevant CSAMs;
(d) Collaborative Engagement: Participation in working groups, seminars, and knowledge-exchange activities as authorized.
Section 3.2 Project Boundaries
The Fellow shall:
(a) Operate exclusively within the scope of approved projects, cases, or analytical mandates;
(b) Refrain from expanding analytical scope beyond authorized parameters without prior written approval;
(c) Escalate requests for scope expansion through designated Program channels.
Section 3.3 Methodological Adherence
All activities shall comply with:
(a) The Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI), attached hereto as Exhibit A;
(b) Current version of the c-ECO framework (version [4.1] or as subsequently updated);
(c) Temporal validity protocols (−Στο) governing the applicability of analytical conclusions.
[FPA-ART-004] Article IV — Limitation of Authority
Section 4.1 Prohibition on Binding Statements
The Fellow shall not:
(a) Issue statements, conclusions, or recommendations that may be construed as official positions of the Program;
(b) Make public representations regarding Program strategy, partnerships, or operational matters;
(c) Commit the Program to specific analytical outcomes, policy positions, or courses of action.
Section 4.2 Public Positioning Restrictions
The Fellow agrees to the following public positioning protocols:
(a) Prior Approval Required: Any public communication (including publications, presentations, media interviews, or social media posts) referencing the Fellowship, c-ECO methodology, or Program-related work requires prior written approval from the Program Director;
(b) Disclaimer Obligation: Approved public communications must include appropriate disclaimers clarifying that views expressed are those of the Fellow and do not necessarily represent the Program's positions;
(c) Embargo Compliance: The Fellow shall respect any embargo periods or publication restrictions specified by the Program.
Section 4.3 External Engagement Protocols
The Fellow shall adhere to the following external engagement protocols:
(a) Engagement with external stakeholders (governments, institutions, communities, media) shall follow protocols specified in the Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP);
(b) The Fellow shall not enter into separate agreements or understandings with third parties regarding Program-related work without written authorization.
[FPA-ART-005] Article V — Intellectual Contributions
Section 5.1 Integration into c-ECO System
The Parties acknowledge and agree that:
(a) System Integration: All analytical work, research outputs, and intellectual contributions produced during the Fellowship ("Contributions") shall be integrated into the c-ECO system and governed by the Program's data architecture;
(b) Repository Rights: The Program retains the right to store, archive, and utilize Contributions within the c-ECO knowledge management system;
(c) Version Control: Contributions shall be subject to version control and quality assurance protocols established by the Program.
Section 5.2 Attribution Framework
Attribution shall be governed as follows:
(a) Fellow Attribution: The Fellow shall receive appropriate attribution for Contributions as specified in the relevant CSAM or project documentation;
(b) Institutional Attribution: The Program shall be identified as the institutional context for the Fellowship;
(c) Co-Authorship Protocols: Collaborative outputs shall follow co-authorship guidelines established by the Program, with attribution determined by substantive contribution.
Section 5.3 Ownership Structure
Contributions shall be jointly owned by the Fellow and the Program. The Program retains exclusive rights to integrate, adapt, and operationalize Contributions within the c-ECO system and associated institutional frameworks.
The Fellow retains rights to use Contributions for academic, research, and professional purposes, subject to confidentiality obligations under the CDGA and prior notification to the Program for any commercial application.
Section 5.4 Pre-Existing Intellectual Property
The Parties acknowledge that:
(a) The Fellow retains ownership of intellectual property developed prior to the Effective Date ("Pre-Existing IP");
(b) Pre-Existing IP incorporated into Contributions shall remain the Fellow's property, with the Program receiving a license as specified in Section 5.3;
(c) The Fellow shall disclose Pre-Existing IP to the Program prior to integration into Fellowship work.
[FPA-ART-006] Article VI — Compensation and Support
Section 6.1 Default Non-Compensation
Unless otherwise specified:
(a) Unpaid Fellowship: Unless otherwise specified in a CSAM or addendum, the Fellowship is unpaid;
(b) No Benefits: The Fellow is not entitled to employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement contributions, paid leave, or disability coverage;
(c) No Expense Reimbursement: Unless pre-authorized in writing, the Fellow bears all costs associated with Fellowship participation.
Section 6.2 Conditional Support
The Program may, at its sole discretion, provide:
(a) Stipends: Fixed periodic payments to support Fellowship participation, specified in separate written agreements;
(b) Grants: Project-specific funding tied to defined deliverables and milestones;
(c) Operational Support: Access to facilities, equipment, data, or logistical support as specified in relevant protocols;
(d) Travel and Expenses: Reimbursement for pre-approved travel and expenses, subject to Program policies and documentation requirements.
Section 6.3 External Funding Integration
The Fellowship may be supported by external funding sources, including grants, institutional partnerships, or research sponsorships. Participation in such funded activities may be subject to additional terms specified in applicable CSAMs or funding agreements.
Section 6.4 Tax Responsibility
The Fellow acknowledges that:
(a) The Fellow is solely responsible for all tax obligations arising from Fellowship participation;
(b) The Program shall issue appropriate tax documentation (e.g., Form 1099 for U.S. tax purposes) for any payments exceeding applicable thresholds;
(c) The Fellow shall indemnify the Program against any tax liabilities, penalties, or claims arising from the Fellow's failure to comply with tax obligations.
[FPA-ART-007] Article VII — Term and Termination
Section 7.1 Term
The Fellowship term is defined as follows:
(a) Initial Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of [twelve (12) months] unless earlier terminated;
(b) Renewal: The Agreement may be renewed by mutual written agreement of the Parties, subject to continued compliance with all Program requirements;
(c) Project-Specific Terms: Individual CSAMs may specify different terms for specific analytical mandates.
Section 7.2 Termination by Program
The Program may terminate this Agreement:
(a) At Will: Upon [thirty (30) days'] written notice to the Fellow, without cause;
(b) For Cause: Immediately upon written notice if the Fellow:
— Breaches any material provision of this Agreement;
— Violates the Confidentiality and Data Governance Agreement (CDGA);
— Fails to adhere to the Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI);
— Engages in conduct that jeopardizes institutional integrity, reputational interests, or stakeholder relationships;
(c) Automatic Termination: Upon expiration of the Fellowship term if not renewed.
Section 7.3 Termination by Fellow
The Fellow may terminate this Agreement:
(a) Upon [thirty (30) days'] written notice to the Program;
(b) Immediately if the Program materially breaches its obligations and fails to cure such breach within [fifteen (15) days] of written notice.
Section 7.4 Effects of Termination
Upon termination:
(a) The Fellow shall immediately cease all Fellowship activities;
(b) The Fellow shall return all Program materials, data, and property;
(c) Confidentiality obligations under the CDGA shall survive termination;
(d) The Program may retain and utilize Contributions as specified in Article V;
(e) Any outstanding stipends or payments shall be prorated to the termination date.
Section 7.5 Automatic Termination on Breach
Notwithstanding Section 7.2, this Agreement shall terminate automatically, without notice, upon:
(a) Breach of the CDGA involving unauthorized disclosure of Tier 3 (Sensitive) or Tier 4 (Critical) information;
(b) Material deviation from c-ECO methodology as defined in the MAI;
(c) Unauthorized representation of the Program causing reputational harm.
[FPA-ART-008] Article VIII — Governance and Dispute Resolution
Section 8.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, United States of America, without regard to conflict of law principles.
Section 8.2 Arbitration (Primary Mechanism)
Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) in accordance with its Rules.
The seat of arbitration shall be New York, New York.
The language of arbitration shall be English.
Section 8.3 Limited Court Recourse
Nothing in this Article shall prevent either Party from seeking injunctive or equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction where such relief is necessary to prevent immediate and irreparable harm.
Section 8.4 Enforcement
Judgment upon the arbitral award may be entered and enforced in any court of competent jurisdiction in accordance with applicable law and international conventions, including the New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards.
[FPA-ART-009] Article IX — Limitation of Analytical Liability
Section 9.1 Non-Reliance
Analytical outputs produced under the Fellowship are exploratory, research-based, and subject to uncertainty. They shall not be construed as definitive predictions, guarantees, or binding assessments.
Section 9.2 No Liability for Use
The Program shall not be liable for any decisions, actions, or omissions taken by third parties based on Fellowship outputs.
Section 9.3 Systemic Uncertainty
The Fellow acknowledges that c-ECO operates within complex, non-linear systems where uncertainty, incomplete data, and emergent dynamics are inherent.
[FPA-ART-010] Article X — Institutional Independence
Section 10.1 Independence of Framework
The c-ECO framework operates as an independent governance architecture and is not dependent on institutional ownership, endorsement, or formal adoption by any participating entity.
Section 10.2 Non-Attribution of Institutional Positions
Participation in the Fellowship shall not be construed as endorsement, adoption, or institutional commitment by any partner organization, funding body, or affiliated entity.
Section 10.3 Autonomy of Analytical Outputs
Analytical outputs generated under the Fellowship remain part of the c-ECO systemic architecture and shall not be attributed as official positions of any external institution unless expressly agreed in writing.
[FPA-ART-011] Article XI — General Provisions
Section 11.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with the attached instruments (CDGA, MAI, LLEP, and applicable CSAMs), constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding Fellowship participation and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, or representations.
Section 11.2 Amendments
This Agreement may only be amended by written instrument signed by both Parties.
Section 11.3 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
Section 11.4 Waiver
No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No waiver shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or subsequent breach.
Section 11.5 Assignment
(a) The Fellow may not assign this Agreement without prior written consent of the Program;
(b) The Program may assign this Agreement to successor entities or affiliated organizations.
Section 11.6 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified above (or as subsequently updated) via email (with confirmation of receipt) or certified mail.
Section 11.7 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one instrument.
Section 11.8 Electronic Signature
Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as original signatures.

Critical Notice
The Fellow acknowledges that breach of any attached instrument constitutes breach of this Agreement and may result in immediate termination.
