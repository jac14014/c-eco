Fellowship Participation Agreement (FPA)
c-ECO Fellowship Program | Johann Christian Hasse Foundation | Silvio Meira Institute LLC
Binding Contractual Instrument
This Fellowship Participation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation ("Foundation"), acting as institutional steward of the c-ECO framework, and Silvio Meira Institute LLC ("ISM"), acting as operational and implementation partner, collectively the "Program Administrators"; and the individual Fellow identified below.
This Agreement establishes a non-employment relationship governed by New York law and creates binding legal obligations regarding participation in the c-ECO Fellowship Program. Execution of this Agreement is required for Fellowship entry.
PARTIES AND PREAMBLE
[FPA-PARTIES-001] Parties
THE JOHANN CHRISTIAN HASSE FOUNDATION, a non-profit organization organized and existing under the laws of [Jurisdiction], with its principal offices at 2504 Grandview Circle. Columbia, MO. 65203 United Stated Of America ("Foundation"), acting as institutional steward of the c-ECO framework; and
SILVIO MEIRA INSTITUTE LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of Delaware, with its principal offices at 4215 Lindy Circle. Orlando, Fl. 32827 United States Of America ("ISM"), acting as operational and implementation partner for applied studies and project execution;
The Foundation and ISM are collectively referred to as the "Program Administrators" (individually or collectively, "Program");
and [FELLOW NAME], an individual residing at [Address] ("Fellow").
The Program Administrators and Fellow may be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."
[FPA-PREAMBLE-001] Preamble
WHEREAS, the Program operates the c-ECO Fellowship Program ("Fellowship"), a structured analytical initiative designed to operationalize the c-ECO governance architecture through collaborative research and systemic analysis;
WHEREAS, the Fellowship operates as a globally accessible program designed to identify and engage high-capacity analytical talent independent of geographic or institutional origin;
WHEREAS, c-ECO constitutes the governing methodological authority for all analytical activity conducted under the Fellowship, including but not limited to the pre-threshold principle, systemic compatibility analysis, temporal validity protocols (−Στο), and non-retrospective logic;
WHEREAS, in the event of interpretative conflict, c-ECO methodological principles shall prevail over individual analytical discretion;
WHEREAS, the Fellow seeks to participate in the Fellowship as an Analytical Agent within a Systemic Governance Architecture, contributing to the Program's objectives while adhering to the governance, methodological, and ethical standards established herein;
WHEREAS, the Parties recognize that this Agreement establishes a non-employment relationship governed by specific contractual parameters designed to ensure institutional integrity, methodological coherence, and systemic compatibility;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:
CONTRACTUAL PROVISIONS
[FPA-ART-001] Article I — Nature of Relationship
Section 1.1 Non-Employment Status
The Parties expressly acknowledge and agree that:
(a) No Employment Relationship: This Agreement does not create an employment relationship between the Fellow and the Program, the Foundation, ISM, or any affiliated entity. The Fellow is not an employee, worker, or staff member for any purpose, including but not limited to labor law, tax law, social security, or benefits eligibility.
(b) Independent Participation: The Fellow participates in the Fellowship as an independent analytical contributor, retaining autonomy over their analytical methodology within the constraints of c-ECO compliance and this Agreement.
(c) No Integration: The Fellow shall not be integrated into the operational command structure of the Foundation, ISM, or any partner institution. Participation does not confer membership, partnership, or equity interest in any entity.
Section 1.2 No Agency Authority
The Fellow has no authority to:
(a) Enter into contracts, agreements, or binding commitments on behalf of the Program, Foundation, ISM, or any third party;
(b) Make representations, warranties, or statements that may be construed as official positions of the Program or its affiliated entities;
(c) Incur expenses, obligations, or liabilities chargeable to the Program without prior written authorization.
Section 1.3 No Representation Rights
Unless expressly authorized in writing by the Program Director or designated authorized representative:
(a) The Fellow shall not represent themselves as a spokesperson, official representative, or decision-maker for the Program;
(b) The Fellow shall not use titles, designations, or affiliations that imply employment or institutional authority beyond "c-ECO Fellow" or such other designation as the Program may expressly approve.
[FPA-ART-002] Article II — Status as Analytical Agent
Section 2.1 Definition of Role
The Fellow serves as an Analytical Agent within a Systemic Governance Architecture, defined as a participant who:
(a) Conducts research and analysis within the scope of assigned or approved projects;
(b) Produces structured outputs consistent with c-ECO methodological standards;
(c) Operates under the systemic governance framework established by the Program.
Section 2.2 Non-Decision-Maker Status
The Fellow acknowledges that:
(a) No Decision Authority: The Fellow does not possess decision-making authority regarding Program strategy, institutional partnerships, funding allocations, or operational directives;
(b) Advisory Function: Analytical contributions may inform decision-making processes but do not constitute binding recommendations or directives;
(c) Hierarchical Position: The Fellow operates within a structured analytical hierarchy, with escalation protocols defined in the Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP) and Case-Specific Analytical Mandates (CSAM).
Section 2.3 Institutional Non-Representation
The Fellow's participation does not confer:
(a) Standing to represent the Foundation, ISM, or c-ECO Program in legal, regulatory, or formal institutional proceedings;
(b) Authority to negotiate, approve, or commit to partnerships, collaborations, or data-sharing arrangements;
(c) Capacity to bind the Program to any course of action, analytical conclusion, or policy position.
[FPA-ART-003] Article III — Scope of Participation
Section 3.1 Nature and Scope of Authorized Participation
Subject to compliance with this Agreement and all attached instruments, the Fellow is authorized to participate in the c-ECO Fellowship Program exclusively within the scope of the controlled analytical environment established by the Program. Fellowship admission grants access to the c-ECO methodological system, supervised analytical structures, and authorized sectoral materials, but does not, by itself, authorize independent operational, contractual, regulatory, or institutional use of the c-ECO framework in real-world environments.
(a) Admission Layer: The Fellow may enter and operate within the Fellowship as an authorized participant in the c-ECO analytical environment, subject to institutional supervision, methodological fidelity, and contractual adherence;
(b) Recognition Layer: The Fellow may obtain institutional recognition for validated function performed within an approved case environment, subject to the applicable scope, standing, and certification conditions;
(c) License Distinction: The Fellow acknowledges that real-world operational use of c-ECO may require a separate institutional license and that no such authorization shall be presumed from admission, participation, or recognition alone;
(d) No Automatic Implementation Rights: Participation in the Fellowship does not create any automatic right to apply, deploy, commercialize, operationalize, or represent c-ECO in external environments without explicit written authorization where applicable.
Section 3.2 Authorized Activities
Within the limits established by this Agreement, the attached instruments, and the applicable case environment, the Fellow may engage in the following activities:
(a) Research Activities: Systematic investigation, data collection, case reading, signal review, and analysis within approved project or case parameters;
(b) Analytical Contributions: Development of analytical frameworks, TFP-based interpretations, models, matrices, assessments, and systemic readings consistent with c-ECO methodology;
(c) Structured Outputs: Production of reports, memoranda, briefings, logs, datasets, matrices, and other deliverables as specified in relevant CSAMs or authorized Fellowship activities;
(d) Collaborative Engagement: Participation in working groups, seminars, supervised exchanges, methodological discussions, and knowledge-sharing activities authorized by the Program;
(e) Case Structuring Support: Preparation or development of case-related materials, including case summaries, signal architectures, boundary definitions, and analytical inputs required for supervised c-ECO application.
Section 3.3 Project and Case Boundaries
The Fellow shall operate exclusively within the boundaries defined by the Program and, where applicable, the relevant Case-Specific Analytical Mandate.
(a) The Fellow shall operate only within approved projects, cases, sectoral environments, or analytical mandates;
(b) The Fellow shall refrain from expanding analytical scope beyond authorized parameters without prior written approval;
(c) Any request for scope expansion, cross-case use, additional sectoral access, or broader interpretive authority shall be escalated through designated Program channels;
(d) No silence in a CSAM, approval email, or Program communication shall be interpreted as authorization to broaden the Fellow’s scope of action.
Section 3.4 Methodological Adherence
All activities conducted by the Fellow under the Fellowship shall comply with the binding c-ECO methodological architecture.
(a) The Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI), attached hereto as Exhibit A;
(b) The current certified version of the c-ECO framework (version [4.1] or as subsequently updated);
(c) Temporal validity protocols (−Στο) governing the applicability, durability, and conditional relevance of analytical conclusions;
(d) Any applicable technical, sectoral, or procedural guidance formally adopted by the Program;
(e) The prohibition of unauthorized methodological deviation, selective reinterpretation, or extra-systemic application incompatible with c-ECO principles.
Section 3.5 Effect-Based Participation and Non-Entitlement
The Fellow acknowledges that the c-ECO system does not compensate activity, attendance, or output production as such. Any possible participation in economic value arising from the system is strictly conditioned upon validated systemic effects within licensed case environments and remains subject to separate institutional determination.
(a) No entitlement to compensation, allocation, or participation arises from the mere production of reports, calculations, CSAMs, or other analytical materials;
(b) Where applicable, discretionary participation linked to validated systemic effect shall be governed by the applicable effect-based framework adopted by the Program;
(c) The Fellow shall not structure, distort, exaggerate, or externalize analytical work in anticipation of possible economic participation;
(d) Nothing in this Agreement shall be interpreted as creating a commission structure, brokerage role, referral entitlement, or commercial intermediation relationship.
[FPA-ART-004] Article IV — Limitation of Authority
Section 4.1 General Limitation of Authority
The Fellow participates in the c-ECO Fellowship Program as an authorized analytical participant within a controlled systemic governance environment. The Fellow does not, by virtue of this Agreement, possess authority to bind, represent, implement on behalf of, or externally operationalize the Program, the Foundation, ISM, or the c-ECO framework except as expressly authorized in writing.
(a) The Fellow shall not issue statements, conclusions, recommendations, or declarations that may be construed as official positions of the Program or the Foundation;
(b) The Fellow shall not commit the Program, the Foundation, ISM, or any affiliated entity to specific analytical outcomes, policy positions, strategic directions, or operational courses of action;
(c) The Fellow shall not represent themselves as holding regulatory, supervisory, enforcement, licensing, or decision-making authority within the c-ECO system unless expressly authorized in writing for a defined purpose.
Section 4.2 Admission Does Not Create License Authority
Fellowship admission and recognition are distinct from licensed real-world authorization.
(a) The Fellow shall not represent Fellowship admission as equivalent to authorization for independent real-world use of the c-ECO framework;
(b) The Fellow shall not claim that a CSAM, analytical output, certificate, or standing determination by itself authorizes operational implementation of c-ECO in contracts, institutions, projects, or regulatory environments;
(c) Where license activation is required, only the competent institutional authority may authorize such use.
Section 4.3 Public Positioning Restrictions
The Fellow agrees to the following public positioning protocols:
(a) Prior Approval Required: Any public communication, including publications, presentations, interviews, conference statements, institutional briefs, media interactions, or social media posts referencing the Fellowship, c-ECO methodology, or Program-related work requires prior written approval from the Program Director or designated authority;
(b) Disclaimer Obligation: Approved public communications must include appropriate disclaimers clarifying that views expressed are those of the Fellow and do not necessarily represent the official position of the Program, the Foundation, or any partner institution;
(c) Embargo Compliance: The Fellow shall comply with any embargo periods, confidentiality restrictions, publication sequencing, or non-disclosure limitations specified by the Program or any applicable instrument;
(d) No False Signaling: The Fellow shall not imply institutional endorsement, certified adoption, licensed application, or external implementation status where such status has not been formally granted.
Section 4.4 External Engagement Protocols
All engagement with external parties shall remain bounded by the Fellow’s authorized analytical role and by the applicable contractual instruments.
(a) Engagement with external stakeholders, including governments, institutions, communities, operators, private entities, funders, media, or partner organizations, shall follow the protocols specified in the Living Lab Engagement Protocol (LLEP) and any applicable CSAM;
(b) The Fellow shall not enter into separate agreements, side understandings, commitments, or representations with third parties regarding Program-related work without prior written authorization;
(c) The Fellow shall not negotiate, offer, or imply licensing terms, commercial conditions, implementation arrangements, or institutional commitments on behalf of the Foundation or the Program unless expressly authorized in writing.
Section 4.5 No Agency, Brokerage, or Commercial Intermediation
Nothing in this Agreement creates or shall be interpreted as creating an agency, brokerage, commission, referral, franchise, commercial representation, or partnership relationship between the Fellow and the Program or the Foundation.
(a) The Fellow shall not act or present themselves as an agent, broker, intermediary, salesperson, originator, or commercial representative of the Foundation or the c-ECO system;
(b) The Fellow shall not solicit, originate, or structure external cases under the representation that they may bind the Foundation or secure c-ECO licensing outcomes;
(c) Any possible participation linked to validated systemic effects shall not convert the Fellow into an intermediary, commission-based actor, or authorized representative of the Foundation.
Section 4.6 Non-Interference and Operational Limitation
The Fellow remains bound by the Non-Interference Principle and shall not become an operative variable in the system under observation except where a separate and explicit authorization has been issued.
(a) The Fellow shall not implement interventions, instruct case subjects, or exercise operational functions in real-world environments solely by virtue of Fellowship participation;
(b) The Fellow shall not provide operational, legal, financial, regulatory, or institutional advice as if acting on behalf of the Program, unless specifically authorized under a separate mandate;
(c) Where a licensed operational role exists, its scope shall be strictly limited to the terms of the applicable institutional authorization.
[FPA-ART-005] Article V — Intellectual Contributions
Section 5.1 Integration into c-ECO System
The Parties acknowledge and agree that:
(a) System Integration: All analytical work, research outputs, and intellectual contributions produced during the Fellowship ("Contributions") shall be integrated into the c-ECO system and governed by the Program's data architecture;
(b) Repository Rights: The Program retains the right to store, archive, and utilize Contributions within the c-ECO knowledge management system;
(c) Version Control: Contributions shall be subject to version control and quality assurance protocols established by the Program.
Section 5.2 Attribution Framework
Attribution shall be governed as follows:
(a) Fellow Attribution: The Fellow shall receive appropriate attribution for Contributions as specified in the relevant CSAM or project documentation;
(b) Institutional Attribution: The Program shall be identified as the institutional context for the Fellowship;
(c) Co-Authorship Protocols: Collaborative outputs shall follow co-authorship guidelines established by the Program, with attribution determined by substantive contribution.
Section 5.3 Ownership Structure
Contributions shall be jointly owned by the Fellow and the Program. The Program retains exclusive rights to integrate, adapt, and operationalize Contributions within the c-ECO system and associated institutional frameworks.
The Fellow retains rights to use Contributions for academic, research, and professional purposes, subject to confidentiality obligations under the CDGA and prior notification to the Program for any commercial application.
Section 5.4 Pre-Existing Intellectual Property
The Parties acknowledge that:
(a) The Fellow retains ownership of intellectual property developed prior to the Effective Date ("Pre-Existing IP");
(b) Pre-Existing IP incorporated into Contributions shall remain the Fellow's property, with the Program receiving a license as specified in Section 5.3;
(c) The Fellow shall disclose Pre-Existing IP to the Program prior to integration into Fellowship work.
[FPA-ART-006] Article VI — Admission, Support, and Economic Conditions
Section 6.1 Admission Structure
The c-ECO Fellowship Program does not operate under a single compensation model. Participation may occur through paid admission, scholarship-supported admission, invitation-based participation, institutional sponsorship, or other formally authorized support structures. Admission to the Fellowship grants access to the c-ECO methodological environment and supervised analytical structures, but does not by itself create any right to compensation, reimbursement, or economic participation.
(a) Paid Admission: The Fellow may be admitted under a standard paid participation structure, as defined by the applicable Program cycle, cohort, or institutional conditions;
(b) Scholarship-Supported Admission: The Fellow may be admitted under a full or partial scholarship allocation granted by the Program in designated cycles, regions, or strategic contexts;
(c) Invitation-Based Participation: The Program may, at its sole discretion, invite strategically relevant professionals to participate under fully sponsored, partially sponsored, or otherwise supported conditions;
(d) Institutional Sponsorship: The Fellow’s participation may be funded or supported by an institution, organization, or partner entity under terms accepted by the Program;
(e) No Presumption of Economic Entitlement: No admission structure shall be construed as creating employee status, guaranteed compensation, guaranteed reimbursement, or any automatic right to future financial participation.
Section 6.2 Recognition Cycle and Renewal Economics
Unless otherwise expressly specified for pilot, sensitive, or exceptional contexts, recognition issued under the Fellowship is valid for twenty-four (24) months from the date of issue and remains subject to renewal under current methodological, contractual, and institutional standards.
(a) Renewal Requirement: Continued standing beyond the initial recognition cycle depends on successful renewal and revalidation under current c-ECO conditions;
(b) Renewal Fees: Renewal may be subject to applicable administrative, methodological, or participation fees determined by the Program;
(c) Scholarship Renewal Waiver: Fellows admitted under scholarship-supported participation must apply separately for any renewal fee waiver; such waiver is not automatic and remains subject to institutional review;
(d) No Continuing Entitlement: Approval of scholarship-supported admission in the initial cycle does not create any right or presumption of continued fee waiver, funding, or renewal support.
Section 6.3 Support and Institutional Assistance
The Program may, at its sole discretion and subject to available capacity, provide limited forms of support connected to Fellowship participation.
(a) Stipends: Fixed or periodic support payments, where expressly approved in separate written instruments;
(b) Grants: Project-specific funding tied to defined deliverables, milestones, or approved applied activities;
(c) Operational Support: Access to facilities, equipment, software, restricted materials, data, logistics, or institutional interfaces as authorized by the Program;
(d) Travel and Expenses: Reimbursement of pre-approved travel, accommodation, field, or related costs, subject to Program rules and documentation requirements;
(e) Technical or Supervisory Support: Access to mentorship, methodological oversight, technical review, or case supervision as permitted under the Fellowship structure.
Section 6.4 External Funding and Sponsored Conditions
The Fellowship may be supported by external funding sources, including grants, institutional partnerships, philanthropic allocations, public-interest support, or research sponsorships. Participation in such funded or sponsored activities may be subject to additional terms specified in applicable CSAMs, addenda, or funding-related instruments.
(a) External support does not alter the non-employment character of the Fellowship;
(b) Sponsored participation does not grant the sponsoring party authority over methodological standards, certification outcomes, or Foundation determinations;
(c) The Program may impose additional conditions to preserve independence, confidentiality, and institutional integrity where external funding is involved.
Section 6.5 License-Dependent Real-World Use
Fellowship admission, recognition, and support are distinct from licensed use of the c-ECO framework in real-world operational, contractual, regulatory, or institutional environments. Where such use is intended, separate licensing conditions may apply.
(a) Admission fees or scholarship support do not include unrestricted operational application rights;
(b) License terms, where applicable, shall be determined separately according to case-specific parameters;
(c) No Fellow may assume that participation in the Fellowship includes the right to deploy c-ECO externally without express authorization where required.
Section 6.6 Outcome-Conditioned Analytical Participation
The c-ECO system does not compensate activity, attendance, deliverable production, or case involvement as such. Where a licensed c-ECO application produces a validated systemic effect, discretionary economic participation may be considered under the applicable institutional framework governing outcome-conditioned analytical participation.
(a) No automatic entitlement arises from the preparation of reports, calculations, matrices, CSAMs, or other analytical outputs;
(b) Any possible participation depends on: (i) a licensed case environment where required, (ii) validated contribution, and (iii) institutional certification of systemic effect;
(c) Such participation, if any, shall remain discretionary, conditional, and governed by the applicable effect-based framework adopted by the Program;
(d) Nothing in this Agreement creates a commission structure, sales entitlement, referral fee right, brokerage relationship, or commercial intermediation role.
Section 6.7 No Employment Benefits
Regardless of admission type, support status, or any discretionary economic participation, the Fellow is not entitled to employee benefits.
(a) The Fellow is not entitled to health insurance, retirement contributions, paid leave, disability coverage, unemployment protection, severance, or any other employee benefit;
(b) No support, stipend, grant, or effect-based participation shall be construed as creating an employment relationship or benefit entitlement.
Section 6.8 Tax Responsibility
The Fellow acknowledges and agrees that all tax consequences associated with participation, support, or any discretionary economic allocation remain the Fellow’s responsibility except to the extent expressly required by applicable law.
(a) The Fellow is solely responsible for all tax obligations arising from Fellowship admission, support received, stipends, grants, reimbursements, or any effect-based participation;
(b) The Program may issue appropriate tax documentation where required by applicable law or reporting thresholds;
(c) The Fellow shall indemnify the Program against tax liabilities, penalties, claims, or losses arising from the Fellow’s failure to comply with applicable tax obligations, to the extent permitted by law.
[FPA-ART-007] Article VII — Term and Termination
Section 7.1 Term
The Fellowship term is defined as follows:
(a) Initial Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of [twelve (12) months] unless earlier terminated;
(b) Renewal: The Agreement may be renewed by mutual written agreement of the Parties, subject to continued compliance with all Program requirements;
(c) Project-Specific Terms: Individual CSAMs may specify different terms for specific analytical mandates.
Section 7.2 Termination by Program
The Program may terminate this Agreement:
(a) At Will: Upon [thirty (30) days'] written notice to the Fellow, without cause;
(b) For Cause: Immediately upon written notice if the Fellow:
— Breaches any material provision of this Agreement;
— Violates the Confidentiality and Data Governance Agreement (CDGA);
— Fails to adhere to the Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI);
— Engages in conduct that jeopardizes institutional integrity, reputational interests, or stakeholder relationships;
(c) Automatic Termination: Upon expiration of the Fellowship term if not renewed.
Section 7.3 Termination by Fellow
The Fellow may terminate this Agreement:
(a) Upon [thirty (30) days'] written notice to the Program;
(b) Immediately if the Program materially breaches its obligations and fails to cure such breach within [fifteen (15) days] of written notice.
Section 7.4 Effects of Termination
Upon termination:
(a) The Fellow shall immediately cease all Fellowship activities;
(b) The Fellow shall return all Program materials, data, and property;
(c) Confidentiality obligations under the CDGA shall survive termination;
(d) The Program may retain and utilize Contributions as specified in Article V;
(e) Any outstanding stipends or payments shall be prorated to the termination date.
Section 7.5 Automatic Termination on Breach
Notwithstanding Section 7.2, this Agreement shall terminate automatically, without notice, upon:
(a) Breach of the CDGA involving unauthorized disclosure of Tier 3 (Sensitive) or Tier 4 (Critical) information;
(b) Material deviation from c-ECO methodology as defined in the MAI;
(c) Unauthorized representation of the Program causing reputational harm.
[FPA-ART-008] Article VIII — Governance and Dispute Resolution
Section 8.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, United States of America, without regard to conflict of law principles.
Section 8.2 Arbitration (Primary Mechanism)
Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) in accordance with its Rules.
The seat of arbitration shall be New York, New York.
The language of arbitration shall be English.
Section 8.3 Limited Court Recourse
Nothing in this Article shall prevent either Party from seeking injunctive or equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction where such relief is necessary to prevent immediate and irreparable harm.
Section 8.4 Enforcement
Judgment upon the arbitral award may be entered and enforced in any court of competent jurisdiction in accordance with applicable law and international conventions, including the New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards.
[FPA-ART-009] Article IX — Limitation of Analytical Liability
Section 9.1 Non-Reliance
Analytical outputs produced under the Fellowship are exploratory, research-based, and subject to uncertainty. They shall not be construed as definitive predictions, guarantees, or binding assessments.
Section 9.2 No Liability for Use
The Program shall not be liable for any decisions, actions, or omissions taken by third parties based on Fellowship outputs.
Section 9.3 Systemic Uncertainty
The Fellow acknowledges that c-ECO operates within complex, non-linear systems where uncertainty, incomplete data, and emergent dynamics are inherent.
[FPA-ART-010] Article X — Institutional Independence
Section 10.1 Independence of Framework
The c-ECO framework operates as an independent governance architecture and is not dependent on institutional ownership, endorsement, or formal adoption by any participating entity.
Section 10.2 Non-Attribution of Institutional Positions
Participation in the Fellowship shall not be construed as endorsement, adoption, or institutional commitment by any partner organization, funding body, or affiliated entity.
Section 10.3 Autonomy of Analytical Outputs
Analytical outputs generated under the Fellowship remain part of the c-ECO systemic architecture and shall not be attributed as official positions of any external institution unless expressly agreed in writing.
[FPA-ART-011] Article XI — General Provisions
Section 11.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, together with the attached instruments (CDGA, MAI, LLEP, and applicable CSAMs), constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding Fellowship participation and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, or representations.
Section 11.2 Amendments
This Agreement may only be amended by written instrument signed by both Parties.
Section 11.3 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
Section 11.4 Waiver
No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No waiver shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or subsequent breach.
Section 11.5 Assignment
(a) The Fellow may not assign this Agreement without prior written consent of the Program;
(b) The Program may assign this Agreement to successor entities or affiliated organizations.
Section 11.6 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified above (or as subsequently updated) via email (with confirmation of receipt) or certified mail.
Section 11.7 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one instrument.
Section 11.8 Electronic Signature
Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as original signatures.
