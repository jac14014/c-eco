[STATUTE-V1]

Systemic Governance Statute
c-ΣCO Regime

Preamble

This International Statute institutes a transnational legal regime founded upon predictive governance and biophysical supremacy...

Preliminary Chapter: Structural Principles

[CECO-ART-001] Article 1
The validity, enforceability, and continuity of any acts, contracts, or operations subject to the c-ΣCO regime are subordinated to the preservation of the Safe Operating Space (SOS).
§ 1º No legal effect may be recognized for acts or arrangements whose execution presupposes or entails the violation of such limits.
[CECO-ART-002] Article 2
Where material technical uncertainty exists regarding risk trajectories, legal decision-making shall prioritize the protection of the system.
§ 1º The absence of absolute technical certainty shall not preclude immediate suspension.
§ 2º Thresholds shall be established by the Technical Standards Committee.
[CECO-ART-005] Article 5
Governance under the c-ΣCO regime operates primarily ex ante, acting upon risk trajectories.
§ 1º Mandatory Preventive Preclusion operates based on the following scale:
I — Systemic-Existential Risk (10–20% Trigger);
II — Climate and Macro-Financial Risk (50% Trigger);
III — Operational and Compliance Risk (80% Trigger).

Chapter II: Operating Regime

[CECO-ART-006] Article 6
Governance is determined by Functional Integrity within the Earth System.
