I

Manual Identity and Legal Nature

This Operational Manual constitutes an integral component of the c-ECO Systemic Governance framework established by Model Law Art. 215-232. Its purpose is to translate the normative architecture established by the c-ECO Statute and the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP) into concrete, auditable, and operational procedures capable of governing economic activity under conditions of systemic, non-linear, and potentially irreversible risk.

Prudential Function Art. 215

Specifies how legal binding pre-threshold governance duties are operationalized in practice, through standardized procedures that reduce discretion, mitigate strategic behavior, and preserve epistemic integrity of data and methodologies. Compliance is condition of prudential validity.

Technical-Normative Infrastructure Art. 224

Incorporated by reference into contracts, licenses, and institutional adherence regimes, binding to the extent expressly recognized by the c-ECO Statute and TFP. Not purely regulatory nor merely informational — an intermediate prudential instrument analogous to financial buffers.

Institutional Legitimacy Sec. 1.3

Provides the due process layer necessary for the c-ECO framework to function credibly within financial markets, regulatory environments, and arbitration fora — the connective tissue between scientific signals, legal effects, and institutional accountability under Hasse Foundation oversight.

II

Structuring Principles

Material Primacy of the Earth System

ML Art. 1

Legal, economic, and institutional arrangements are ultimately constrained by biophysical realities that are non-negotiable and, in certain conditions, irreversible. Legal validity and contractual performance cannot be meaningfully assessed in abstraction from these material limits. The Manual operationalizes this primacy by requiring all procedures to detect and respond to material risk before irreversible thresholds are crossed.

Pre-Threshold Governance

ML Art. 5

Traditional legal frameworks rely predominantly on ex post mechanisms: liability, remediation, and compensation after harm. The c-ECO framework shifts the locus of legal relevance upstream, to the zone where intervention remains possible and effective. The Manual embeds continuous monitoring, early warning signals, and automatic prudential responses into routine governance.

Prudential Asymmetry of Uncertainty (Σ)

ML Art. 2; Sec. 4.5

Uncertainty is an inherent feature of complex systems. The principle of asymmetrical uncertainty holds that uncertainty must never be used to justify inaction where potential consequences include irreversible harm. Margins of error contract operational space rather than expand it; confidence intervals are applied in the direction of risk; worst-case plausible scenarios are privileged.

Restoration First

ML Art. 7, 112-120

When certified risk of irreversibility emerges, preservation of systemic reversibility takes precedence over financial performance, contractual expectations, or creditor interests. Procedures for liquidity, guarantees, intervention, and execution ensure resources are available, mobilizable, and legally protected for restoration when needed. Level 4 (Black) activation per Model Law Art. 137-138.

III

TFP Core Variables

The TFP is structured around fundamental variables defined in Model Law Art. 217 capturing different dimensions of systemic risk. The Manual defines standardized procedures for measuring, certifying, and combining these variables, ensuring consistency across sectors and jurisdictions:

P
POSITION Art. 217 §1.I

Measures proximity to relevant biophysical or systemic limits. Not a generic environmental indicator — a boundary-referenced, context-locked metric whose meaning is defined by sectoral SPS specification and calibration parameters. Legal function: imposes non-negotiable material constraint upon contractual execution. Collection per Sec. 4; Calibration Council per Sec. 17.

ΔV
VELOCITY Art. 217 §1.II

Captures the temporal dimension of change — direction and rate at which the system moves toward or away from limits. Functions as the primary anticipatory signal: systems far from threshold but accelerating rapidly may present higher danger than systems closer but stabilizing. Calculation per Sec. 7; TRS derivation per Sec. 8.

σ
UNCERTAINTY Art. 217 §1.III

Modulates all other variables, ensuring prudential conservatism. Applied asymmetrically: where P approaches boundary, σ reduces distance to limit; where data suggests safety, σ erodes that safety margin. Never justifies delay or optimism — always contracts operational space. Treatment per Sec. 4.5; Prudential Asymmetry per Art. 218.

Lr
REVERSIBILITY LIQUIDITY Art. 217 §1.IV

Assesses availability and mobilizability of resources required to reverse or contain harmful trajectories. Ratio between Resources Mobilizable Immediately (Rmi) and Projected Technical Cost of Containment (Ct). Lr ≥ 1.0 indicates sufficient capacity; Lr < 0.5 triggers Restoration First (Model Law Art. 137-138). Calculation per Sec. 9.

Trigger Function (Art. 217): Γ = f(P, ΔV, σ, Lr) → Output: Normalized prudential score (0-100) → Classification: Green/Amber/Red/Black Band → Legal Effects: IEX, Safe Mode, Restoration First (Model Law Art. 15-22, 44-47)

IV

Operational Scores

From TFP variables, operational scores are derived enabling classification, comparison, and trigger activation per Model Law Art. 217-219. The Manual specifies how these scores are calculated, audited, disclosed, and used to produce legally binding effects:

SPS

Safe Operating Space Proximity Score

Measures current distance between asset/operation and applicable SOS boundary. Scale 0-100: 80-100 (safe margin), 60-79 (attention zone), 40-59 (material risk), <40 (critical proximity). Derived exclusively from certified data, not managerial discretion. SOS boundaries defined by Calibration Council per Sec. 17; legal effect per Model Law Art. 23-26.

TRS

Trajectory Risk Score

Measures direction, speed, and persistence of movement toward material limits. Scale 0-100: 80-100 (stabilized/improving), 60-79 (slow deterioration), 40-59 (accelerating deterioration), <40 (predictable collapse trajectory). Framework does not wait for limits to be crossed. TRS < 60 triggers Safe Mode (Red Band) per Art. 219 §1.III; TRS < 40 triggers Restoration First (Black Band) per Art. 219 §1.IV.

RLS

Reversibility Liquidity Score (RLS/Lr)

Measures immediate financial capacity to contain, mitigate, restore, or safely decommission. Lr ≥ 1.2 (fully financeable), 0.8-1.19 (limited margin), 0.5-0.79 (relevant insufficiency), <0.5 (reversibility insolvency). Operation may be financially solvent yet ecologically insolvent. RLS < 0.8 triggers Safe Mode; RLS < 0.5 triggers Restoration First per Art. 219.

V

Data Verification & Certification (DVB)

Data Custodian Certification

Sec. 5-6; ML Art. 71

Data Custodians operate as epistemic fiduciaries — their sole function is preserving signal chain integrity from physical reality to juridical fact. Certification requires: technical qualifications (Earth system science, environmental engineering, remote sensing), operational experience, doctrinal competence (pre-threshold logic, asymmetric uncertainty), and integrity/independence. Certification by Hasse Foundation per Sec. 5.3; revocation per Sec. 5.4.3.

Chain of Custody & Immutability

Sec. 4.4; ML Art. 71

Mandatory procedures: continuous measurement (not episodic sampling), chain-of-custody constraints (sensor → extraction → attestation → hashing → ledger), minimum metadata completeness for P reconstruction, and procedural triggers converting integrity deficits into escalation events. Produces "Systemic Proof" per Model Law Art. 35-39.

QA/QC and Asymmetric Uncertainty Treatment

Sec. 4.5; ML Art. 2, 218

Quality Assurance/Quality Control determines data admissibility for P, ΔV calculations, or triggers conservative adjustment. Strict distinction between measurement error (governed statistically) and procedural failure (escalatory, non-compensable). Uncertainty always contracts operational margin — never expands it. Prudential Asymmetry per Model Law Art. 218.

VI

Prudential Levels & Trigger Catalogue

The Trigger Catalogue defines conditions for prudential band activation and automatic effects per Model Law Art. 219. Embodies graduated, proportional, non-discretionary response logic. The Manual ensures trigger activation is procedurally sound, transparently recorded, and resistant to manipulation:

GREEN

Watch

SPS 80-100, TRS stable, Lr ≥ 1.0

Enhanced monitoring, no execution restrictions

ML Art. 219 §1.I

AMBER

Capital Protection

TRS 60-79 or SPS 60-79 or Lr 0.8-1.19

Buffer reinforcement, partial cash flow redirection

ML Art. 219 §1.II

RED

Safe Mode

TRS < 60 or SPS < 40 or Lr < 0.8

Execution reconfiguration, NOT default

ML Art. 44-47; Art. 219 §1.III

BLACK

Restoration First

TRS < 40 or Lr < 0.5 or point-of-no-return

External intervention, automatic asset conversion

ML Art. 137-138; Art. 219 §1.IV

Master Rule (Art. 219 §3): No trigger creates new obligations. It activates obligations already contractually pre-agreed. Safe Mode ≠ default (Art. 219 §4). Restoration First ≠ expropriation (Art. 220). All effects are ex ante, non-discretionary, and scientifically justified.

VII

Auditability, Transparency & Due Process

Continuous QA/QC and Technical Audits

Sec. 11; ML Art. 227

All data flows, calculations, classifications, and trigger activations subject to continuous Quality Assurance/Quality Control. Automatically intensified upon: procedural deviation detection, statistically significant anomalies, or activation of Level 2 (Amber) or higher. Audits are strictly technical — verify protocol application, not policy merit. Independent audit per Model Law Art. 227.

Immutable Public Ledger

Sec. 13; ML Art. 71

All prudential indicators and classifications recorded in secure, immutable ledger preserving full data lineage: certified raw inputs, applied methodologies, uncertainty margins, resulting scores, triggered effects, timestamps, responsible custodians. Public access proportionate to systemic relevance. "Immutable Technical Record" per Model Law Art. 71.

Execution During Review

Sec. 15; ML Art. 81, 222

Framework adopts execution during review principle — where prudential triggers are activated, effects remain operative throughout any audit, review, or arbitral process. Due process preserved without granting suspensive effect. Arbitral review limited to: procedural failure, evidentiary integrity, fraud, material measurement error. Technical Curatorship Chamber per Model Law Art. 81; Anti-forum shopping per Art. 222.
