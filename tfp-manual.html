TFP Manual

Threshold Function Protocol Operational Manual — translating the normative architecture of the c-ECO Statute and TFP Blueprint into concrete, auditable, and operational procedures. Specifies how legal binding pre-threshold governance duties are operationalized through standardized procedures for data collection, certification, monitoring, audit, intervention, and restoration.

I

Manual Identity and Legal Nature

This Operational Manual constitutes an integral component of the c-ECO Systemic Governance framework. Its purpose is to translate the normative architecture established by the c-ECO Statute and the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP) into concrete, auditable, and operational procedures capable of governing economic activity under conditions of systemic, non-linear, and potentially irreversible risk.

Prudential Function

Specifies how legal binding pre-threshold governance duties are operationalized in practice, through standardized procedures that reduce discretion, mitigate strategic behavior, and preserve epistemic integrity of data and methodologies.

Technical-Normative Infrastructure

Incorporated by reference into contracts, licenses, and institutional adherence regimes, binding to the extent expressly recognized by the c-ECO Statute and TFP. Not purely regulatory nor merely informational — an intermediate prudential instrument.

Institutional Legitimacy

Provides the due process layer necessary for the c-ECO framework to function credibly within financial markets, regulatory environments, and arbitration fora — the connective tissue between scientific signals, legal effects, and institutional accountability.

II

Structuring Principles

Material Primacy of the Earth System

Legal, economic, and institutional arrangements are ultimately constrained by biophysical realities that are non-negotiable and, in certain conditions, irreversible. Legal validity and contractual performance cannot be meaningfully assessed in abstraction from these material limits. The Manual operationalizes this primacy by requiring all procedures to detect and respond to material risk before irreversible thresholds are crossed.

Pre-Threshold Governance

Traditional legal frameworks rely predominantly on ex post mechanisms: liability, remediation, and compensation after harm. The c-ECO framework shifts the locus of legal relevance upstream, to the zone where intervention remains possible and effective. The Manual embeds continuous monitoring, early warning signals, and automatic prudential responses into routine governance.

Prudential Asymmetry of Uncertainty (Σ)

Uncertainty is an inherent feature of complex systems. The principle of asymmetrical uncertainty holds that uncertainty must never be used to justify inaction where potential consequences include irreversible harm. Margins of error contract operational space rather than expand it; confidence intervals are applied in the direction of risk; worst-case plausible scenarios are privileged.

Restoration First

When certified risk of irreversibility emerges, preservation of systemic reversibility takes precedence over financial performance, contractual expectations, or creditor interests. Procedures for liquidity, guarantees, intervention, and execution ensure resources are available, mobilizable, and legally protected for restoration when needed.

III

TFP Core Variables

The TFP is structured around fundamental variables capturing different dimensions of systemic risk. The Manual defines standardized procedures for measuring, certifying, and combining these variables, ensuring consistency across sectors and jurisdictions:

P POSITION

Measures proximity to relevant biophysical or systemic limits. Not a generic environmental indicator — a boundary-referenced, context-locked metric whose meaning is defined by sectoral SPS specification and calibration parameters. Legal function: imposes non-negotiable material constraint upon contractual execution.

ΔV VELOCITY

Captures the temporal dimension of change — direction and rate at which the system moves toward or away from limits. Functions as the primary anticipatory signal: systems far from threshold but accelerating rapidly may present higher danger than systems closer but stabilizing.

σ UNCERTAINTY

Modulates all other variables, ensuring prudential conservatism. Applied asymmetrically: where P approaches boundary, σ reduces distance to limit; where data suggests safety, σ erodes that safety margin. Never justifies delay or optimism — always contracts operational space.

Lr REVERSIBILITY LIQUIDITY

Assesses availability and mobilizability of resources required to reverse or contain harmful trajectories. Ratio between Resources Mobilizable Immediately (Rmi) and Projected Technical Cost of Containment (Ct). Lr ≥ 1.0 indicates sufficient capacity; Lr < 0.5 triggers Restoration First.

Trigger Function: Γ = f(P, ΔV, σ, Lr) → Output: Normalized prudential score (0-100) → Classification: Green/Amber/Red/Black Band

IV

Operational Scores

From TFP variables, operational scores are derived enabling classification, comparison, and trigger activation. The Manual specifies how these scores are calculated, audited, disclosed, and used:

SPS

Safe Operating Space Proximity Score

Measures current distance between asset/operation and applicable SOS boundary. Scale 0-100: 80-100 (safe margin), 60-79 (attention zone), 40-59 (material risk), <40 (critical proximity). Derived exclusively from certified data, not managerial discretion.

TRS

Trajectory Risk Score

Measures direction, speed, and persistence of movement toward material limits. Scale 0-100: 80-100 (stabilized/improving), 60-79 (slow deterioration), 40-59 (accelerating deterioration), <40 (predictable collapse trajectory). Framework does not wait for limits to be crossed.

RLS

Reversibility Liquidity Score (RLS/Lr)

Measures immediate financial capacity to contain, mitigate, restore, or safely decommission. Lr ≥ 1.2 (fully financeable), 0.8-1.19 (limited margin), 0.5-0.79 (relevant insufficiency), <0.5 (reversibility insolvency). Operation may be financially solvent yet ecologically insolvent.

V

Data Verification & Certification (DVB)

Data Custodian Certification

Data Custodians operate as epistemic fiduciaries — their sole function is preserving signal chain integrity from physical reality to juridical fact. Certification requires: technical qualifications (Earth system science, environmental engineering, remote sensing), operational experience, doctrinal competence (pre-threshold logic, asymmetric uncertainty), and integrity/independence.

Chain of Custody & Immutability

Mandatory procedures: continuous measurement (not episodic sampling), chain-of-custody constraints (sensor → extraction → attestation → hashing → ledger), minimum metadata completeness for P reconstruction, and procedural triggers converting integrity deficits into escalation events.

QA/QC and Asymmetric Uncertainty Treatment

Quality Assurance/Quality Control determines data admissibility for P, ΔV calculations, or triggers conservative adjustment. Strict distinction between measurement error (governed statistically) and procedural failure (escalatory, non-compensable). Uncertainty always contracts operational margin — never expands it.

VI

Prudential Levels & Trigger Catalogue

The Trigger Catalogue defines conditions for prudential band activation and automatic effects. Embodies graduated, proportional, non-discretionary response logic. The Manual ensures trigger activation is procedurally sound, transparently recorded, and resistant to manipulation:

GREEN

Watch

SPS 80-100, TRS stable, Lr ≥ 1.0

Enhanced monitoring, no execution restrictions

AMBER

Capital Protection

TRS 60-79 or SPS 60-79 or Lr 0.8-1.19

Buffer reinforcement, partial cash flow redirection

RED

Safe Mode

TRS < 60 or SPS < 40 or Lr < 0.8

Execution reconfiguration, NOT default

BLACK

Restoration First

TRS < 40 or Lr < 0.5 or point-of-no-return

External intervention, automatic asset conversion

Master Rule: No trigger creates new obligations. It activates obligations already contractually pre-agreed. Safe Mode ≠ default. Restoration First ≠ expropriation. All effects are ex ante, non-discretionary, and scientifically justified.

VII

Auditability, Transparency & Due Process

Continuous QA/QC and Technical Audits

All data flows, calculations, classifications, and trigger activations subject to continuous Quality Assurance/Quality Control. Automatically intensified upon: procedural deviation detection, statistically significant anomalies, or activation of Level 2 (Amber) or higher. Audits are strictly technical — verify protocol application, not policy merit.

Immutable Public Ledger

All prudential indicators and classifications recorded in secure, immutable ledger preserving full data lineage: certified raw inputs, applied methodologies, uncertainty margins, resulting scores, triggered effects, timestamps, responsible custodians. Public access proportionate to systemic relevance.

Execution During Review

Framework adopts execution during review principle — where prudential triggers are activated, effects remain operative throughout any audit, review, or arbitral process. Due process preserved without granting suspensive effect. Arbitral review limited to: procedural failure, evidentiary integrity, fraud, material measurement error.
