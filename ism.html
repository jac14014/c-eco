Instituto Silvio Meira

Institutional Cooperation Brief

"Our foundational institutional cooperation with ISM establishes a juridical and administrative basis for c-ECO in Brazil, integrating ISM's expertise in Roman Law and international legal scholarship with the Foundation's governance architecture for systemic risk."

Institutional Profile

Overview

Founded September 2013
Location Belém, Pará, Brazil
Legal Status Public utility entity (State Law 10.226/2023, Municipal Law 9.946/2023)
Cooperation Term February 2026 — February 2029

The Instituto Silvio Meira (ISM) is a nonprofit juridical and cultural institution dedicated to the advancement of juridical science, with particular emphasis on Roman Law, legal theory, and the preservation of Brazil's legal heritage.

The institute honors Silvio Augusto de Bastos Meira (1919–1995) — a distinguished Brazilian jurist, professor emeritus at the Federal University of Pará (UFPA), and internationally recognized authority on Roman Law, whose work bridged Brazilian and European legal traditions.

Leadership

Institutional Direction

Dr. André Augusto Malcher Meira

President, Instituto Silvio Meira

President of the Colégio de Presidentes das Academias Jurídicas do Brasil (COPAJUB) (2025–2027), Dr. Meira is an advocate in Belém and Lisbon, professor and legal scholar. He led the institutional transition of ISM into a recognized public-interest entity.

Framework

Strategic Cooperation

The cooperation establishes a structured institutional interface for c-ECO activities in Brazil. Within this framework, ISM may act as:

⊘

Administrative Proponent

For grant applications under Brazilian law

⊘

Local Coordination Node

For research and technological development projects aligned with c-ECO

⊘

Access Facilitator

To national funding mechanisms and institutional networks

All activities are subject to prior written approval by the Foundation and governed by project-specific agreements.
