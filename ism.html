Instituto Silvio Meira

Institutional Cooperation Brief

"Our foundational institutional cooperation with ISM establishes a juridical and administrative basis for c-ECO in Brazil, integrating ISM's expertise in Roman Law and international legal scholarship with the Foundation's governance architecture for systemic risk."

Institutional Profile

Overview

Founded September 2013
Location Belém, Pará, Brazil
Legal Status Public utility entity declared by State Law 10.226/2023 and Municipal Law 9.946/2023.

The Instituto Silvio Meira (ISM) is a nonprofit juridical and cultural institution dedicated to the advancement of juridical science, with particular emphasis on Roman Law, legal theory, and the preservation of Brazil's legal heritage.

The institute honors Silvio Augusto de Bastos Meira (1919–1995) — a distinguished Brazilian jurist, professor emeritus at the Federal University of Pará (UFPA), and internationally recognized authority on Roman Law, whose work bridged Brazilian and European legal traditions.

Leadership

Institutional Direction

Dr. André Augusto Malcher Meira

President, Instituto Silvio Meira

Director, Silvio Meira Center for Juris-Roman Studies — Lisbon

André Meira is a preeminent Brazilian academic leader and international attorney whose career is defined by a profound commitment to the advancement of Legal Science, Business Law, and the preservation of Roman Law traditions. Based in Belém (Brazil) and Lisbon (Portugal), he operates at the highest levels of legal scholarship and institutional governance.

In the sphere of academic leadership and presidency, Dr. Meira currently serves as Honorary President of the Brazilian Academy of Law (ABD), following his distinguished tenure as President (2021–2023), and holds Chair No. 02 of this prestigious institution. He is also Titular of Chair No. 27 at the Academy of Legal Letters of Pará (APLJ), where he served as Vice-President from 2017 to 2019. As President of the Instituto Silvio Meira (ISM) in Brazil and Director of the Silvio Meira Center for Juris-Roman Studies in Lisbon, he bridges continental legal traditions. A key figure in the Asociación Iberoamericana de Derecho Romano (Spain), he presided over major international congresses in 2011 and 2016, cementing his role as a guardian of Latin legal foundations.

His professional practice and institutional influence extend across borders. A practicing attorney admitted to both the Brazilian Bar Association (OAB/PA) and the Portuguese Bar Association (OA/PT), he is the founder of André Meira Sociedade Individual de Advocacia. As an effective member of the Institute of Brazilian Lawyers (IAB) Commercial Law Commission, he has served as National Representative for the State of Pará since 2019. Further recognized through honorary and corresponding memberships in nearly a dozen regional legal academies across Brazil — including São Paulo, Minas Gerais, Ceará, Amazonas, and Rio de Janeiro — as well as the CNAF (National Confederation of Families) in Portugal, his cross-border expertise anchors ISM's international legal framework.

Recognized as the "Legal Personality of Pará" (2013, 2015), his contributions have been celebrated with numerous high-level decorations:

The Order of Judicial Merit (Official and Commander) from the Court of Justice of Pará (TJE/PA) • The Luiz Vaz de Camões Medal (Belém) • The John Paul II Medal (Portugal) • The Bernardo Cabral Medal (Amazonas)

Flávia Christiane de Alcântara Figueira Secco

International Liaison

Director of the Scientific Research Chair in Digital Law and Technology

Flávia Figueira is a distinguished Brazilian attorney and academic leader operating at the forefront of Digital Law, Privacy, and Data Governance. She holds a prominent position as Director of the Scientific Research Chair in Digital Law and Technology at Silvio Meira Institute (ISM), where she plays a vital role in bridging legal tradition with contemporary technological challenges.

She serves as Academic Coordinator of the Law Program at the University of the Amazon (UNAMA) and is a doctoral candidate in Law at UNICURITIBA, with a research focus on digital law and data protection. She is affiliated with the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP) and contributes as a researcher to the IDP Privacy Lab, where her work addresses data protection as a fundamental right and its implications for governance systems.

With a career that spans Brazil and Portugal, her expertise is essential for the legal architecture of data governance and systemic risk. She serves as President of the LGPD Commission at IBDFAM/PA and is a member of the Family and Succession Law Commission at OAB/PA and the Family and Technology Commission at IBDFAM Nacional.

She is the author of "Neurorights and Digital Childhood"(Neurodireitos e Infância Digital), a forthcoming work examining the legal implications of emerging technologies on cognitive and informational autonomy, particularly in vulnerable populations and high-risk digital environments.

Her profile is directly relevant to c-ECO in the context of data governance, certified information systems, and the legal structuring of digital evidence and monitoring architectures, especially where human rights, systemic risk, and decision-making processes intersect.
