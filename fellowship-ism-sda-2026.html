1. Purpose

This Subsequent Definitive Agreement ("SDA") establishes the operational, administrative, and institutional cooperation structure applicable to the implementation and support of the c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab activities conducted in Belém, Pará, Brazil.

This SDA is executed pursuant to Section 5 of the Memorandum of Understanding between the Parties dated February 27, 2026. It supplements the MOU and does not alter the ownership, governance, or intellectual property provisions established therein.

The Parties acknowledge that the Institute serves as the primary institutional anchor for c-ECO Fellowship operations in the Amazon region, providing local legitimacy, academic infrastructure, and operational coordination capacity distinct from individual referral channels.

2. Nature of the Relationship

2.1 Supplemental Status

This SDA supplements the MOU and does not alter the ownership, governance, or intellectual property provisions established therein.

2.2 Institutional Support Role

The Institute acts solely as a local institutional support and operational coordination entity for expressly approved Fellowship-related activities in Brazil. This role is institutional, not individual; nonprofit, not commercial; and collaborative, not controlling.

2.3 Prohibited Implications

Nothing in this SDA creates:

  1. joint ownership of c-ECO;
  2. partnership, joint venture, or fiduciary relationship;
  3. authority for the Institute to independently license, commercialize, sublicense, certify, or operationalize the c-ECO framework;
  4. authority to bind the Foundation to financial or contractual obligations without prior written approval;
  5. any employment, agency, or representation relationship between the Parties;
  6. territorial exclusivity or monopoly over c-ECO activities in Brazil or the Amazon region.

2.4 Institutional Legitimacy

The Parties acknowledge that the Institute's status as a publicly recognized entity of interest under State and Municipal law confers local institutional legitimacy for Fellowship activities, but does not confer doctrinal, methodological, or governance authority over the c-ECO framework.

Institutional Distinction
The Institute is not a referral partner, sales agent, or commercial intermediary. It is an institutional cooperation entity with public-interest recognition, academic infrastructure, and local operational capacity. All financial flows, admissions authority, and doctrinal custody remain exclusively with the Foundation. No commission, fee, revenue share, or success-based compensation structure applies to the Institute under this SDA.

3. Approved Activities

Subject to prior written approval by the Foundation, the Institute may support:

  1. Local Fellowship coordination — scheduling, venue preparation, candidate communication, and logistical organization in Belém;
  2. Institutional outreach and academic articulation — engagement with universities, research centers, public agencies, and civil society organizations aligned with c-ECO objectives;
  3. Logistical organization of Fellowship-related meetings, workshops, seminars, and events;
  4. Candidate support and onboarding coordination — local orientation, document collection, and integration support for admitted Fellows;
  5. Venue coordination and institutional hosting — provision of or coordination for physical spaces, digital infrastructure, and institutional branding for Fellowship activities;
  6. Local operational assistance connected to the Amazon Living Lab structure, including field visits, community engagement, and territorial protocol coordination;
  7. Approved public-interest educational and research initiatives connected to c-ECO, including legal research chairs, policy briefs, and academic publications subject to Foundation approval;
  8. Translation and local facilitation support for Foundation representatives, visiting Fellows, and international guests.

4. Fellowship Participation Structure

4.1 FPA Governance

Fellowship Admission remains exclusively governed by the Fellowship Participation Agreement ("FPA") executed between the Foundation and each admitted Fellow.

4.2 Foundation Sole Authority

The Foundation retains sole authority regarding:

  1. admissions and selection criteria;
  2. scholarships and fee determinations;
  3. standing classifications (Active, Conditional, Lapsed, Suspended, Revoked);
  4. certification and credential issuance;
  5. methodological supervision and doctrinal updates;
  6. disciplinary determinations;
  7. instructional architecture and module design;
  8. any future licensing authorization or commercial deployment.

4.3 Institute Coordination Limit

The Institute may assist with local participant coordination and communication support but shall not promise or guarantee admission, certification, scholarship approval, or operational licensing.

4.4 Tracking Attribution

Candidates and Fellows linked to the Institute's coordination activities shall use the institutional tracking code "ism-amazonlab" in all application materials and communications to enable proper operational attribution and cohort mapping.

5. Operational Support and Institutional Cost Allocation

Non-Commercial Principle
The Institute operates under a nonprofit, public-interest mandate. All financial support is structured as operational reimbursement or approved institutional allocation. No commission, fee, revenue share, percentage participation, success-based compensation, or commercial incentive structure applies. This preserves the Institute's institutional legitimacy, the Foundation's 501(c)(3) status, and the collaborative integrity of the c-ECO Fellowship Program.

5.1 Fellowship Participation Fees

All Fellowship participation fees, admission costs, instructional support fees, certificate costs, renewal fees, and associated amounts shall be payable exclusively to the Foundation through approved institutional channels. The Institute shall not:

  1. independently collect Fellowship participation fees;
  2. represent itself as authorized to receive payments on behalf of the Foundation;
  3. establish independent pricing for Fellowship participation;
  4. intermediate, hold, or process Fellowship payments.

5.2 Approved Operational Costs — Reimbursement

The Institute may receive reimbursement for documented actual costs reasonably incurred in connection with expressly approved operational support activities. Eligible categories include, without limitation:

  1. local venue coordination and rental;
  2. event organization support (materials, signage, interpretation, catering for institutional events);
  3. communication and scheduling assistance;
  4. institutional articulation with local stakeholders;
  5. approved logistical expenses (transportation, accommodation for invited guests, field visit costs);
  6. local operational coordination staff time (documented hourly or project-based);
  7. translation or local facilitation support;
  8. approved technology, equipment, or infrastructure costs;
  9. other documented costs pre-approved in writing by the Foundation.

5.3 Documentation and Transparency

Any reimbursement request:

  1. must be supported by original receipts, invoices, time records, or activity reports;
  2. must correspond to actual approved operational support;
  3. shall not constitute commission, revenue sharing, percentage participation, or success fee;
  4. shall comply with Section 6 of the MOU (Financial Principles);
  5. shall be pre-approved in writing by the Foundation before expenditure, except for emergency expenses not exceeding USD $500 per incident, subject to post-hoc documentation within fifteen (15) days;
  6. shall be submitted for reconciliation within thirty (30) days of activity completion.

5.4 Annual Institutional Support Allocation

The Foundation may, at its sole discretion and subject to available institutional resources, approve annual operational support allocations for Institute activities associated with the c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab.

Any such allocation:

  1. shall be determined annually upon operational proposal submitted by the Institute and prior written approval by the Foundation;
  2. shall be subject to quarterly reporting on activities and expenditures;
  3. shall be subject to Foundation discretion to modify, suspend, or terminate upon thirty (30) days written notice;
  4. shall not create any entitlement, expectation, or obligation for continuation beyond the approved period;
  5. shall be used exclusively for approved operational purposes and not for general institutional overhead, unrelated activities, or personal benefit.

5.5 Event-Specific Budget

For individual events, workshops, or initiatives requiring dedicated resources, the Institute may submit a specific budget proposal to the Foundation for pre-approval. Such proposals shall include:

  1. event description, objectives, and expected outcomes;
  2. itemized budget with cost estimates;
  3. timeline and milestones;
  4. participant or audience estimates;
  5. post-event reporting commitments.

5.6 Fellowship Participation Reference Structure

The following ranges are illustrative reference structures for Fellowship participation pathways. They do not constitute binding pricing, guaranteed availability, or operational authorization:

Participation Pathway Reference Range
Full Scholarship — Module 1 Only USD $0
Waiver-Supported Participation Instructional support only, where separately arranged
Standard Fellowship Admission — Modules 1–6 USD $1,500–2,500 total

Additional notes:

  1. Standard Fellowship admission may be payable in installments where approved by the Foundation;
  2. Fellowship admission grants access to the methodological and instructional environment only;
  3. Fellowship participation does not automatically authorize operational or institutional use of c-ECO in real-world environments;
  4. All amounts are payable directly to the Foundation. The Institute does not participate in, intermediate, or benefit from Fellowship fee revenue.

5.7 License Reference Ranges — Informational Only

The following ranges are non-binding illustrative reference structures for potential future institutional deployment pathways. They do not constitute binding pricing commitments, guaranteed availability, or operational authorization:

License Type Reference Range
Pilot / Small-Scale Case License USD $2,500–7,500
Structured Operational License USD $7,500–20,000
Institutional / Infrastructure License USD $20,000–50,000+

Final license conditions depend on project complexity, sectoral exposure, systemic sensitivity, data classification, institutional scale, implementation scope, monitoring requirements, and the applicable CSAM and licensing structure. No Institute entitlement arises from License activation.

5.8 Payment Mechanics

Reimbursement of approved operational costs shall be processed within thirty (30) business days following:

  1. submission of complete documentation (receipts, reports, time records);
  2. Foundation verification of activity alignment with approved scope;
  3. Foundation approval of expenditure reconciliation.

5.9 Tax and Regulatory Compliance

The Institute remains solely responsible for all taxes, social contributions, regulatory compliance, and institutional filings arising from operational support allocations and reimbursements received under this SDA. The Foundation shall issue applicable documentation as required by law.

6. Instructional and Methodological Structure

6.1 Foundation Authority

Instructional architecture, methodological supervision, analytical review, TFP-related supervision, and Fellowship content governance remain under Foundation authority exclusively.

6.2 No Doctrinal Control

Local operational support provided by the Institute does not constitute doctrinal control, methodological ownership, or curriculum authority over c-ECO.

6.3 Guest Participation Approval

Any guest participation, local speaker invitation, institutional collaborator, or event participation associated with the Fellowship shall remain subject to Foundation approval.

6.4 Academic Publications

Research outputs, policy briefs, or academic publications arising from Fellowship activities coordinated by the Institute shall be subject to Foundation review for doctrinal accuracy and attribution compliance before publication.

7. Intellectual Property

7.1 MOU Applicability

All intellectual property protections established under Section 4 of the MOU remain fully applicable to this SDA.

7.2 Institute Acknowledgment

The Institute acknowledges that:

  1. c-ECO remains the exclusive intellectual property of Jacqueline A. Ennis (or designated entity);
  2. no ownership transfer occurs under this SDA;
  3. operational support does not create co-authorship, co-ownership, or governance authority over c-ECO;
  4. the Institute shall not register trademarks, copyrights, translations, or derivative designations incorporating "c-ECO" without prior written authorization.

7.3 Publication Attribution

All public references to c-ECO must include clear attribution to Jacqueline A. Ennis and the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation. The Institute may be acknowledged as "local institutional partner" or "operational coordination entity" as approved by the Foundation.

8. Public Communications

8.1 Prior Approval

Public communications relating to Fellowship activities conducted under this SDA shall require prior written approval by the Foundation.

8.2 Accurate Reflection

Public communications must accurately reflect:

  1. the Foundation's ownership and governance role;
  2. the Institute's operational and institutional support role;
  3. the non-exclusive and collaborative nature of the relationship;
  4. the public-interest and nonprofit character of the Institute's involvement.

8.3 Prohibited Implications

Neither Party shall publicly imply:

  1. territorial exclusivity;
  2. institutional control over the other Party;
  3. ownership transfer of c-ECO;
  4. governmental endorsement not formally granted;
  5. commercial partnership, profit-sharing, or revenue-sharing arrangement.

9. License Activation Limitation

9.1 No License Authority

Fellowship participation does not authorize operational, contractual, regulatory, or commercial deployment of c-ECO.

9.2 Future Licensing Requirements

Any future operational or institutional licensing activity involving c-ECO shall require:

  1. separate written authorization from the Foundation;
  2. applicable CSAM review;
  3. operational platform availability;
  4. additional licensing instruments where applicable.

9.3 Infrastructure Status

As of the Effective Date of this SDA, operational licensing infrastructure remains under development. The Institute shall not solicit, negotiate, represent, market, or imply availability of c-ECO License opportunities.

10. Term and Termination

10.1 Initial Term

This SDA shall remain effective for twelve (12) months unless earlier terminated.

10.2 Renewal

This SDA may renew for additional twelve (12)-month terms subject to satisfactory collaboration performance, as determined by the Foundation in its sole discretion. Renewal requires written confirmation by both parties.

10.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this SDA upon thirty (30) days written notice.

10.4 Immediate Termination by Foundation

The Foundation may terminate this SDA immediately upon written notice if the Institute:

  1. breaches confidentiality or data governance obligations;
  2. engages in unauthorized use of Foundation intellectual property;
  3. commits reputational misconduct or misrepresentation regarding the Foundation, c-ECO, or License availability;
  4. attempts to independently license, commercialize, or operationalize c-ECO;
  5. fails to maintain public-interest status or loses institutional recognition under applicable law.

10.5 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

  1. approved confidential information protections survive;
  2. unauthorized public references shall cease immediately;
  3. pending approved operational reimbursements may still be processed subject to documentation compliance;
  4. intellectual property protections remain fully enforceable;
  5. the Institute shall return or destroy all Foundation proprietary materials within fifteen (15) days.

11. Governing Law

This SDA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Missouri, United States of America, consistent with the governing law clause of the MOU.

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this SDA shall be resolved in accordance with the dispute-resolution framework established in the MOU, unless the Parties agree in writing to arbitration or another appropriate mechanism.

Nothing in this Section prevents either Party from seeking injunctive, equitable, or emergency relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to protect confidential information, intellectual property, institutional integrity, or proprietary rights.
