Risk Matrix & Mitigation

Six critical risks with specific mitigation strategies.

Key-Person Dependency

High

Project depends on single founder for vision, partnerships, and execution.

Mitigation: Hire Science-Policy Lead and Integration Officer within 60 days. Document all TFP protocols. Establish GIH succession plan.

Partner Formalization Delay

Medium

INPA, MPEG, or ACTO may delay MOU execution due to institutional bureaucracy.

Mitigation: Start with Letter of Intent (1 page). Parallel track with 2 institutions. Use Hasse Foundation board connections.

Currency & Inflation Volatility

Medium

BRL volatility may erode purchasing power for local operations and salaries.

Mitigation: Hold 40% of local costs in USD reserve. Index salaries to IPCA. Use multi-currency accounting.

Technology & Data Access

Medium

Satellite data gaps, sensor failure in remote areas, or INPE access restrictions.

Mitigation: Multi-source redundancy (Planet + Sentinel + Landsat). Spare sensor inventory. Local technician training.

Political & Regulatory Shift

Medium

Changes in Brazilian environmental policy may affect Forest Code enforcement or data access.

Mitigation: Diversify regulatory hooks (subnational, private sector, international treaties). Maintain non-partisan positioning.

Funding Continuity

High

18-month runway with no guaranteed Beta phase funding creates cliff-edge risk.

Mitigation: Begin Beta fundraising at Month 6. Develop revenue model (TFP licensing, enterprise contracts). Build endowment case.

Meeting Script — First 5 Minutes

Objective: make the funder feel they should have built this already.

Minute 0:00 — 0:30 | The Hook
Opening Line
"The Amazon is not underfunded. It is under-instrumented for decision."

Pause. Let the sentence resonate. Do not explain yet.

Why it works: Challenges the funder's premise. Repositions the problem as technical, not financial. Creates curiosity.

Minute 0:30 — 1:30 | The Gap
Their Problem, Not Yours
"You already spend [X millions/billions] on Amazon exposure. Insurance, sovereign bonds, development finance, supply chains. The detection exists. The capital exists. But there is no layer that connects them in time to prevent loss."

Rhetorical question: "How much did you lose last year on mispriced Amazon risk? Not because you lacked data. Because you lacked the infrastructure to act on it."

Minute 1:30 — 3:00 | The Solution
In One Sentence
"c-ECO is the missing layer. We build risk reduction infrastructure — not research, not charity. A computational and contractual system that translates ecological detection into financial and legal action in under 72 hours."

Concrete example: "Imagine: deforestation in Pará crosses 15% in a quarter. Instead of reading about it in The Guardian next month, your sovereign bond covenant auto-adjusts the interest rate. Your insurance premium recalibrates. Your supply chain contract triggers a sourcing alternative. All within 72 hours."

Minute 3:00 — 4:00 | The Proof
Why You

"I've spent five years developing this methodology. LLM from Washington University. Background in AI, complex systems, and international law. The TFP framework is already documented. The legal architecture is drafted. The institutional relationships — INPA, MPEG, ACTO, INMET — are established."

If asked "why you?": "Because I am the only person who has built the bridge between these three worlds: Earth system science, computational infrastructure, and contractual law."

Minute 4:00 — 5:00 | The Ask
The Next Step

Never ask for money in the first 5 minutes.

"I'm not asking you to fund a project. I'm asking you to stop paying for a gap that already costs you money. The question is not whether c-ECO works. The question is whether you can afford another year without it."

Then: "What I'd like from this conversation is your assessment: does this infrastructure solve a problem you already have? And if so, what would it take for your institution to be the first to deploy it?"

Anticipated Objections (15-second responses)

"You're just one person. Scale?"

"Phase Alpha is 8 FTE, not me alone. But more importantly: c-ECO is methodology-first, not personality-dependent. Every TFP protocol is documented, every trigger is codified, every contract is templated. The system outlives any individual."

"Why doesn't this exist already?"

"Because detection and finance evolved in silos. Scientists don't write contracts. Bankers don't read satellite data. c-ECO is the translator — and translators only exist when both sides are speaking loudly enough to need one. They are now."

"What's the ROI?"

"For a reinsurer: every 1% improvement in trigger accuracy on Amazon cat bonds is worth $50M in mispriced exposure. For a development bank: avoiding one stranded asset pays for this entire lab. The ROI is 'return on risk reduction.'"

"How do I know this works?"

"You don't. Yet. That's why Phase Alpha is 18 months and $792K — not $50M. We validate the architecture with 3 pilots, measurable KPIs, and independent audit. You fund proof, not promise."
