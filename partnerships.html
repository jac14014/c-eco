Partnerships

Institutional & Academic Collaboration

c-ECO develops partnerships with academic institutions, research centers, public-interest organizations, and long-horizon actors engaged in systemic risk governance and pre-threshold decision-making.

Partnerships are structured as conceptual and applied research collaborations. At this stage, they are non-commercial, non-certifying, and focused on refining legal architectures rather than promoting adoption or endorsement.

Principle:
Institutional names are disclosed publicly only upon formal agreement and mutual consent.

Partner categories

  • Academic partners (Earth System Law, contract theory, governance)
  • Research institutions (policy labs, systemic risk research)
  • Public or hybrid institutions (guardianship, infrastructure)
  • Pilot organizations (applied study participation)
