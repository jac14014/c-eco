Pre-Threshold Contractual Governance

Governance
before collapse.

A legal architecture designed to safeguard future decision-space by preventing irreversible trajectories.

"Legality persists while the capacity to change course is exhausted."

The **c-ECO Doctrine** reconceptualizes contracts as temporal architectures of decision. Traditional law intervenes in damage; we intervene before the threshold.

In complex systems, decisions accumulate until reaching a point of foreclosure—the definitive closing of alternatives. By the time damage occurs, Law no longer has anything to govern, only to sanction. c-ECO breaks with this temporal impunity.

Σ

Time-as-Status

Replacing "deadline" time with "capacity" time: the sum of decisions that still allow for the system's survival.

⊘

Limits to Authorization

Systemic integrity operates as a negative condition of validity: if the system collapses, the contract loses its legal purpose.

♽

Adaptive Design

The duty of active monitoring and mandatory renegotiation as prerequisites for systemic security.

Doctrinal Governance

Constitutes the non-delegable doctrinal nucleus. It is the site in which the legal ontology of the regime is protected against external substitution.

02

Threshold Function Protocol (TFP)

Qualifies temporal states of risk and irreversibility, serving as a juridical interface between physical reality and legal consequence.

03

AI Decision Architecture

Defined only negatively: as epistemic mediation and never as normative authority. Prevents algorithmic governing power.

04

Safeguard Against Capture

Prevents "Scientific Capture" where science informs law, but never redefines what counts as legal validity.
