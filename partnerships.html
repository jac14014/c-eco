Jacqueline A. Ennis

Legal Scholar & Researcher

Jacqueline A. Ennis is a Brazilian legal scholar and independent researcher. She is the author of the c-ECO Doctrine (Contractual Equity & Ecological Co-Responsibility).

Her work reconceptualizes contracts as temporal architectures of decision. Her thesis demonstrates that legally consequential decisions occur mostly during periods of regulatory compliance, exhausting future decision-making space. c-ECO introduces the concepts of temporal validity and pre-threshold responsibility.

The formulation is anchored in her academic trajectory at Washington University in St. Louis and studies at Boston University, strategically supported by the Silvio Meira Institute (ISM).

Layer 1

Doctrinal Governance (Closed Core)

Nature, Function, and Non-Delegability: This core defines what cannot be delegated, calibrated, or substituted.

Nature of the Closed Core

The Closed Core constitutes the non-delegable doctrinal nucleus of the c-ECO Doctrine. It is the site in which the legal ontology of the regime is defined, stabilized, and protected against external substitution.

Unlike operational layers, validation frameworks, or sectoral applications, the Closed Core is not subject to calibration, expansion, delegation, or experimental modification. Its function is to define the conditions under which governance remains legally possible in a destabilized Earth System.

Components of the Closed Core

1 — Core Conceptual Framework

Foundational legal concepts: trajectory illegality, ex-ante unenforceability, systemic perclusion, conditioned immediate object, temporal validity, and legal irreversibility. These are constitutive categories, not interpretive tools.

2 — Systemic Governance Statute

A constitutive legal framework that subordinates legality to biophysical reality. It derives authority from material reality, not from delegation or consensus.

3 — Threshold Function Protocol (TFP)

The juridical interface between physical reality and legal consequence. It qualifies temporal states of risk and defines when legality must be interrupted.

4 — AI Decision Architecture (Conceptual)

AI is defined only as epistemic mediation. This prevents algorithmic systems from acquiring normative authority or de facto governing power.

5 — Doctrinal Integrity and Evolution

Evolution is limited to clarification and internal coherence. Substitution of ontological premises is categorically excluded.

Layer Logic

This card defines what cannot be delegated, calibrated, or substituted. Earth System Law and Compliance frameworks operate around this core, not within it.

Layer 1

Compliance & Institutional Integrity Architecture (Non-Substitutive)

Institutional Translation, Preventive Design, and Anti-Capture Safeguards.

Layer Logic

This institutional layer translates Closed Core legality into decision structures and contractual design. It prevents "compliance theater" by ensuring institutions remain structurally capable of lawful interruption when the TFP triggers.

Nature and Function

The function of this layer is not to define legality, but to ensure that the binding conditions established by the Closed Core are faithfully translated into institutional design and decision structures.

It exists to prevent a recurrent legal failure: situations in which formally compliant institutions remain materially incapable of respecting systemic limits due to fragmented governance or incentive misalignment.

Distinction from Conventional Compliance

Conventional: Optimizes risk within accepted operational parameters and ensures procedural diligence.

c-ECO Architecture: Asks: "Is this institutional structure capable of remaining lawful under conditions of irreversibility and threshold proximity?"

Core Functions

  • 01.

    Institutional Translation

    Translates ontological conditions into governance constraints without reinterpreting or diluting them.

  • 02.

    Preventive Structural Design

    Ensures institutions detect and act upon pre-threshold signals before irreversibility is reached.

  • 03.

    Integrity Preservation

    Prevents internal fragmentation or incentive distortion from eroding the force of systemic limits.

Relationship to the TFP

Compliance becomes a condition of enforceability, not a shield against it. When the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP) signals legal interruption, this layer ensures the institution is:

  • Structurally capable of halting execution.
  • Contractually bound to respect interruption.
  • Procedurally insulated against override through managerial pressure.

"Compliance within c-ECO does not mediate legality; it preserves the conditions under which legality remains possible."
