Partnerships

Institutional and academic collaborations supporting pre-threshold contractual governance research.

c-ECO is building a network of partners to develop and test legal architectures that preserve decision-space under systemic risk and potential irreversibility. Partnerships are structured as research-oriented collaborations and applied study participation.

Principle:
Partner institutions are acknowledged publicly only upon formal agreement and mutual consent.

Partner categories

  • Academic Partners — legal theory, Earth System Law, contract doctrine, governance research.
  • Research Institutions — applied governance labs, policy labs, systems risk research.
  • Public-Interest Institutions — guardianship of non-disposable interests, preventive legal action.
  • Pilot Organizations — long-horizon contracting environments where pre-threshold tools can be tested.

Partner pathway

Institutions interested in collaboration can engage through one of the pathways below:

  • Research collaboration — conceptual interface and joint study design.
  • Applied study participation — pilot review of contract archetypes and governance tools.
  • Sponsorship support — funding of research outputs, workshops, or applied study tracks.

Please do not share confidential or proprietary information via web forms or email unless an NDA or formal process is in place.
