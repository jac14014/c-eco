Σc-ECO Systemic Security Protocol

"The c-ECO Doctrine introduces pre-threshold contractual governance: a legal architecture that safeguards the space for future decisions."

In complex systems, decisions accumulate until reaching a point of foreclosure — the definitive closing of alternatives. By the time damage occurs, Law no longer has anything to govern, only to sanction.

Legality persists while the capacity to change course is exhausted. c-ECO breaks with the temporal impunity of current legal design through Residual Material Validity and Reversibility Protocols activated at 80% of a critical threshold.

Layer 1 Architecture

Core I

Doctrinal Governance (Closed Core)

Nature, Function, and Non-Delegability

Layer logic: This card defines what cannot be delegated, calibrated, or substituted. Earth System Law and Compliance frameworks operate around this core, not within it.

Nature of the Closed Core

The Closed Core constitutes the non-delegable doctrinal nucleus of the c-ECO Doctrine. Its function is not to govern conduct directly, but to define the conditions under which governance remains legally possible in a destabilized Earth System. These commitments are binding and cannot be overridden by scientific findings, compliance standards, or institutional preferences.

Components of the Closed Core
  • 1 — Core Conceptual Framework Concepts like trajectory illegality and systemic perclusion are constitutive categories that determine the architecture of the Statute.
  • 2 — Systemic Governance Statute Establishes the hierarchy of legal relevance, subordinating legality to biophysical reality.
  • 3 — Threshold Function Protocol (TFP) Qualifies temporal states of risk. Its inclusion prevents it from being reduced to a discretionary compliance tool.
  • 4 — AI Decision Architecture (Conceptual) AI is defined only as epistemic mediation; never as normative authority.
  • 5 — Doctrinal Integrity and Evolution Evolution is permitted only at the level of clarification. Substitution of premises is categorically excluded.
Core II

Earth System Law Advisory

Material Alignment & Non-Governing Authority

Layer logic: This advisory layer confirms ontological alignment with Earth System Law while remaining non-executive. It prevents scientific capture and normative dilution.

Earth System Law recognizes planetary boundaries as conditions for long-term habitability. c-ECO advances this by elevating the Safe Operating Space from a scientific concept to a juridical condition of validity.

The Advisory confirms alignment but does not substitute scientific judgment for legal ontology. It ensures that law must adapt to the Earth System, not the reverse.

Core III

Compliance & Institutional Integrity

Institutional Translation & Preventive Design

Layer logic: This institutional layer translates Closed Core legality into decision structures, ensuring institutions remain capable of lawful interruption when the TFP triggers.

Conventional compliance asks "how can this activity be made acceptable?". c-ECO compliance asks "is this institutional structure capable of remaining lawful under conditions of irreversibility?"

It ensures that, once the TFP signals legal interruption, institutions are contractually bound to respect interruption and procedurally insulated against override through managerial or financial pressure.
