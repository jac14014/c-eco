01.

The Temporal
Mismatch

Current legal systems intervene in the damage phase. c-ECO operates in the pre-threshold state—where the capacity to change course still exists.

"Legality persists while the capacity to change course is exhausted."

In complex systems, decisions accumulate until reaching a point of foreclosure—the definitive closing of alternatives. By the time damage occurs, Law no longer has anything to govern, only to sanction.

c-ECO breaks with this temporal impunity. It recognizes that the material validity of a contract depends on maintaining the biophysical conditions that allowed its authorization. If the system's integrity is at stake, the contract's legal purpose dissolves.

Pillar A

Residual Material Validity

Contractual validity is tethered to the real conditions (ecological and social) of day one. Once those are eroded, the legal force of the trajectory is precluded.

Pillar B

Reversibility Protocols

Mechanisms triggered at the 80% mark of a critical threshold. Law acts as a circuit breaker before the systemic "point of no return."
