The Model

The Fellowship Operates
as an Execution Lab.

Fellows do not merely study c-ECO. They operate it on real institutional cases — the same cases that feed the c-ECO Pilot and Enterprise streams. This creates a live ecosystem where risk moves from measurement to market signal.

Typical Fellowship

  • Lectures and theoretical frameworks
  • General case studies (not real)
  • Certificates as the end output
  • No connection to market actors

Result: Fellows get a certificate. Institutions get nothing.

c-ECO Execution Lab

  • Real institutional cases from the Pilot stream
  • TDR/TFP applied to live systems
  • Verifiable diagnostic outputs
  • Direct pipeline to Pilot deliverables
  • Contractual accountability and governance

Result: A functioning micro-market of c-ECO. Fellows operate. Clients receive.

The Charter

What This Is.
What This Is Not.

The c-ECO Fellowship is a controlled applied system. Here is everything you need to understand before applying.

What You Gain

  • Operate c-ECO on a real institutional case
  • Produce a verifiable diagnostic report
  • Build board-ready governance instruments
  • Author a systemic risk brief for market audiences
  • Fellowship standing under the Hasse Foundation
  • Access to the c-ECO methodology and TFP
  • Connection to a network of serious practitioners

What This Is Not

  • Not an academic degree or university qualification
  • Not a traditional fellowship with unrestricted autonomy
  • Not permanent licensure or accreditation
  • Not a consulting engagement for client optimization
  • Not a neutral platform detached from planetary constraints
  • Not a course where you watch lectures and take tests
  • Not a networking event disguised as a program

Ideal Fellow Profile

Candidates capable of operating in high-complexity systemic environments

Professionals in contracts, infrastructure, regulation
Economists and finance specialists
Jurists and policy analysts
Engineers and sustainability practitioners
Researchers in systemic risk
Infrastructure and environmental analysts

Preference given to: candidates with prior experience in contracts, infrastructure, finance, environmental systems, mineral extraction, water systems, energy systems, or adjacent institutional fields. Ability to engage with applied — not merely abstract — problem environments is essential.
