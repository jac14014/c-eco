c-eco.org · Methodology

Science → Clause

How certified scientific data becomes legally operative contractual obligation — without managerial discretion, without delay, without a human veto. The methodology that converts monitoring into governance.

The Problem

Every major ecological and financial threshold breach documented by c-ECO was preceded by certified monitoring data that predicted it. In each case, the data existed before the failure. The intervention came after. This is not a coordination problem. It is a design problem.

The gap is structural: modern governance systems were built on the assumption that certified scientific data produces legal response. It does not. Monitoring systems generate data. Data generates reports. Reports generate discussions. Discussions generate recommendations. Recommendations generate — nothing, unless a non-discretionary mechanism connects the data to a binding obligation.

Science → Clause is the methodology c-ECO uses to close that gap: tracing how a specific scientific signal becomes a specific legal trigger in a specific contractual instrument — and documenting what happens when it does not.

Detection

InSAR satellites detect millimeter-scale surface deformation in tailings structures. The Córrego do Feijão dam showed 3.4 mm/day acceleration starting 47 days before collapse. The signal was commercially available, publicly observable, and unambiguous.

Certification

The Calibration Council (or equivalent certifying body) validates the deformation alert against agreed parameters. Certification converts raw monitoring data into legally operative evidence — admissible and binding without further interpretation.

Trigger Logic

The certified deformation rate exceeds the TFP Article 36 threshold parameter. Obligations activate automatically. No managerial discretion enters the chain. No human veto is permitted. The clause executes.

The counterparty's contractual obligations become operative. Remediation timelines, liability allocations, third-party notification requirements, and suspension protocols activate as written — not as negotiated under pressure.

Operational execution under certified protocols — 47 days before collapse, not 47 days after. The dam does not fail. The 270 people are not killed. The clause worked because it did not require permission to work.

The Science

InSAR Surface Deformation
Early Warning System

Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar detects surface deformation in tailings structures at millimeter precision from orbital altitude. Repeat-pass InSAR compares phase differences between two radar acquisitions taken days or weeks apart to detect changes in surface position at sub-centimeter scale.

Available commercially since 2017 and standard in dam safety monitoring practice by 2019 — including, explicitly, for the Córrego do Feijão structure that failed at Brumadinho. The signal existed 47 days before collapse. It was not connected to a legal obligation. The monitoring worked. The governance did not.

Source: InSAR monitoring program · Córrego do Feijão, Vale S.A. · 2018–2019
The Clause

TFP Article 36
Certified Sensory Data

"Upon certification by the Calibration Council that observed deformation rates have exceeded the agreed threshold parameter for the relevant structure, the counterparty's obligations under Schedule B activate automatically and without discretionary review. Activation is non-delegable and non-waivable."

This model clause connects the InSAR signal to a binding legal obligation. Certification by the Calibration Council is the mechanism. The threshold parameter is agreed in advance, not negotiated under crisis conditions. Activation requires no human decision — only the certified signal.

Model clause · Full text: hasse.foundation · TFP Manual v1.1
Why It Matters

This clause would have required binding remediation action 47 days before the Córrego do Feijão dam collapsed. The InSAR data existed. The monitoring system was active. The deformation signal was commercially available and unambiguous. Without a non-discretionary trigger, certified risk remained legally inert. The governance architecture did not translate the signal into obligation. 270 people died in a failure the data had already predicted.

Doctrine

The Science → Clause methodology is grounded in the doctrine of temporal integrity of law — the principle that a legal instrument which cannot respond to certified risk before a threshold is crossed has failed its primary function. Delay is not a neutral attribute of legal systems. In irreversible systems, it is the mechanism of failure. The canonical statement of this doctrine is published at hasse.foundation. The model clause library and TFP Protocol are available at c-eco.io.
