c-ECO Systemic Governance Initiative

c-ECO Fellowship Program — Applied Entry Portal

This portal constitutes the official entry interface for the c-ECO Fellowship Program, a selective applied environment designed for participation in sector-based systemic governance under threshold-conditioned legal and operational logic. You are not applying to a program. You are entering a conditioned system.
SECTION 1 — PROGRAM OVERVIEW
The c-ECO Fellowship Program is not structured as a general academic course, open lecture cycle, or unrestricted training environment. It operates as a controlled applied system through which selected participants engage with real or proposed case environments under the c-ECO decisional governance architecture.
The Fellowship is organized as an institutional entry layer into systemic governance by thresholds. Fellows do not merely study concepts. They enter a conditioned analytical environment in which conduct, outputs, and methodological validity are subordinated to the Safe Operating Space (SOS), the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP), and the logic of pre-threshold intervention.
System Character — Critical Distinction
This is not an educational program with optional participation. It is a systemic governance environment where behavior, analysis, and contractual obligations are conditioned by certified systemic states. Entry constitutes adherence to a regime of pre-threshold conduct, not enrollment in a curriculum.
1.1 — What This System Is
The Fellowship operates as a four-layer applied architecture in which participants progress from conceptual understanding to supervised case execution, with each layer conditioned by the integrity of threshold-based governance mechanisms.
I — A Policy-Conditioned Environment: Conduct is governed by the Fellowship Policy Framework, which subordinates individual action to systemic compatibility requirements
II — A Contractually-Bound System: Participation requires execution of the Contractual Adhesion Stack, including binding commitment to methodological adherence
III — A Threshold-Triggered Regime: Safe Mode activation is automatic upon certified TFP conditions, not subject to discretionary override
IV — A Sector-Based Application: All engagement occurs through specific sectoral tracks, each integrating real-world systemic risk environments
1.2 — What This System Is Not
To prevent misalignment of expectations and ensure appropriate participant self-selection, the following clarifications are binding:
I — Not a Traditional Fellowship: There is no independent research agenda, no discretionary output selection, and no individual publication priority independent of systemic validity
II — Not an Academic Course: There are no grades, no general curriculum, and no theoretical examination independent of applied case work
III — Not a Consulting Engagement: Fellows do not serve client interests, do not optimize for operational efficiency, and do not assume fiduciary responsibility toward specific stakeholders
IV — Not a Neutral Analytical Platform: Analysis is explicitly normative toward systemic preservation; trajectories incompatible with SOS stability are classified as invalid regardless of economic or political preference
SECTION 2 — METHODOLOGICAL CORE
The c-ECO Fellowship operates through the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP), a trajectory-based analytical framework that monitors systemic position, velocity, uncertainty, and reversibility liquidity to determine governance interventions before irreversible thresholds are crossed.
2.1 — TFP Variables
All sectoral applications and case analyses must explicitly reference and compute the following four variables:
P — Position

Current systemic state relative to known thresholds. Measured as distance to tipping points, carrying capacity limits, or structural failure boundaries.

ΔV — Velocity

Rate of change in systemic state. Critical for determining time-to-threshold and intervention urgency. High velocity reduces response window.

σ — Uncertainty

Confidence interval around position and velocity measurements. High uncertainty expands precautionary margins and may trigger earlier intervention.

Lr — Reversibility Liquidity

Availability of viable pathways to return system to previous state. Low Lr indicates irreversibility proximity and mandates heightened precaution.

2.2 — Classification Bands
TFP computation results in classification into one of four bands, each triggering specific governance postures and analytical constraints:
Band Condition Fellow Posture System State
Green P comfortable, ΔV stable, σ manageable, Lr adequate Standard monitoring; optimization permissible Safe Operating Space maintained
Amber P approaching threshold OR ΔV increasing OR σ elevated Enhanced monitoring; precautionary analysis required Pre-threshold zone; vigilance mandated
Red P near threshold OR high ΔV OR low Lr Safe Mode activation; containment priority Critical proximity; automatic triggers engaged
Black Threshold crossed OR irreversibility initiated Damage limitation; post-threshold protocols Emergency state; recovery focus
Safe Mode Activation — Non-Discretionary
Upon Red or Black Band classification, Safe Mode is automatically triggered. This is not a recommendation subject to institutional preference. It is a systemic state that conditionally governs all subsequent analytical and operational activity. Fellows must immediately transition to containment-priority posture and suspend optimization-oriented analysis.
2.3 — Signal Viability Requirement
No case may be admitted to the Fellowship program unless it demonstrates adequate signal viability — the existence of measurable indicators capable of supporting TFP computation with sufficient reliability for governance application.
§1 — Cases lacking signal viability must be either rejected or restructured to establish viable monitoring pathways before admission.
§2 — Signal viability is assessed during the selection process as a mandatory threshold condition, weighted at 25% of total evaluation criteria.
SECTION 3 — LIVING LAB FRAMEWORK
The Fellowship integrates real-world application through Living Lab environments — structured, iterative, participatory research settings where systemic analysis is tested against actual stakeholder dynamics and operational constraints. The Belém Living Lab, coordinated through Instituto Silvio Meira, serves as the primary Brazilian operational node.
3.1 — Living Lab Protocol with TFP Integration
All Living Lab phases must integrate Threshold Function Protocol monitoring. No scenario is considered valid without TFP classification.
Phase Duration Activity TFP Output
Diagnostic 2 weeks Stakeholder mapping; system boundary definition Initial P baseline; σ assessment
Co-Design 4 weeks Participatory workshops (World Café methodology) Trajectory scenarios; ΔV projections
Prototype 6 weeks Safe Mode simulation in controlled environment Stress test results; Lr evaluation
Validation 2 weeks Feedback loops with affected communities Validated response protocol; band classification
3.2 — Field Conduct Requirements
Fellows engaging in Living Lab environments operate under supervised conditions and must adhere to community and ecological constraints that may exceed standard research ethics requirements.
I — All field interactions require prior coordination with Instituto Silvio Meira local liaison
II — Community consent is mandatory and documented through protocols adapted from FPIC (Free, Prior, and Informed Consent)
III — Ecological impact minimization takes precedence over research efficiency
IV — TFP monitoring continues throughout all phases; band reclassification triggers immediate protocol adjustment
SECTION 4 — SELECTION CRITERIA
Admission to the c-ECO Fellowship is selective and case-based. Evaluation prioritizes the systemic characteristics of the proposed case environment over individual candidate credentials, ensuring that program capacity is allocated to contexts where threshold governance can be meaningfully applied.
4.1 — Weighted Evaluation Criteria
1. Case Specificity (30%)

Concrete operational environment with identifiable stakeholders, clear system boundaries, and defined decision points amenable to TFP analysis.

2. Reversibility Exposure (25%)

Demonstrated presence of irreversible risk dynamics where pre-threshold intervention would prevent permanent systemic degradation.

3. Signal Viability (25%)

Mandatory threshold condition. Availability of measurable indicators capable of TFP integration with adequate reliability for governance application.

4. Governance Readiness (15%)

Institutional capacity for Safe Mode implementation; existence of stakeholders capable of acting on TFP-triggered recommendations.

5. Cross-Sector Potential (5%)

Applicability to hybrid systems (Sector 13); potential for cascading risk analysis across multiple domains.

4.2 — Selection Committee
The Selection Committee comprises three representatives: one from the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, one from Instituto Silvio Meira, and one rotating Fellow alumnus. Decisions are made by consensus, with tie-breaking authority vested in the JCHF representative on matters of methodological integrity.
§1 — Selection decisions are final and not subject to appeal, except on procedural grounds.
SECTION 5 — SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE
The Fellowship operates as a four-layer progressive system. Advancement between layers is gated by demonstrated competency and case progression, not by temporal completion.
Layer 4 — Advisory
Individual Case Supervision

Direct mentorship by c-ECO architects and institutional partners. Fellows execute supervised analytical work with real-time methodological validation. This constitutes Fellowship completion.

Gate from Layer 3: Contractual instrument execution + successful Safe Mode simulation
Layer 3 — Contractual
Translation to Enforceable Structures

Conversion of systemic analysis into binding contractual instruments. Integration of IEX (Irreversibility Exchange) clauses and TFP-triggered operational provisions.

Gate from Layer 2: Approved case structure + stakeholder validation
Layer 2 — Applied
Sector-Specific Systemic Analysis

Living Lab engagement with TFP integration. Pre-threshold dynamics identification and Safe Mode protocol testing in real-world or simulated environments.

Gate from Layer 1: Sectoral competency test + case proposal approval
Layer 1 — Entry
Conceptual Foundation

c-ECO methodology introduction, TFP fundamentals, and systemic risk foundations. Completion of conceptual module and case proposal submission.

Admission: Selective application process based on case viability

SECTION 7 — FELLOWSHIP POLICY FRAMEWORK
The c-ECO Fellowship Program operates as a structured systemic governance environment in which participants act as analytical agents within a pre-threshold decision architecture. Fellow conduct, outputs, and participation are conditioned not only by institutional rules, but by alignment with the Safe Operating Space (SOS) and the integrity of systemic risk analysis.
Governance Principle
This is not "program governance" in the traditional sense. It is governance under systemic constraint architecture — where decisions are conditioned, humans operate within limits, and triggers are technical, not discretionary.
7.1 — Status of Fellows
Fellows act as non-decisional analytical agents within the c-ECO system. Their role is to identify systemic risk trajectories, apply TFP logic, and produce structured decision-support outputs.
I — Fellows do not exercise regulatory authority
II — Fellows do not issue binding determinations
III — Fellows do not assume operational control over real-world systems
§1 — All outputs are classified as: "Systemic Analysis under c-ECO Methodology — Non-binding, Pre-Threshold Advisory Output"
§2 — Participation does not constitute employment, partnership, or equity interest in any affiliated entity.
7.2 — Safe Mode Conduct Protocol
When a case enters Red or Black Band classification under the TFP, Fellows must immediately transition to Safe Mode analytical posture.
I — Prioritize containment, stabilization, and reversibility
II — Suspend optimization or profit-oriented analytical assumptions
III — Reframe all outputs toward systemic preservation
§1 — Fellows are prohibited from producing analysis that supports continuation of trajectories classified as incompatible with systemic stability.
Critical Distinction
Safe Mode activation is not subject to discretionary recommendation. It is automatically triggered upon certified TFP threshold conditions. The Advisory Layer may only validate technical integrity, coordinate response execution, and oversee compliance — never override certified systemic conditions.
7.3 — Rights & Benefits
I — Access to c-ECO methodology, Model Law, and TFP infrastructure
II — Participation in Living Lab environments, including Belém Living Lab
III — Mentorship and technical supervision by c-ECO architects
IV — Inclusion in the c-ECO Case Repository (subject to confidentiality constraints)
V — Eligibility for authorship attribution in derivative publications
VI — Access to institutional network including Instituto Silvio Meira and JCHF
7.4 — Duties & Obligations
I — Apply c-ECO analytical framework faithfully, including TFP logic and pre-threshold assessment
II — Maintain integrity of data inputs and avoid manipulation or selective omission
III — Respect confidentiality of case-sensitive information
IV — Avoid representation of outputs as binding legal, regulatory, or operational decisions
V — Maintain alignment with Safe Operating Space principles in all analytical outputs
§1 — Any material deviation from methodological integrity may result in suspension or removal from the program.
7.5 — Confidentiality Review Protocol
Fellows may request confidentiality restrictions. Final determination rests with the Institutional Oversight Layer, subject to systemic relevance and public interest considerations.
I — Fellow submits restriction request with justification
II — Institutional Oversight Layer evaluates against: (a) systemic relevance (impact on SOS), (b) public interest (affected populations), (c) scientific integrity (reproducibility)
III — Determination issued within 10 business days
IV — Appeals to Joint Committee (JCHF-ISM)
7.6 — Intellectual Property & Use Rights
The c-ECO methodology, Model Law, and associated frameworks remain the intellectual property of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation and its institutional partners.
I — Fellows retain moral authorship over individual analytical outputs
II — Fellows grant non-exclusive, perpetual license for institutional use
III — Derivative methodologies remain under c-ECO institutional ownership
7.7 — Data Governance
All activities involving personal or sensitive data must comply with applicable data protection regimes:
I — Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (Brazil)
II — General Data Protection Regulation (European Union)
§1 — Data used in Living Lab environments must be anonymized whenever possible.
7.8 — Suspension and Removal
The Fellowship may be suspended or terminated for:
I — Breach of methodological integrity
II — Misrepresentation of c-ECO outputs
III — Violation of confidentiality or data governance rules
IV — Conduct incompatible with systemic governance principles
SECTION 8 — CONTRACTUAL ADHESION FRAMEWORK
Participation in the c-ECO Fellowship Program is conditioned upon formal adherence to a structured contractual framework. This framework operationalizes the c-ECO governance architecture at the individual level, ensuring that all analytical, institutional, and operational interactions are aligned with the Safe Operating Space (SOS), the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP), and pre-threshold governance principles.
The contractual system is modular and cumulative. Fellows are required to execute and comply with all applicable instruments as a condition of participation.
8.1 — Core Contract Stack
FPA — Fellowship Participation Agreement

Defines non-employment status, establishes role as analytical agent, incorporates c-ECO Model Law by reference, and conditions participation upon systemic governance principles.

CDGA — Confidentiality & Data Governance Agreement

Governs treatment of sensitive data, mandates cross-jurisdictional compliance (LGPD/GDPR), and establishes anonymization obligations.

MAI — Methodological Adherence Instrument

Critical binding commitment. Establishes fidelity to TFP variables (P, ΔV, σ, Lr), pre-threshold triggers, and systemic compatibility priority.

LLEP — Living Lab Engagement Protocol

Governs field participation, stakeholder interaction, and community/ecological constraints in real-world environments.

CSAM — Case-Specific Analytical Mandate

Defines scope, constraints, and deliverables for individual cases. Cross-sector cases default to Sector 13.

8.2 — Methodological Adherence Instrument (MAI)
The MAI is the cornerstone of the contractual stack. By executing the MAI, Fellows formally recognize that:
I — All analysis must be grounded in TFP variables (P, ΔV, σ, Lr)
II — Pre-threshold dynamics constitute valid triggers for analytical conclusions
III — Systemic compatibility prevails over economic or operational preference
IV — Outputs must not support continuation of trajectories classified as incompatible
§1 — Any analytical output that contradicts certified systemic conditions is deemed methodologically invalid.
§2 — Methodological Evolution Clause: The MAI binds to the c-ECO framework as certified current at execution date. Major version updates require re-execution or addendum. Fellows are notified of material changes affecting TFP variables or Safe Mode protocols.
8.3 — Legal Nature of the Contractual Framework
The contractual instruments shall be interpreted as a systemic governance framework and not as traditional service, employment, or partnership agreements.
I — Contracts operate under conditional validity linked to systemic compatibility
II — Obligations are dynamically modulated under TFP conditions
III — Enforcement is subject to pre-threshold triggers and IEX principles

SECTION 10 — TRIGGER / ACTION SEPARATION PROTOCOL
To maintain the integrity of threshold-based governance and prevent the reintroduction of discretionary override, all sector applications must explicitly distinguish four distinct layers of activity. This separation resolves the tension between automatic TFP triggers and distributed institutional responsibility.
Core Principle
Fellows detect and declare. Institutions coordinate. Operators execute. No layer may unilaterally override certified TFP conditions, but each layer has distinct, non-transferable responsibilities.
I — Detection Layer (TFP)

Function: Continuous monitoring and classification
Output: Band classification (Green/Amber/Red/Black)
Basis: Certified systemic data only
Automation: Algorithmic where possible, expert validation required

II — Analytical Layer (Fellow)

Function: Mandatory issuance of Systemic Notice
Constraint: No discretionary delay allowed upon Red/Black
Output: Structured declaration of systemic state
Liability: Methodological integrity only

III — Coordination Layer (Institutional)

Function: Safe Mode activation coordination
Activity: Interface with stakeholders, resource mobilization
Constraint: Must respect TFP classification; cannot downgrade
Output: Coordinated response protocol

IV — Execution Layer (Operational)

Function: Implementation of containment/modulation
Actors: System owners, regulators, field operators
Constraint: Subject to IEX clauses and contractual triggers
Liability: Operational outcomes

10.1 — Safe Mode Activation Chain
Upon TFP certification of Red or Black Band conditions, the following chain is activated:
I — TFP system issues automatic classification
II — Fellow MUST issue immediate analytical notice (no discretion to delay)
III — Institutional Oversight Layer assumes activation coordination
IV — Fellow transitions to monitoring/support role
V — Operational actors execute under IEX-conditioned contracts
§1 — Failure to issue notice when TFP conditions are met constitutes breach of methodological integrity under Section 7.8.
§2 — The Institutional Oversight Layer may not override or downgrade TFP classification, but may escalate to Joint Committee for exceptional review.
