Institutional Cooperation Portal
Two distinct institutional pathways operate within the broader c-ECO ecosystem. ISM LLC conducts Applied Studies under the IOFA framework. ISM Brazil conducts TDR-related and Brazil-based institutional pathways under the governing MOU framework. These pathways are not interchangeable and do not share the same legal basis, operational routing, or documentation logic.
Purpose
This portal provides the institutional environment for the organization, screening, protection, structuring, execution, and documentation of matters linked to the c-ECO framework through two distinct operational pathways. It is not a generic contact page, nor does it function as an automatic contracting instrument. Its purpose is to clarify institutional routing, indicate the applicable legal architecture, and guide users, reviewers, and internal operators through the correct documentary and procedural logic for each pathway.
Portal Logic
The portal separates Applied Studies and TDR matters because they do not arise from the same legal instrument, do not follow the same operational chain, and do not necessarily produce the same contractual or financial outputs.
Institutional Scope
Three institutional layers sit behind the broader c-ECO ecosystem, but they do not merge into a single operating entity. The Johann Christian Hasse Foundation acts as the governing institutional layer retaining control over the framework and its protected Know-How. ISM LLC, organized in Florida, operates the Applied Studies pathway under the Institutional Operational Framework Agreement (IOFA). ISM Brazil operates the TDR pathway and Brazil-based institutional structuring within the scope of the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) entered into with the Foundation. Institutional Separation
The existence of coordinated institutional pathways does not erase the separation of legal personality, documentation requirements, governance logic, or contractual authority among the Foundation, ISM LLC, and ISM Brazil.
Routing Logic
Routing depends on the nature of the matter. Applied commercial, executive, implementation-facing, and privately structured engagements should generally be routed to ISM LLC. Threshold Dynamics Research, Brazil-based institutional proposals, public-interest initiatives, and TDR-linked technical structuring should generally be routed to ISM Brazil. A matter should not be submitted through one pathway merely because the subject overlaps conceptually with the other.
The routing logic exists to preserve legal coherence. The U.S. pathway is governed by IOFA-based operational logic. The Brazil pathway is governed by the institutional cooperation logic of the MOU, under which ISM Brazil may act as an administrative and institutional proponent in Brazil for approved opportunities, without becoming owner, autonomous controller, or independent governing authority over the c-ECO framework. Record Logic
Each matter should be treated as a structured dossier. Record logic is party-based and document-based, meaning that the same counterparty may generate multiple proposals, NDAs, SDAs, operational orders, financial records, or archival entries over time. However, the underlying institutional route must remain identifiable from the outset. A matter opened under ISM LLC is not automatically transformed into a TDR file, and a matter opened under ISM Brazil is not automatically converted into an Applied Studies engagement.
Legal Limits
No legal, financial, or operational obligation arises from the portal itself. The portal serves as an institutional orientation and routing environment. In the case of ISM Brazil, the MOU expressly establishes a cooperative relationship only, not a partnership, joint venture, agency, employment relationship, fiduciary relationship, or automatic legal representation. It also provides that project-specific obligations require a separate SDA and that the c-ECO framework remains outside any transfer of ownership to the Institute. Formalization Rule
Where a matter proceeds beyond preliminary review, the applicable obligation must be formalized through the relevant project-specific instrument, including an SDA where required by the governing framework.
ISM LLC — Applied Studies
ISM LLC · Florida

Applied Studies

Guided by the Institutional Operational Framework Agreement (IOFA). Designed for pilots, applied diagnostics, contractual packages, governance briefings, and implementation-facing structured studies.

The Applied Studies pathway is the operational route for pilot deployments, executive-facing studies, contract-sensitive diagnostics, implementation-oriented governance work, and structured outputs prepared for private or commercially framed matters. It is the appropriate route when the engagement is expected to involve scoping, confidentiality review, project-specific legal structuring, execution logic, and invoicing through the U.S. operating layer.
Typical Use Cases
  • Rapid risk scans and pilot diagnostics
  • Contract addendum and clause package development
  • Board briefings and governance setup
  • Client-facing applied studies and structured outputs
  • Private or internationally framed engagements
Applied Studies Workflow

Submission through this pathway begins with a pilot request. That request is reviewed internally and may require confidentiality protection before detailed scoping occurs. If the matter advances, the project is structured through an SDA, then translated into operational execution and invoice logic through the appropriate downstream instruments.

Guiding Instrument

The Applied Studies pathway is governed by the IOFA framework and its related NDA, SDA, Operational Order, and Invoice logic.

Call to Action

Use this route if the matter is pilot-based, privately structured, implementation-facing, or expected to generate operational or financial documentation through the U.S. pathway.

Pilot Workflow Sequence
Step 1
Request a Pilot through the Applied Studies intake page.
Step 2
Internal review and confidentiality assessment, including NDA where required.
Step 3
Scoping and project-specific structuring through SDA, then execution and invoicing.

ISM Brazil — TDR
Instituto Silvio Meira · Brazil

Threshold Dynamics Research (TDR)

Guided by the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU). Designed for Brazil-based institutional proposals, research structuring, public-interest initiatives, and TDR-linked technical pathways.

The TDR pathway is the institutional and research-oriented route for Threshold Dynamics Research, Brazil-based project articulation, public-interest initiatives, technical studies, and proposal environments requiring local institutional presence or Brazil-specific structuring. Under the governing MOU, ISM Brazil may act as an administrative and institutional proponent in Brazil for approved grants, research calls, public funding opportunities, and collaborative projects, subject to prior written approval by the Foundation.   • Brazil-based institutional submissions and grant pathways

TDR Workflow

Submission through this pathway begins with a TDR request or institutional proposal inquiry. The matter is screened for institutional fit, scope, sensitivity, and documentation requirements. If the matter advances into a formal project environment, the relevant project-specific instrument, including an SDA where required, must be prepared separately.

Guiding Instrument

The TDR pathway is governed by the MOU between the Foundation and ISM Brazil. That MOU frames institutional cooperation, preserves c-ECO ownership outside the Institute, and requires project-specific SDA formalization for approved initiatives. Signature Routing
Signature routing is a formalization function, not a universal stage. Certain matters may advance to signature after NDA or SDA preparation. Others may remain under review, be suspended, or be archived without ever reaching execution. Signature routing should therefore be understood as a downstream function of the correct pathway, not as the defining feature of the pathway itself.
Filing & Archival Records
Filing and archival records operate continuously across both pathways. They apply to active matters, abandoned prospects, unsigned proposals, executed project instruments, completed project cycles, and suspended initiatives. Filing is therefore independent of whether a matter reaches execution. This distinction is especially important where proposal environments, research submissions, or early pilot requests do not proceed into a signed project structure.
This portal is designed as an institutional routing and explanatory layer. It does not itself create legal obligations. The Brazil pathway is framed by the MOU between the Foundation and ISM Brazil, including the limits expressly stated therein.
