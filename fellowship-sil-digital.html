3. Global Institutional Landscape
  • International Telecommunication Union (ITU): UN Agency for ICTs. Defines standards and allocates spectrum. Relevance: Primary regulatory source for P (Spectral allocation/usage) and SPS (Systemic Proximity Score).
  • Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN): Coordinates DNS root. Relevance: Governance of identifier systems; P (Routing integrity).
  • International Cable Protection Committee (ICPC): Cross-sector industry association founded 1958 for submarine cable resilience. Relevance: Source for historical failure data (Lr indicators). NOTE: Class B (Industry Association), NOT Class A.
  • Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC): International governmental forum of 13 space agencies (NASA, ESA, JAXA, ASI, CNES, CNSA, CSA, DLR, ISRO, KARI, ROSCOSMOS, SSAU, UKSA), established 1993. Guidelines inform UNOOSA/COPUOS. Relevance: Orbital debris mitigation; P (Collision probability), ΔV (Orbit saturation velocity). CORRECTION: Replaces erroneous "ISPC (Via UNOOSA)" entry.
  • European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA): EU agency for cybersecurity policy and resilience. Relevance: σ (Governance gaps), Lr (Response protocols).
  • US Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA): US national cybersecurity authority. Relevance: σ (Vulnerability severity), Lr (Incident response).
  • National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST): Cybersecurity frameworks (CSF). Relevance: σ (Vulnerability severity), Lr (Response protocols).
  • Forum of Incident Response & Security Teams (FIRST): CVSS/EPSS vulnerability severity scoring. Relevance: σ (Exploit probability), P (Probability of exploitation).

5. Secondary Systemic Risks (Amplification Mechanisms)
  • Concentration Risk: Single-provider cloud dependency (AWS, Azure, GCP). A regional outage has global cascade effects.
  • Hidden Dependency: GPS timing for financial transactions. No alternative timing source in most markets.
  • Compound Risk: Simultaneous water & power loss for data center cooling. Either loss causes thermal shutdown.
  • Human Capital Erosion: Loss of analog skills (e.g., celestial navigation, paper-based operations). Digital maps/GPS failure paralyzes logistics.
  • Insurance Mismatch: Cyber insurance does not cover operational replacement (NotPetya lesson).
  • Regulatory Fragmentation: Conflicting data sovereignty laws prevent cross-border failover.
  • Vendor Lock-in: Proprietary formats prevent rapid migration during outage.
6. Emerging Systemic Risks (Horizon Scanning)
  • Quantum Computing Threat: "Harvest now, decrypt later" attacks on encrypted archives.
  • AI-Generated Disinformation at Scale: Automated BGP hijacking via synthetic intelligence.
  • Neuromorphic Computing Fragility: Novel hardware architectures with untested failure modes.
  • Space-Based Data Centers: Orbital compute nodes exposed to radiation and debris.
  • DNA Data Storage: Biological contamination risks for archival infrastructure.
  • Post-Quantum Cryptography Transition: Migration chaos during algorithmic rollover.
  • Autonomous Network Management: AI-controlled routing with emergent instability.
