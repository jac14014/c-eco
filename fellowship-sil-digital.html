▼ PART I — DOMAIN INTELLIGENCE PROFILE (Sections 1-3)

1. Domain Overview

Definition: Physical and virtual assets—fiber optic cables, data centers, satellite constellations, radio frequency spectrum, cloud platforms—that process, store, and transmit digital information.
Scope: Terrestrial (fiber, 5G/6G), submarine (cable landing stations), orbital (LEO, MEO, GEO), plus power grids and cooling systems.
Systemic Relevance: Central nervous system for Finance, Energy, Health. Failure causes cascading collapses.
Key Infrastructures: Submarine cables (99% intercontinental traffic), hyperscale data centers, GNSS (GPS/Galileo), LEO constellations (Starlink, OneWeb), IXPs (Internet Exchange Points).

2. Historical Evolution (ΔV Calibration)

Selected milestones for velocity calibration: 1859 Carrington Event (telegraphs fail globally — benchmark SPS threshold); 1989 Quebec blackout (GIC, 6M without power); 2006 Hengchun earthquake (8 cables severed — concentration risk); 2012 Carrington-class CME near miss (missed Earth by 9 days — σ); 2021 Log4Shell (90% Java apps vulnerable); 2022 Tonga eruption (5-week blackout, Lr=0); 2025 Spain/Portugal blackout (cross-domain Energy→Digital, 60-90% traffic drop). Full 60-event historical library in Part IX.

3. Global Institutional Landscape

Key institutions with TFP relevance: ITU (spectrum, SPS), ICANN (DNS root), ICPC (cable protection, Class B, not A), IADC (13 space agencies: NASA, ESA, JAXA, ASI, CNES, CNSA, CSA, DLR, ISRO, KARI, Roscosmos, SSAU, UKSA), ENISA (EU cyber), CISA (US cyber), NIST (frameworks), FIRST (CVSS/EPSS).
