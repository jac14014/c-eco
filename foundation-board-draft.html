I. Nature of the Mandate

Your appointment shall take effect on [START DATE], for an initial term of three (3) years, renewable by Board resolution, in accordance with Article IV, Section 4.04, of the Foundation's Bylaws. This is a voluntary service agreement and does not constitute an employment contract. No compensation shall be due for the performance of your duties as Director, except for reimbursement of reasonable expenses previously approved, in compliance with the Foundation's Compensation & Reasonableness Policy.

II. Functions and Responsibilities

As a member of the Board, you shall be responsible for:

(a) The exercise of governance, oversight, and ultimate fiduciary authority over the affairs of the Foundation;

(b) Participation in annual Board meetings, ordinary or extraordinary, in person or by electronic means;

(c) Contribution to the integrity, continuity, and alignment of the systemic governance frameworks sponsored by the Foundation, including the c-ECO Model Law, the Threshold Function Protocol (TFP), and the Integrated Operational Manual (IOM);

(d) Representation of the Foundation in relevant international forums, promoting the understanding of the c-ECO regime as prudential market infrastructure;

(e) Participation in technical or special Committees, when established by the Board, in accordance with Article VI of the Bylaws.

III. Fiduciary Duties

You undertake to discharge your duties in good faith, with the diligence of a prudent person in a like position, in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Foundation, in strict compliance with the Bylaws, the ECO Institutional Governance Protocol, and the policies on Conflict of Interest, Whistleblower & Governance Integrity, and Compensation & Reasonableness, with immediate recusal from any deliberation in which a real or apparent conflict of interest exists.

IV. Supplementary Documentation

Attached to this letter (available for download on the digital portal), you will find:

1. Copy of the Foundation's Bylaws;

2. ECO Institutional Governance Protocol;

3. Conflict of Interest Policy;

4. Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form (to be completed online);

5. Whistleblower & Governance Integrity Policy;

6. Compensation & Reasonableness Policy;

7. Personalized Pre-Clearance Matrix (mandatory recusal guide).

We request the submission of the Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form, duly completed, within thirty (30) days from the date of this letter, via the secure portal.

V. Formal Acceptance

This appointment is conditioned upon your express acceptance, which may be communicated by reply to this letter, by email addressed to board@c-eco.org, or by any other means that leaves unequivocal record of the appointee's will.

VI. Renewal and Resignation

The mandate may be renewed by Board resolution. Resignation may be communicated at any time, in writing, with a minimum advance notice of sixty (60) days, addressed to the President or Secretary of the Foundation.

VII. Final Provisions

The Foundation recognizes that your contribution transcends the formal exercise of corporate governance. This is an invitation to co-construct a legal-institutional architecture capable of operationalizing, at transnational scale, the primacy of the Safe Operating Space over private, administrative, or state autonomy — without delegation of sovereignty, without substitution of the environmental public order, and without creating new regulation, but providing prudential market infrastructure for the implementation of existing expectations.

Confident that this appointment will significantly strengthen the Foundation's institutional capacity to fulfill its mission of charity, education, science, and research, I remain available for any additional clarifications.

PREAMBLE

WHEREAS, the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation (the "Foundation") was duly incorporated as a Missouri Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation and is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, scientific, and research purposes consistent with Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code;

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors (the "Board") possesses ultimate governance, oversight, and fiduciary authority over the affairs of the Foundation, pursuant to Article IV of the Bylaws;

WHEREAS, the Foundation seeks to strengthen its institutional capacity, transnational reach, and doctrinal authority in systemic governance by expanding the composition of the Board to include distinguished professionals of international standing in corporate law, transnational litigation, and juridical-academic institutions;

WHEREAS, Rodolfo Rudy Rivera and André Augusto Malcher Meira have been formally invited to serve as Directors and have communicated their acceptance in writing;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Board of Directors hereby adopts the following resolutions:

Section 1 — Confirmation of Acceptance

RESOLVED, that the acceptance by Rodolfo Rudy Rivera and André Augusto Malcher Meira of their appointment to the Board of Directors is hereby confirmed and ratified; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, that their respective appointments shall be effective as of the date of this Resolution.

Section 2 — Appointment of Directors

RESOLVED, that the following individuals are hereby formally appointed as Directors of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation, each to serve an initial term of three (3) years, unless sooner removed, resigned, or deceased:

2.1 Rodolfo Rudy Rivera

Chief International Counsel, Fidelity National Financial

Adjunct Professor, Washington University in St. Louis

Term: [DATE] through [DATE + 3 years]

2.2 André Augusto Malcher Meira

President, Instituto Silvio Meira (ISM)

President, Colégio de Presidentes das Academias Jurídicas do Brasil (COPAJUB)

Term: [DATE] through [DATE + 3 years]

Section 3 — Ratification of Governance Documents

RESOLVED, that each newly appointed Director shall be bound by, and shall acknowledge receipt of, the following governing documents of the Foundation: (a) Bylaws; (b) ECO Institutional Governance Protocol; (c) Conflict of Interest Policy; (d) Compensation & Reasonableness Policy; (e) Whistleblower & Governance Integrity Policy; (f) Document Retention Policy.

Section 4 — Fiduciary Duties and Standards of Conduct

RESOLVED, that each Director shall discharge their duties: (a) In good faith; (b) With the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances; (c) In a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Foundation; (d) In compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and the Foundation's governing documents.

Section 5 — Committee Assignments

RESOLVED, that Committee assignments shall be determined by the Board at the next annual meeting, or by the President with subsequent ratification by the Board; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Technical Committee and the Calibration Committee, when established, shall be available for participation by all Directors, subject to expertise and availability.

Section 6 — Compensation and Expense Reimbursement

RESOLVED, that service as Director is voluntary and without compensation, except for reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of Board duties, pre-approved by the President or Treasurer and documented in accordance with the Compensation & Reasonableness Policy; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, that no Director shall receive any financial benefit from Foundation activities except as expressly authorized by the Board and consistent with Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Section 7 — Conflict of Interest

RESOLVED, that each Director shall complete and submit the Foundation's Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form within thirty (30) days of the effective date of this Resolution; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, that any Director with a real or apparent conflict of interest in any matter before the Board shall recuse themselves from discussion and vote on such matter, in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy.

Section 8 — Indemnification

RESOLVED, that each Director shall be entitled to indemnification to the fullest extent permitted under Section 355.476 of the Missouri Revised Statutes, as provided in Article VIII of the Bylaws.

Section 9 — Effective Date

RESOLVED, that this Resolution shall take effect immediately upon adoption.

Adoption

This Resolution was duly adopted by the Board of Directors of the Johann Christian Hasse Foundation at a meeting held on [DATE], by unanimous vote, constituting a quorum.

Certification

The undersigned hereby certifies that the foregoing Resolution was duly adopted by the Board of Directors and constitutes a true and complete record of the proceedings.

Recusal Activation Protocol

1. DETECTION: Self-identification by Director | Identification by Secretary/Compliance Officer | Report by another Director

2. NOTIFICATION: Director notifies Board Chair in writing | Secretary records in minutes | Urgent: verbal + confirmation within 24h

3. ASSESSMENT: Board Chair consults disinterested Directors | Majority concurrence = mandatory recusal | Disagreement = majority vote of disinterested

4. DOCUMENTATION: Recusal recorded in minutes | Director leaves the room | Decision attributed to "Board acting without [Name]"

5. REVIEW: Conflicts reviewed annually | Underlying condition changes = reassessment

Special Clause — Resolutive Condition (A. Meira)

The mandate of André Augusto Malcher Meira is subject to the following resolutive condition:

If Meira is appointed, invested, or sworn into the office of trial judge, appellate judge, or equivalent in any court of Brazil, his mandate as Director shall be automatically suspended as of the date of investiture.

(b) If such determination is not possible or favorable, the mandate shall be deemed ipso facto resigned, without need for further manifestation.
