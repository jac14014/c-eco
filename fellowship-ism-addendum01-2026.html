1. Purpose and Scope

This Institutional Operations Addendum ("Addendum") establishes the operational workflow, communication protocol, and coordination structure for c-ECO Fellowship activities conducted through the Belém institutional node operated by ISM.

This Addendum does not create financial obligations, licensing rights, or governance authority beyond those established in the MOU and SDA. Its sole purpose is to ensure institutional clarity, operational efficiency, and seamless coordination between HF and ISM.

The Parties acknowledge that ISM serves as the primary local institutional interface for candidates, Fellows, academic partners, public agencies, and civil society organizations in the Belém/Pará region.

Addendum Status
This is a coordination instrument, not a contractual obligation layer. It formalizes workflow, not liability. All substantive rights and obligations remain in the MOU and SDA.

2. Tracking and Attribution

2.1 Institutional Tracking Code

All candidates, Fellows, and activities linked to ISM coordination shall use the institutional tracking code:

ism-amazonlab

2.2 Application of Tracking Code

The tracking code shall be applied in:

  1. Fellowship application materials submitted by or through ISM;
  2. email communications between ISM and HF regarding candidates;
  3. event registration and attendance records;
  4. Living Lab activity reports;
  5. institutional outreach documentation;
  6. quarterly operational reports submitted by ISM.

2.3 Attribution Purpose

The tracking code serves operational mapping and cohort identification only. It does not create:

  1. financial entitlement or compensation;
  2. admissions influence or selection priority;
  3. territorial exclusivity;
  4. commercial origination rights.

3. Operational Workflow

3.1 Candidate Flow

Standard Candidate Workflow — Belém Node

  1. Discovery — Candidate learns about Fellowship through ISM outreach, event, or referral
  2. Pre-orientation — ISM provides initial information, directs to c-eco.org/fellowship.html
  3. Application — Candidate submits application directly to HF via official portal, using code ism-amazonlab
  4. HF Review — Foundation processes application through internal admissions procedures
  5. ISM Notification — HF notifies ISM of application receipt (weekly batch)
  6. Local Support — ISM assists admitted Fellow with onboarding, venue, logistics
  7. Participation — Fellow engages in Fellowship activities under FPA governance
  8. Reporting — ISM includes Fellow in quarterly operational report

3.2 ISM Shall Not

ISM shall not:

  1. make final admission, scholarship, standing, certification, or licensing determinations on behalf of HF;
  2. collect application fees or participation payments;
  3. promise admission, certification, scholarship, standing, or license outcomes;
  4. modify application deadlines, requirements, or procedures established by HF;
  5. create independent selection criteria or evaluation rubrics binding on HF.

3.3 Event and Operational Coordination Workflow

For Fellowship-related events or approved operational activities in Belém:

  1. ISM shall submit the proposed activity, date, venue, operational needs, and estimated budget to HF in advance whenever reasonably practicable;
  2. HF shall review the proposal and approve, modify, or decline the activity in writing;
  3. upon written approval, ISM may coordinate the approved activity within the authorized scope and budget;
  4. ISM shall submit supporting documentation, receipts, invoices, or activity records within fifteen (15) days after completion of the approved activity;
  5. HF shall process eligible approved reimbursements according to the SDA and any approved operational budget.

3.4 Emergency / Expedited Workflow

For time-sensitive opportunities (speakers in transit, funding deadlines, institutional windows):

  1. ISM submits expedited request via email with "URGENT — BELÉM" in subject line;
  2. HF responds within 48 hours or provides interim guidance;
  3. Post-hoc documentation follows within 7 days.

  5. Annual planning: joint session for next-cycle activity mapping and budget pre-approval.

4.3 Response Timeframes

Event approval request 10 business days Auto-escalate after 14 days

4.4 Language

Official communications between HF and ISM shall be in English. Communications with local candidates, partners, and stakeholders may be in Portuguese, with ISM responsible for translation where HF review is required.

5. Onboarding and Integration

5.1 Fellow Onboarding Support

ISM shall provide local onboarding assistance to admitted Fellows, including:

  1. venue orientation and access instructions;
  2. local logistics guidance (transportation, accommodation, safety);
  3. introduction to local academic and institutional ecosystem;
  4. coordination of welcome session or integration meeting;
  5. distribution of approved HF materials (not ISM-created substitutes).

5.2 Onboarding Limits

ISM onboarding does not include:

  1. methodological instruction or doctrinal explanation;
  2. TFP training or analytical supervision;
  3. standing classification or disciplinary matters;
  4. certificate issuance or credential validation.

5.3 Integration with Living Lab

Fellows connected to the Belém node shall be integrated into the Amazon Living Lab structure under the LLEP / Exhibit C, with ISM facilitating:

  1. field visit logistics;
  2. community engagement protocols;
  3. territorial partner introductions (subject to HF approval);
  4. local data collection support (subject to CDGA compliance).

6. Brand and Public Use

6.1 Approved Use

ISM may use the following in public communications with prior HF written approval:

  1. "c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab" as event or program title;
  2. "in cooperation with Johann Christian Hasse Foundation" as institutional attribution;
  3. HF logo and seal in approved formats and sizes;
  4. Jacqueline A. Ennis attribution as c-ECO architect.

6.2 Prohibited Use

ISM shall not:

  1. use "c-ECO" as ISM's own brand, program name, or institutional identifier;
  2. imply ownership, co-creation, or governance authority over c-ECO;
  3. create derivative logos, seals, or visual identities incorporating c-ECO elements;
  4. issue certificates, credentials, or endorsements in the name of c-ECO or HF;
  5. use c-ECO branding for non-approved activities or independent initiatives.

6.3 Social Media and Digital Presence

ISM may share HF-approved content on its social media and website, provided:

  1. content is verbatim or pre-approved by HF;
  2. attribution to HF and Jacqueline A. Ennis is included;
  3. no modification of doctrinal claims, methodological descriptions, or program scope;
  4. link to c-eco.org is included where digitally feasible.

7. Reporting

7.1 Quarterly Operational Report

ISM shall submit a quarterly report to HF containing:

  1. summary of activities conducted (events, meetings, outreach);
  2. candidate flow metrics (inquiries, applications, admissions, tracking code usage);
  3. expenditure summary with receipts for approved activities;
  4. institutional partner engagement update;
  5. challenges, risks, or recommendations;
  6. planned activities for next quarter.

7.2 Annual Institutional Review

Annually, the Parties shall conduct a joint review of:

  1. Addendum effectiveness and workflow efficiency;
  2. ISM operational capacity and resource needs;
  3. candidate quality and Fellowship outcomes from Belém node;
  4. budget allocation for next cycle;
  5. strategic opportunities and partnership expansion.

7.3 Report Format

Reports may be submitted in English or Portuguese, provided that English summaries or translations are supplied where HF review, international reporting, grant documentation, or official Foundation records require English-language documentation.

Reports should be sent to contact@c-eco.org, with copy to the designated ISM institutional contact, using the subject line: "c-ECO Belém — [Quarter/Year] Report".

8. Operational Support and Cost Coordination

Non-Commercial Principle
ISM operates under a nonprofit, public-interest mandate. All financial support is structured as operational reimbursement or approved institutional allocation. No commission, fee, revenue share, percentage participation, or commercial incentive structure applies. This preserves ISM's institutional legitimacy, HF's 501(c)(3) status, and the collaborative integrity of the c-ECO Fellowship Program.

8.1 Fellowship Fees — Direct to Foundation

All Fellowship participation fees, admission costs, instructional support fees, certificate costs, renewal fees, and associated amounts shall be payable exclusively to HF through approved institutional channels. ISM shall not collect, intermediate, hold, or process Fellowship payments.

8.2 Approved Operational Costs — Reimbursement

ISM may receive reimbursement for documented actual costs reasonably incurred in connection with expressly approved operational support activities. Eligible categories include, without limitation:

  1. local venue coordination and rental;
  2. event organization support (materials, signage, interpretation, catering for institutional events);
  3. communication and scheduling assistance;
  4. institutional articulation with local stakeholders;
  5. approved logistical expenses (transportation, accommodation for invited guests, field visit costs);
  6. local operational coordination staff time (documented hourly or project-based);
  7. translation or local facilitation support;
  8. approved technology, equipment, or infrastructure costs;
  9. other documented costs pre-approved in writing by HF.

8.3 Documentation Requirements

Any reimbursement request must be supported by:

  1. original receipts, invoices, or third-party documentation;
  2. time records or activity reports for staff time;
  3. pre-approval written confirmation from HF (except emergency expenses not exceeding USD $500 per incident, subject to post-hoc documentation within 15 days);
  4. submission within 30 days of activity completion.

8.4 Annual Institutional Support Allocation

HF may, at its sole discretion and subject to available institutional resources, approve annual operational support allocations for ISM activities associated with the c-ECO Fellowship Program — Amazon Living Lab.

Any such allocation:

  1. shall be determined annually upon operational proposal submitted by ISM and prior written approval by HF;
  2. shall be subject to quarterly reporting on activities and expenditures;
  3. shall be subject to HF discretion to modify, suspend, or terminate upon thirty (30) days written notice;
  4. shall not create any entitlement, expectation, or obligation for continuation beyond the approved period;
  5. shall be used exclusively for approved operational purposes and not for general institutional overhead, unrelated activities, or personal benefit.

8.5 Event-Specific Budget

For individual events, workshops, or initiatives requiring dedicated resources, ISM may submit a specific budget proposal to HF for pre-approval. Such proposals shall include:

  1. event description, objectives, and expected outcomes;
  2. itemized budget with cost estimates;
  3. timeline and milestones;
  4. participant or audience estimates;
  5. post-event reporting commitments.

8.6 Fellowship Participation Reference Structure

The following ranges are illustrative reference structures for Fellowship participation pathways. They do not constitute binding pricing, guaranteed availability, or operational authorization:

Participation Structure Reference Range Description
Institutional Scholarship — Module 1 USD $0 Full tuition waiver for Module 1 only, without operational or licensing authorization.
Waiver-Supported Participation USD $50–90/hour Partial tuition waiver with separately billed instructional, review, mentoring, analytical, or implementation support hours.
Standard Fellowship Participation — Modules 1–6 USD $1,500–2,500 Total participation structure for the Fellowship cycle, excluding any future operational or institutional licensing.

The Foundation may determine, in its sole discretion, whether a participant receives: (a) full scholarship support; (b) partial waiver support with separate instructional-hour allocation; or (c) standard Fellowship participation terms.

Instructional support hours may include review sessions, analytical guidance, implementation discussions, case supervision, written feedback, methodological clarification, or operational mentoring associated with Fellowship activities.

8.6-A Institutional Administrative Support Allocation

In recognition of ISM's operational coordination, institutional articulation, candidate support, and local administrative activities associated with the Fellowship Program, HF may provide a fixed institutional administrative support allocation for admitted participants associated with the institutional tracking code designated for ISM.

Unless otherwise approved in writing by HF, the reference operational support allocation shall be USD $200 per admitted participant associated with the ISM institutional coordination structure.

Such allocation:

  1. constitutes institutional operational support and not commission, profit-sharing, revenue participation, brokerage, or sales compensation;
  2. shall be payable exclusively by HF after confirmed Fellowship admission and payment processing;
  3. shall be used by ISM for administrative, logistical, coordination, outreach, or operational support purposes connected to the Fellowship Program;
  4. does not create exclusivity, admissions authority, ownership rights, or licensing rights;
  5. may be modified, suspended, or discontinued by HF upon written notice.

All amounts are payable directly to HF. ISM does not participate in, intermediate, or benefit from Fellowship fee revenue.

Important Distinction
Fellowship participation grants access to the instructional, methodological, and research environment of the c-ECO Fellowship Program only. It does not automatically authorize operational deployment, institutional implementation, consulting activity, commercial use, or licensing of the c-ECO Framework or TFP.

8.7 Future Licensing Structures — Informational Only

Separate licensing structures may become available for approved operational, institutional, infrastructure, governmental, research, or enterprise applications of the c-ECO Framework and associated TFP structures.

The following ranges are non-binding illustrative reference structures only and do not constitute guaranteed availability, pricing commitments, implementation authorization, or operational approval.

License Structure Reference Range Illustrative Scope
Pilot / Small-Scale Case License USD $2,500–7,500 Limited institutional pilots, controlled case studies, academic or research implementation environments.
Structured Operational License USD $7,500–20,000 Operational implementation involving structured workflows, analytical review environments, or institutional coordination.
Institutional / Infrastructure License USD $20,000–50,000+ Large-scale institutional, governmental, infrastructure, systemic-risk, or enterprise-level implementation environments.

Final licensing structures may vary depending on sectoral exposure, implementation complexity, institutional scale, territorial scope, data sensitivity, monitoring requirements, systemic-risk exposure, governance architecture, and applicable operational safeguards.

No license rights arise from Fellowship participation alone. All licensing activity remains subject to separate written authorization by the Foundation.

8.7-A Institutional Implementation Support Allocation

In addition to Fellowship-related operational support allocations, HF may, at its sole discretion and subject to separate written approval, authorize limited institutional implementation support allocations connected to pilot projects, institutional implementations, operational deployments, or licensing-related initiatives originating directly from ISM's approved institutional articulation and coordination activities in the Amazon region.

Such implementation support allocations are intended exclusively to support:

  1. local institutional coordination;
  2. stakeholder articulation and engagement;
  3. implementation logistics and administrative support;
  4. territorial facilitation and operational assistance;
  5. approved implementation-related activities connected to institutional c-ECO initiatives in the Amazon Living Lab structure.

These implementation support allocations:

  1. shall not constitute commission, percentage participation, revenue sharing, sales compensation, licensing participation, brokerage, or commercial representation;
  2. shall not create ownership, co-ownership, sublicensing authority, exclusivity, governance rights, or independent commercialization authority over c-ECO;
  3. shall remain subject to separate written authorization by HF for each individual project, pilot, institutional implementation, or licensing-related initiative;
  4. shall be structured exclusively as institutional operational support allocations associated with approved implementation-related activities;
  5. may include implementation support allocations of up to USD $2,000 per qualifying pilot, institutional implementation, or licensing-related initiative, unless otherwise approved in writing by HF.

Eligibility for any implementation support allocation depends on documented evidence demonstrating that the applicable institutional opportunity originated substantially from ISM's approved coordination, institutional articulation, or implementation support activities.

Preservation of Institutional Character
The Parties expressly acknowledge that these implementation support allocations are intended solely to preserve the nonprofit, public-interest, educational, and institutional nature of the relationship between the Parties. Nothing in this Addendum shall be interpreted as creating a commercial agency relationship, sales partnership, sublicensing structure, profit-sharing arrangement, revenue-sharing mechanism, or independent licensing authority for ISM.

9. Term and Amendment

9.1 Term

This Addendum shall remain effective for the duration of SDA No. 1 and the MOU, unless earlier terminated or amended in writing by the Parties.

9.2 Review

The Parties may conduct annual operational reviews to update workflow, reporting practices, budget coordination, and implementation procedures. Such review shall not constitute automatic termination or renewal unless expressly agreed in writing.

9.3 Conflict Resolution

In case of conflict between this Addendum and the MOU or SDA, the MOU and SDA shall control. This Addendum is subordinate to and interpretively consistent with those instruments.
